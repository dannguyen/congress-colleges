BARNARD, Druie Douglas, Jr., (1922 - )

Image courtesy of the Library of Congress

BARNARD, Druie Douglas, Jr., a Representative from Georgia; born in Augusta, Richmond County, Ga., March 20, 1922; attended the Richmond County public schools; graduated, Academy of Richmond County, Augusta, Ga., 1939; attended Augusta College, 1939-1940; A.B., Mercer University, Macon, Ga., 1943; served in United States Army, 1943-1945; LL.B., Walter F. George School of Law, Mercer University, 1948; engaged in banking profession, 1948-1962; executive secretary to Governor Carl E. Sanders, Georgia, 1963-1966; board member, Georgia State Department of Transportation, 1966-1976; delegate to Georgia State Democratic convention, 1962; delegate to Democratic National Convention, 1964; elected as a Democrat to the Ninety-fifth and to the seven succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1977-January 3, 1993); was not a candidate for renomination in 1992 to the One Hundred Third Congress; is a resident of Augusta, Ga.

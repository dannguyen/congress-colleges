WOLF, Frank Rudolph, (1939 - )

Collection of the U.S. House of Representatives

WOLF, Frank Rudolph, a Representative from Virginia; born in Philadelphia, Philadelphia County, Pa., January 30, 1939; attended the University of Mississippi, Oxford, Miss., 1957-1958; B.A., Pennsylvania State University, State College, Pa., 1961; LL.B., Georgetown University Law School, Washington, D.C., 1965; United States Army Reserve, 1962-1967; lawyer, private practice; staff, United States Representative Edward G. Biester, Jr., of Pennsylvania, 1968-1971; staff, United States Secretary of the Interior Rogers C.B. Morton, 1971-1974; deputy assistant secretary, United States Department of the Interior, 1974-1975; unsuccessful candidate for nomination to the United States House of Representative in 1976; unsuccessful candidate for election to the United States House of Representative in 1978; elected as a Republican to the Ninety-seventh and to the sixteen succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1981-present); was not a candidate for reelection to the One Hundred Fourteenth Congress in 2014.

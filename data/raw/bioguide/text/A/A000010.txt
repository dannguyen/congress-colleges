ABEL, Hazel Hempel, a Senator from Nebraska
born in Plattsmouth, Cass County, Nebr., July 10, 1888
attended the public schools of Omaha, Nebr., graduated from the University of Nebraska at Lincoln in 1908
high school teacher of mathematics and principal of high schools in Papillion, Ashland, and Crete, Nebr. 1908-1916
president of Abel Construction Co. 1937-1952
chairwoman of the board of directors of Abel Investment Co., Lincoln, Nebr. 1952-1953
vice chairwoman of State Republican Central Committee in 1954
elected on November 2, 1954, as a Republican to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy in the term ending January 3, 1955, caused by the death of Dwight Griswold, and served from November 8, 1954, until her resignation December 31, 1954
delegate to White House Conference on Education in 1955
chairwoman of Nebraska delegation to the Republican National Convention in 1956
member of the Theodore Roosevelt Centennial Commission 1955-1959
chairwoman, board of trustees, Doane College
member, board of trustees of Nebraska Wesleyan College
died in Lincoln, Nebr., on July 30, 1966
interment in Wyuka Cemetery.

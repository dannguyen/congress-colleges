ADAMS, Silas, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Pulaski County, Ky., February 9, 1839
moved to Casey County with his parents in 1841
attended the public schools, Kentucky University at Harrodsburg, and Transylvania University at Lexington
entered the Union Army during the Civil War as a first lieutenant, First Regiment, Kentucky Volunteer Cavalry
promoted to captain, lieutenant colonel, and colonel of the regiment
was mustered out December 31, 1864
entered Lexington Law School in 1867
was admitted to the bar and practiced
served two terms as county attorney
member of the State house of representatives 1889-1892
unsuccessful Republican candidate for the United States Senate in 1892
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-third Congress (March 4, 1893-March 3, 1895)
unsuccessful independent candidate for reelection in 1894 to the Fifty-fourth Congress
resumed the practice of law
died in Liberty, Casey County, Ky., May 5, 1896
interment in Brown Cemetery, Humphrey, Ky.

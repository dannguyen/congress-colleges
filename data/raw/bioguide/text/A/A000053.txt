ADDAMS, William, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Lancaster County, Pa., April 11, 1777
moved to Berks County, near Reading, and served as auditor in 1813 and 1814
commissioner of Berks County 1814-1817
member of the State house of representatives 1822-1824
elected as a Jacksonian to the Nineteenth and Twentieth Congresses (March 4, 1825-March 3, 1829)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1828
member of the committee for the Deaf and Dumb Institution for the States of New York and Ohio
elected associate judge of Berks County and served from 1839 to 1842
captain of the Reading City Troop
largely interested in agricultural pursuits
died in Spring Township, Berks County, Pa., May 30, 1858
interment in St. JohnÂ’s Church Cemetery, Sinking Springs, Pa.

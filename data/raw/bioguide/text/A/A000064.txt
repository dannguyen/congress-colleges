AIKEN, Wyatt, (son of David Wyatt Aiken), a Representative from South Carolina
born near Macon, Ga., December 14, 1863
reared in Cokesbury, Abbeville (now Greenwood) County, S.C.
attended the public schools of Cokesbury and of Washington, D.C.
official court reporter for the second South Carolina judicial circuit and, later, for the eighth circuit
volunteered as a private in Company A, First South Carolina Regiment of Infantry, during the war with Spain
later appointed battalion adjutant by Governor Ellerbe, and acted as regimental quartermaster during the greater portion of his service
was mustered out in Columbia, S.C., November 10, 1898
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-eighth and to the six succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1903-March 3, 1917)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1916 and again in 1918
lived in retirement until his death in Abbeville, S.C., February 6, 1923
interment in Melrose Cemetery.

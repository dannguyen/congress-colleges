ALLEN, William, a Representative and a Senator from Ohio
born in Edenton, Chowan County, N.C., December 18 or December 27, 1803
moved to Lynchburg, Va., and attended private schools
moved to Chillicothe, Ohio, in 1819
attended Chillicothe Academy
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1827 and commenced practice in Chillicothe
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third Congress
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1834 to the Twenty-fourth Congress
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate in January 1837
reelected in 1843, and served from March 4, 1837, to March 3, 1849
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1849
chairman, Committee on Foreign Relations (Twenty-ninth Congress)
retired to his estate, Fruit Hill, near Chillicothe, Ross County, Ohio, and engaged in farming and stock raising
Governor of Ohio 1874-1876
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1875
resumed agricultural pursuits
died at Fruit Hill, July 11, 1879
interment in Grand View Cemetery, Chillicothe, Ohio.

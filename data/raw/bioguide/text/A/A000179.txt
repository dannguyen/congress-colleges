ANDERSEN, Herman Carl, a Representative from Minnesota
born in Newcastle, Kings County, Wash., January 27, 1897
moved with his parents to a farm near Tyler, Lincoln County, Minn., in 1901
attended the rural schools
attended the University of Washington and later the Naval Academy
engaged in cattle raising and agricultural pursuits 1919-1925 and as a civil engineer 1925-1930
resumed agricultural pursuits near Tyler, Minn., 1930-1938
member of the State house of representatives in 1935
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth and to the eleven succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1939-January 3, 1963)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination as an Independent in 1962 to the Eighty-eighth Congress
resided in Falls Church, Va.
died in Arlington, Va., July 26, 1978
cremated
ashes interred in Danebod Lutheran Cemetery, Tyler, Minn.

ANDERSON, Charles Marley, a Representative from Ohio
born near Mifflintown, Juniata County, Pa., January 5, 1845
moved to Ohio in 1855 with his parents, who settled in Darke County
attended the common schools
was graduated from the Lebanon Normal School, Lebanon, Ohio, in 1868
enlisted in the Union Army and served from March 15, 1861, in Company B, Seventy-first Regiment, Ohio Volunteer Infantry, until discharged on November 30, 1865
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1868 and commenced practice in Greenville, Ohio
manager of the Central Branch of the National Soldiers Home, Dayton, Ohio, for twenty years
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1885-March 3, 1887)
was an unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1886
resumed the practice of law
Ohio State commissioner to the Worlds Fair at Chicago in 1892 and 1893
died in Greenville, Ohio, December 28, 1908
interment in Greenville Cemetery.

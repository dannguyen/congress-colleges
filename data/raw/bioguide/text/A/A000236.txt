ANDERSON, William Black, a Representative from Illinois
born in Mount Vernon, Ill., April 2, 1830
attended the common schools
was graduated from McKendree College, Lebanon, Ill., in 1850
surveyor of Jefferson County in 1851
studied law
was admitted to the bar but never practiced
engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives in 1856 and 1858
during the Civil War entered the Union Army as a private in the Sixtieth Regiment, Illinois Volunteer Infantry
commissioned lieutenant colonel of the regiment February 17, 1862, and colonel, April 4, 1863
brevetted brigadier general of Volunteers March 13, 1865
resigned December 26, 1864
member of the constitutional convention of Illinois in 1869
served in the State senate in 1871
elected as an Independent to the Forty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1877)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1876
collector of internal revenue for the southern district of Illinois 1885-1889
United States pension agent in Chicago from November 9, 1893, to January 17, 1898
died in Chicago, Ill., August 28, 1901
interment in Oakwood Cemetery, Mount Vernon, Ill.

ANKENY, Levi, a Senator from Washington
born near St. Joseph, Buchanan County, Mo., August 1, 1844
crossed the plains to Oregon in 1850 with his parents and settled in Portland
attended the rural schools and Kingsley Academy, Portland, Oreg.
engaged in business in Lewiston, Orofino, and Florence, Idaho
interested in the cattle business
first mayor of Lewiston
moved to Walla Walla, Wash., and engaged in banking
appointed a member of the Pan American Exposition Commission and became its chairman
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate, and served from March 4, 1903, to March 3, 1909
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1908
chairman, Committee on Coast and Insular Survey (Fifty-eighth and Fifty-ninth Congresses), Committee on Irrigation (Fifty-ninth Congress), Committee on Irrigation and Reclamation (Sixtieth Congress)
engaged in banking in Walla Walla, Wash., until his death on March 29, 1921
interment in Masonic Cemetery.

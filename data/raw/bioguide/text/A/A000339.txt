AUSTIN, Albert Elmer, (stepfather of Clare Boothe Luce), a Representative from Connecticut
born in Medway, Norfolk County, Mass., November 15, 1877
attended the public schools and was graduated from Amherst (Mass.) College in 1899 and from Jefferson Medical College, Philadelphia, Pa., in 1905
member of the faculty of Attleboro (Mass.) High School 1899-1900
practicing physician in Old Greenwich, Conn., 1907-1939
health officer of Greenwich, Conn., 1917-1937
engaged in banking in Old Greenwich, Conn., 1926-1942
during the First World War served as regimental surgeon in the Two Hundred and Fourteenth Engineers, Fourteenth (Wolverine) Division, 1918-1919
member of the State house of representatives 1917-1919 and 1921-1923
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth Congress (January 3, 1939-January 3, 1941)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1940 to the Seventy-seventh Congress
continued his former professional pursuits until his death in Greenwich, Conn., January 26, 1942
interment in Ferncliff Cemetery, Hartsdale, Westchester County, N.Y.

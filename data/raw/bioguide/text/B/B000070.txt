BAKER, Lucien, (brother of John Harris Baker), a Senator from Kansas
born near Cleveland, Fulton County, Ohio, June 8, 1846
moved with his parents to Morenci, Mich.
attended the public schools and graduated from Adrian College, Michigan, and from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor
admitted to the bar in 1868 and commenced practice in Leavenworth, Kans., in 1869
city attorney of Leavenworth 1872-1874
member of the State senate 1893-1895
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1895, to March 3, 1901
unsuccessful candidate for renomination
chairman, Committee on Civil Service and Retrenchment (Fifty-sixth Congress)
resumed the practice of law in Leavenworth, Kans., where he died on June 21, 1907
interment in Mount Muncie Cemetery.

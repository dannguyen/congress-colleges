BAKER, Osmyn, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Amherst, Mass., May 18, 1800
attended Amherst Academy
was graduated from Yale College in 1822
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Amherst in 1825
member of the State house of representatives 1833, 1834, 1836, and 1837
county commissioner of Hampshire County 1834-1837
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of James C. Alvord
reelected to the Twenty-seventh and Twenty-eighth Congresses and served from January 14, 1840, to March 3, 1845
chairman, Committee on Accounts (Twenty-seventh Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1844
resumed the practice of law at Northampton in 1845
first president of Smith Charities 1860-1870
died in Northampton, Mass., February 9, 1875
interment in Bridge Street Cemetery.

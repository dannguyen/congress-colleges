BAKER, Robert, a Representative from New York
born at Bury St. Edmunds, England, in April 1862
attended the common schools
immigrated to the United States in 1882 and settled in Albany, N.Y.
moved to Brooklyn, N.Y., in 1889
unsuccessful candidate for election to the State assembly in 1894
auditor of New York City in 1902
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1903-March 3, 1905)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1904 to the Fifty-ninth Congress and for election in 1906 to the Sixtieth Congress
became secretary of the New York City Department of Docks and Ferries in 1906
engaged in stone paving and in the general real-estate business in Brooklyn, N.Y., until his death there on June 15, 1943
interment in Evergreen Cemetery.

BAKER, William Henry, a Representative from New York
born in Lenox Township, Madison County, N.Y., January 17, 1827
moved with his parents to Oswego County in 1829
attended the common schools and Red Creek and Mexico Academies
studied law
was admitted to the bar in Syracuse, N.Y., in November 1851 and commenced practice in Cleveland, N.Y.
moved to Constantia, Oswego County, N.Y., in 1853
served as district attorney for Oswego County from January 1863 to January 1870
elected as a Republican to the Forty-fourth and Forty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1879)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1878
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1884
engaged in agricultural pursuits
died in Constantia, N.Y., November 25, 1911
interment in Trinity Church Cemetery.

BARBOUR, Henry Ellsworth, a Representative from California
born in Ogdensburg, St. Lawrence County, N.Y., March 8, 1877
attended the public schools of his native city, the local Free Academy at Ogdensburg, Union College at Schenectady, N.Y., and the law department of George Washington University, Washington, D.C.
was admitted to the New York bar in 1901
moved to Fresno, Fresno County, Calif., in 1902 and engaged in the practice of law
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-sixth and to the six succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1919-March 3, 1933)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1932 to the Seventy-third Congress
resumed the practice of his profession in Fresno, Calif., where he died on March 21, 1945
interment in Belmont Memorial Cemetery.

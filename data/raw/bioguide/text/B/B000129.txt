BARBOUR, John Strode, Jr., (son of the John Strode Barbour), a Representative and a Senator from Virginia
born at Catalpa, near Culpeper, Culpeper County, Va., December 29, 1820
attended the common schools and graduated from the law department of the University of Virginia at Charlottesville
admitted to the bar in 1841 and commenced practice in Culpeper
member of the State house of delegates 1847-1851
president of the Orange & Alexandria Railroad Co. 1852-1881
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-seventh, and the two succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1881-March 3, 1887)
chairman, Committee on the District of Columbia (Forty-eighth and Forty-ninth Congresses)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1886
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1889, until his death in Washington, D.C., May 14, 1892
interment in the burial ground at Poplar Hill, Prince Georges County, Md.

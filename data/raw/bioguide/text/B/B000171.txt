BARR, Samuel Fleming, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Coleraine, County Antrim, Ireland, June 15, 1829
immigrated to the United States in 1831 with his parents, who settled in Harrisburg, Pa.
attended the common schools
freight agent of the Pittsburgh, Fort Wayne & Chicago Railroad in 1855 and 1856
early in the Civil War was employed upon government railways in and about Washington, D.C.
editor of the Harrisburg (Pa.) Telegraph 1873-1878
elected as a Republican to the Forty-seventh and Forty-eighth Congresses (March 4, 1881-March 3, 1885)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1884
lived in retirement until his death, residing in San Diego, Calif., in the winter and in Seal Harbor, Maine, during the summer season
died in San Diego, Calif., May 29, 1919
interment in Odd Fellows Cemetery.

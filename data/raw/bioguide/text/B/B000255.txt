BAYLIES, Francis, (brother of William Baylies), a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Taunton, Mass., October 16, 1783
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1810 and commenced practice in Taunton, Mass.
register of probate for Bristol County 1812-1820
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1818 to the Sixteenth Congress
elected as a Federalist to the Seventeenth Congress, a Jackson Federalist to the Eighteenth Congress, and a Jacksonian to the Nineteenth Congress (March 4, 1821-March 3, 1827)
unsuccessful candidate in 1827 for reelection to the Twentieth Congress
member of the State house of representatives 1827-1832
United States Chargé dAffaires to Argentina, 1832
again elected to the State house of representatives in 1835
engaged in literary pursuits
died in Taunton, Bristol County, Mass., October 28, 1852
interment in the Old Plain Cemetery.

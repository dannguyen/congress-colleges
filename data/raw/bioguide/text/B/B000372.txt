BENNETT, Charles Goodwin, a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y., December 11, 1863
attended the public schools
was graduated from the Brooklyn High School and from the New York Law School in 1882
was admitted to the bar in 1882 and commenced practice in Brooklyn, N.Y.
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1892 to the Fifty-third Congress
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth and Fifty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1899)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1898 to the Fifty-sixth Congress
Secretary of the United States Senate from January 29, 1900, to March 3, 1913, when a successor was elected
returned to Brooklyn, N.Y., discontinued active business pursuits, and lived in retirement until his death on May 25, 1914
interment in Evergreen Cemetery.

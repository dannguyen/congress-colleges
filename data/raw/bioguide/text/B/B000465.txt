BILLMEYER, Alexander, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Liberty Township, Montour County, Pa., January 7, 1841
attended the common schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
interested in the manufacture of lumber
director of a national bank in Washingtonville, Montour County, Pa.
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-seventh Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Rufus K. Polk and served from November 4, 1902, to March 3, 1903
was not a candidate for renomination in 1902
resumed agricultural pursuits in Montour County, Pa.
died near Washingtonville, Pa., May 24, 1924
interment in Odd Fellows Cemetery, Danville, Pa.

BLACK, Loring Milton, Jr., a Representative from New York
born in New York City, May 17, 1886
attended the public schools and was graduated from Fordham University, New York City, in 1907
studied law at Columbia University, New York City
was admitted to the bar in 1909 and commenced practice in New York City
member of the State senate in 1911 and 1912
resumed the practice of his profession in New York City
again a member of the State senate in 1919 and 1920
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-eighth and to the five succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1923-January 3, 1935)
chairman, Committee on Claims (Seventy-second and Seventy-third Congresses)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1934
resumed the practice of law in New York City and Washington, D.C.
died in Washington, D.C., May 21, 1956
interment in Fort Lincoln Cemetery.

BLAKE, Thomas Holdsworth, a Representative from Indiana
born in Calvert County, Md., June 14, 1792
attended the public schools
studied law in Washington, D.C.
member of the militia of the District of Columbia which took part in the Battle of Bladensburg in 1814
moved to Kentucky and thence to Indiana
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Terre Haute, Ind.
prosecuting attorney and judge of the circuit court
abandoned the practice of law to engage in business
member of the State house of representatives
elected as an Adams to the Twentieth Congress (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1829)
was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1828 to the Twenty-first Congress
was appointed Commissioner of the General Land Office by President Tyler on May 19, 1842, and served until April 1845
chosen president of the Erie & Wabash Canal Co.
visited England as financial agent of the State of Indiana and, while returning, died in Cincinnati, Ohio, November 28, 1849
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery, Terre Haute, Ind.

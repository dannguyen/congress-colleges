BOARDMAN, William Whiting, (son of Elijah Boardman), a Representative from Connecticut
born in New Milford, Conn., October 10, 1794
attended Bacon Academy, Colchester, Conn.
was graduated from Yale College in 1812
studied law in Cambridge and Litchfield Law Schools and commenced the practice of his profession in New Haven in 1819
clerk of the State senate in 1820
judge of probate
member of the State house of representatives 1836-1839, serving as speaker in 1836 and 1839
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of William L. Storrs
reelected to the Twenty-seventh Congress and served from December 7, 1840, to March 3, 1843
chairman, Committee on Public Buildings and Grounds (Twenty-seventh Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1842 to the Twenty-eighth Congress
member of the State house of representatives in 1845, 1849, and 1851, serving as speaker in 1845
resumed the practice of law
died in New Haven, Conn., August 27, 1871
interment in Grove Street Cemetery.

BOATNER, Charles Jahleal, a Representative from Louisiana
born in Columbia, Caldwell Parish, La., January 23, 1849
completed preparatory studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1870 and practiced
member of the State senate from 1876 until May 1878
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-first, Fifty-second, and Fifty-third Congresses (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1895)
presented credentials as a Member-elect to the Fifty-fourth Congress but on March 20, 1896, the House declared the seat vacant, the election having been contested by Alexis Benoit
elected to fill the vacancy caused by the House declaring the seat vacant and served from June 10, 1896, to March 3, 1897
declined to be a candidate for reelection in 1896
moved to New Orleans and resumed the practice of law
died in New Orleans, La., on March 21, 1903
interment in Monroe Cemetery, Monroe, La.

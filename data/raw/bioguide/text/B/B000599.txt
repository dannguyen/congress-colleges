BOKEE, David Alexander, a Representative from New York
born in New York City, October 6, 1805
attended the public schools
engaged in mercantile pursuits
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
president of the Brooklyn Board of Aldermen 1840-1843 and 1845-1848
member of the State senate 1846-1849
trustee of the New York Life Insurance Co., 1848-1860
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1851)
appointed by President Fillmore as naval officer of customs of the port of New York and served from 1851 to 1853
engaged as a shipping merchant
died in Washington, D.C., March 15, 1860
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

BOOTH, Walter, a Representative from Connecticut
born in Woodbridge, Conn., December 8, 1791
attended the common schools
settled in Meriden and engaged in manufacturing
colonel of the Tenth Regiment, Second Battalion of Militia, 1825-1827, brigadier general in 1827 and 1828, and major general of the First Division 1831-1834
judge of the county court in 1834
member of the State house of representatives in 1838
elected as a Free-Soiler to the Thirty-first Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1851)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1850 to the Thirty-second Congress
resumed his former manufacturing pursuits
died in Meriden, New Haven County, Conn., April 30, 1870
interment in East Cemetery.

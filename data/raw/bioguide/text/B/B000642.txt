BORLAND, Solon, a Senator from Arkansas
born near Suffolk, Nansemond County, Va., September 21, 1808
attended preparatory schools in North Carolina
studied and afterwards practiced medicine
settled in Little Rock, Ark.
served throughout the Mexican War as a major in the Arkansas Volunteer Cavalry
was appointed and subsequently elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Ambrose H. Sevier and served from March 30, 1848, to April 11, 1853, when his resignation became effective
chairman, Committee on Printing (Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses), Committee on Public Lands (Thirty-third Congress)
served as United States Minister to Nicaragua and to the other Central American Republics 1853-1854
declined an appointment as Governor of the Territory of New Mexico
returned to Arkansas and resumed the practice of medicine in Little Rock until 1861
during the Civil War raised a brigade of troops for the Confederate Army
later was appointed a brigadier general in the Confederate Army
died near Houston, Tex., on January 1, 1864
interment in City Cemetery, Houston, Tex.

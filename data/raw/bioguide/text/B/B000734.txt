BRADBURY, James Ware, a Senator from Maine
born in Parsonsfield, Maine, June 10, 1802
attended the common schools and Gorham Academy
graduated from Bowdoin College, Brunswick, Maine, in 1825
principal of Hallowell Academy and founder of the first normal school in New England, at Effingham, N.H., in 1829
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Augusta, Maine, in 1830
prosecuting attorney 1834-1838
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1847, until March 3, 1853
declined to be a candidate for reelection
chairman, Committee on Printing (Thirtieth Congress), Committee on Retrenchment (Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses)
trustee of Bowdoin College 1861
president of the Maine Historical Society 1867-1887
practiced law in Augusta, Maine
died in Augusta, Maine, January 6, 1901
interment in Forest Grove Cemetery.

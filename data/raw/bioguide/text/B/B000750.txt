BRADLEY, Willis Winter, a Representative from California
born in Ransomville, Niagara County, N.Y., June 28, 1884
moved with his parents to Milnor, N.Dak., in July 1884 and to Forman, N.Dak., in 1891
attended the public schools, and Hamlin University, St. Paul, Minn.
deputy registrant of deeds of Sargent County, N.Dak., in 1902 and 1903
was graduated from the United States Naval Academy in 1906
during the First World War served as gunnery officer and as chief of the Explosives Section, Bureau of Ordnance, Navy Department
awarded the Congressional Medal of Honor
Governor of Guam 1929-1931
captain of the Pearl Harbor Navy Yard 1933-1935
attached to the Board of Inspection and Survey, Pacific Coast Section, 1940-1946
in 1946 retired from the United States Navy because of physical incapacity incurred in line of duty
took up residence in Long Beach, Calif., in 1931
elected as a Republican to the Eightieth Congress (January 3, 1947-January 3, 1949)
was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1948 to the Eighty-first Congress
assistant to the president of the Pacific Coast Steamship Co., 1949-1952
member of the State assembly from 1952 until his death
died in Santa Barbara, Calif., August 27, 1954
interment in Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery, San Diego, Calif.

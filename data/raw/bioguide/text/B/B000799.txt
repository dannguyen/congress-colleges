BRENNAN, Martin Adlai, a Representative from Illinois
born in Bloomington, McLean County, Ill., September 21, 1879
attended parochial schools
employed as a reporter for the Bloomington Bulletin
was graduated from the Wesleyan College of Law, Bloomington, Ill., in 1902
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice in Bloomington, Ill.
served as presiding judge of the Illinois Court of Claims 1913-1917
served as census supervisor for McLean County, Ill., in 1920
member of the State house of representatives 1921-1923
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1924
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-third and Seventy-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1933-January 3, 1937)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1936
resumed the practice of law in Bloomington, Ill., until his death there on July 4, 1941

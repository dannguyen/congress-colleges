BRIGGS, Frank Parks, a Senator from Missouri
born in Armstrong, Howard County, Mo., February 25, 1894
attended Armstrong and Fayette schools and Central College at Fayette, Mo. 1911-1914
graduated from the University of Missouri at Columbia in 1915
engaged in the newspaper business in 1915 and in the publishing business at Macon, Mo., in 1925
mayor of Macon, Mo. 1930-1932
member, State senate 1933-1944
appointed on January 18, 1945, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Harry S. Truman and served from January 18, 1945, to January 3, 1947
unsuccessful candidate for election to the United States Senate in 1946
resumed the newspaper publishing business
chairman, Missouri State Conservation Commission 1955-1956
Assistant Secretary of the Interior for Fish and Wildlife 1961-1965
was a resident of Macon, Mo., until his death on September 23, 1992
interment in Walnut Ridge Cemetery, Fayette, Mo.

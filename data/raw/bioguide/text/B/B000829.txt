BRIGGS, George, a Representative from New York
born near Broadalbin, Fulton County, N.Y., May 6, 1805
moved to Vermont, in 1812 with his parents, who settled in Bennington
attended the public schools
engaged in business as a dealer in hardware
member of the Vermont house of representatives in 1837
returned to New York, settled in New York City in 1838, and continued in the hardware business
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1853)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1852
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1859-March 3, 1861)
chairman, Committee on Revolutionary Claims (Thirty-sixth Congress)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1860 and retired
delegate to the Union National Convention at Philadelphia in 1866
died at his summer home, Woodlawn, at Saratoga Springs, N.Y., June 1, 1869
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, New York City.

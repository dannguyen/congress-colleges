BROMBERG, Frederick George, a Representative from Alabama
born in New York City June 19, 1837
moved with his parents to Mobile, Ala., in February 1838
attended the public schools
was graduated from Harvard University in 1858
studied chemistry at Harvard University 1861-1863
tutor of mathematics at Harvard University 1863-1865
appointed treasurer of the city of Mobile in July 1867 by Maj. Gen. John Pope, who commanded the department, and served until January 19, 1869
member of the State senate 1868-1872
appointed postmaster of Mobile in July 1869 but was removed in June 1871
chairman of the Alabama delegation to the Liberal Republican Convention at Cincinnati in 1872
elected as a Liberal Republican to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1875)
unsuccessfully contested the election of Jeremiah Haralson to the Forty-fourth Congress
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1877 and commenced practice in Mobile, Ala.
Alabama commissioner of the WorldÂ’s Columbian Exposition at Chicago in 1893
president of the State bar association in 1906
died in Mobile, Ala., on September 4, 1930
interment in Magnolia Cemetery.

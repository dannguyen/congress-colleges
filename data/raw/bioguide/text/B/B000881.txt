BROOKS, James, a Representative from New York
born in Portland, Maine, November 10, 1810
attended the public schools
attended the academy at Monmouth, Maine
taught school at sixteen years of age in Lewiston
was graduated from Waterville (Maine) College in 1831
studied law and also edited the Portland Advertiser, and in 1832 was its Washington correspondent
member of the State house of representatives in 1835
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1836 to the Twenty-fifth Congress
moved to New York City in 1836 and established the New York Daily Express, of which he was editor in chief the remainder of his life
served in the State assembly in 1847
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1853)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1852 to the Thirty-third Congress
resumed his editorial pursuits
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1863-March 3, 1865)
presented credentials as a Member-elect to the Thirty-ninth Congress and served from March 4, 1865, to April 7, 1866, when he was succeeded by William E. Dodge, who contested the election
elected to the Fortieth and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1867, until his death

member of the State constitutional convention in 1867
appointed a Government director of the Union Pacific Railroad in October 1867
died in Washington, D.C., April 30, 1873
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

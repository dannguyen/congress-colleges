BROOM, James Madison, (father of Jacob Broom), a Representative from Delaware
born near Wilmington, Del., in 1776
was graduated from Princeton College in 1794
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1801 and practiced in New Castle and Wilmington, Del., and Baltimore, Md.
elected as a Federalist to the Ninth and Tenth Congresses and served from March 4, 1805, until his resignation in 1807, before the assembling of the Tenth Congress
moved to Philadelphia, Pa., in 1819 and resumed the practice of law
member of the Pennsylvania house of representatives in 1824
died in Philadelphia, Pa., January 15, 1850
interment in St. Mary's Churchyard, Hamilton Village (now a part of Philadelphia), Pa.

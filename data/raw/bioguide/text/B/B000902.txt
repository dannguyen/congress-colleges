BROWN, Arthur, a Senator from Utah
born near Kalamazoo, Kalamazoo County, Mich., March 8, 1843
attended the common schools and graduated from Antioch College, Yellow Springs, Ohio, in 1862
pursued graduate work at the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor
graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan in 1864
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Kalamazoo
moved to Salt Lake City, Utah, in 1879
upon the admission of Utah as a State into the Union was elected as a Republican to the United States Senate and served from January 22, 1896, until March 3, 1897
was not a candidate for renomination
resumed the practice of law in Salt Lake City
shot in Washington, D.C. on December 8, 1906, by a woman who claimed to be the mother of his children, and died on December 12
interment in Mount Olivet Cemetery, Salt Lake City, Utah.

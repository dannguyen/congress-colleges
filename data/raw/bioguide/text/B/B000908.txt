BROWN, Charles Harrison, a Representative from Missouri
born in Coweta, Wagoner County, Okla., October 22, 1920
attended the public schools in Humansville and Republic, Mo., and high school in Springfield, Mo.
attended Drury College, Springfield, Mo., in 1937, 1938, and 1940, and George Washington University, Washington, D.C., in 1939
program director for a radio station in Springfield, Mo., in 1937 and 1938
radio publicity director for Missouri Conservation Commission in 1940
account executive for an advertising company in St. Louis, Mo., 1943-1945
founder and president of Brown Radio-TV Productions, Inc., Springfield, Mo.
partner, Brown Brothers Advertising Agency, Nashville, St. Louis, and Springfield
delegate to Democratic State and National Conventions in 1956, 1960, and 1964
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-fifth and Eighty-sixth Congresses (January 3, 1957-January 3, 1961)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1960 to the Eighty-seventh Congress
public relations consultant in Washington, D.C., and Los Angeles
senior vice president of an oil refining company in Los Angeles, 1973-1979
died on June 10, 2003, in Henderson, Nev.

BROWN, Jeremiah, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Little Britain (now Fulton) Township, Lancaster County, Pa., April 14, 1785
engaged in milling and agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives in 1826
delegate to the convention to revise the State constitution in 1836
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-seventh and Twenty-eighth Congresses (March 4, 1841-March 3, 1845)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1844 to the Twenty-ninth Congress
first associate judge for Lancaster and served from 1851 to 1856
died in Goshen, Fulton Township, Lancaster County, Pa., March 2, 1858
interment in the cemetery adjoining Penn Hill Quaker Meeting House, Little Britain (later Fulton) Township, Pa.

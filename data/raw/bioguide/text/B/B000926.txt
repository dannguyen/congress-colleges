BROWN, John, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Kishacoquillas Valley, near Lewistown, Mifflin County, Pa., August 12, 1772
attended the common schools
moved to Lewistown, Pa., in 1800
engaged in the gristmill and sawmill business
member of the State house of representatives 1809-1813
elected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress and as a Jackson Republican to the Eighteenth Congresses (March 4, 1821-March 3, 1825)
resumed his former business pursuits
moved to Limestone, Buncombe County, N.C., in 1827 and engaged in agricultural pursuits and in the real estate business
died in a section of Buncombe County, N.C., then called Limestone, near Skyland, on October 12, 1845
interment in Riverside Cemetery, Asheville, N.C.

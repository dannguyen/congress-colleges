BROWN, John Robert, a Representative from Virginia
born near Snow Creek, Franklin County, Va., January 14, 1842
attended private schools in Franklin and Henry Counties
entered the Confederate Army in 1861 as a private in Company D, Twenty-fourth Regiment, Virginia Volunteers
formed a partnership with his father in the tobacco business at Shady Grove in 1870
moved to Martinsville, Henry County, in 1882 and continued in the tobacco business
also engaged in banking
mayor of Martinsville 1884-1888
elected as a Republican to the Fiftieth Congress (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1889)
unsuccessfully contested the election of Claude A. Swanson to the Fifty-fifth Congress
reengaged in the tobacco business
retired from active business pursuits
died in Martinsville August 4, 1927
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

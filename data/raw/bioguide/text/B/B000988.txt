BRYAN, John Heritage, a Representative from North Carolina
born in New Bern, N.C., November 4, 1798
studied under private teachers and attended New Bern Academy
was graduated from the University of North Carolina in 1815
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1819 and commenced practice in New Bern, N.C.
member of the State senate in 1823 and 1824
trustee of the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill 1823-1868
elected as Jacksonian to the Nineteenth Congress and as an Adams candidate in the Twentieth Congress (March 4, 1825-March 3, 1829)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1828
resumed the practice of law in New Bern
moved to Raleigh in 1839 and continued the practice of law
died in Raleigh, N.C., May 19, 1870
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

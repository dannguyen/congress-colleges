BRYCE, Lloyd Stephens, a Representative from New York
born in Flushing, Queens County, N.Y., September 4, 1851
attended the public schools and Georgetown University, Washington, D.C.
was graduated from Oxford University, England, in 1869
studied law at Columbia Law School, New York City
paymaster general for the State of New York in 1886 and 1887
elected as a Democrat to the Fiftieth Congress (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1889)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1888 to the Fifty-first Congress
editor of the North American Review 1889-1896
appointed Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary to the Netherlands August 12, 1911, and served to September 10, 1913
died in Flushing, N.Y., April 2, 1917
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

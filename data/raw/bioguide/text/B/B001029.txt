BUCKMAN, Clarence Bennett, a Representative from Minnesota
born in Doylestown, Bucks County, Pa., April 1, 1851
attended the public and normal schools
moved to Minnesota in 1872 and settled in what is now known as Buckman
engaged in agricultural pursuits and in the lumber business
appointed justice of the peace in 1873
member of the State house of representatives 1881-1883
served in the State senate 1887-1891 and 1899-1903
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-eighth and Fifty-ninth Congresses (March 4, 1903-March 3, 1907)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1906
deputy United States marshal 1912-1917
resumed the lumber business in Little Falls, Morrison County, Minn.
died in Battle Creek, Mich., March 1, 1917
interment in Oakland Cemetery, Little Falls, Minn.

BUEL, Alexander Woodruff, a Representative from Michigan
born in Castleton, Vt., December 13, 1813
attended the public schools in Poultney, Vt., and was graduated from Middlebury College, Vermont, in 1830
taught school and studied law
moved to Detroit, Mich., in 1834
was admitted to the bar in 1835 and commenced practice in Detroit, Mich.
city attorney in 1837
member of the State house of representatives in 1838 and 1848, serving as speaker the latter year
prosecuting attorney for Wayne County 1843-1846
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-first Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1851)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1850 to the Thirty-second Congress
resumed the practice of law
again a member of the State house of representatives, in 1859 and 1860
appointed postmaster of Detroit on September 28, 1860, and served until March 18, 1861
died in Detroit, Mich., April 19, 1868
interment in Elmwood Cemetery.

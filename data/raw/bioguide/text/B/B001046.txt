BULL, John, a Representative from Missouri
born in Virginia in 1803
studied medicine in Baltimore, Md.
moved to Howard County, Mo., and settled near Glasgow
engaged in the practice of medicine
studied theology
was ordained to the ministry and became a Methodist minister in that locality
unsuccessful candidate for Governor of Missouri
presidential elector on the ticket of Jackson and Calhoun in 1828
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian candidate to the Twenty-third Congress (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1835)
resumed his ministerial duties and also the practice of medicine
died near Rothville, Chariton County, Mo., in February 1863
interment in Hutcheson Cemetery, a family burial ground, near Rothville, Mo.

BUNDY, Hezekiah Sanford, a Representative from Ohio
born in Marietta, Ohio, August 15, 1817
moved with his parents to Athens County in 1819
attended the public schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1850 and practiced until 1860, when he became engaged in the iron business
member of the State house of representatives in 1848 and 1850
served in the State senate in 1855
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1862 to the Thirty-eighth Congress
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1865-March 3, 1867)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1866
elected to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1875)
chairman, Committee on Mileage (Forty-third Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Forty-fourth Congress
moved to Wellston, Jackson County, in 1887 and resumed the practice of law
elected to the Fifty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of William H. Enochs and served from December 4, 1893, to March 3, 1895
died in Wellston, Jackson County, Ohio, December 12, 1895
interment in the City Cemetery.

BURKE, Thomas A., a Senator from Ohio
born in Cleveland, Cuyahoga County, Ohio, October 30, 1898
attended parochial schools
graduated from Holy Cross College, Worcester, Mass., in 1920, and Western Reserve University Law School, Cleveland, Ohio, in 1923
during the First World War served in the United States Army
admitted to the bar in 1923 and commenced practice in Cleveland, Ohio
assistant prosecutor of Cuyahoga County 1930-1936
special counsel to the Ohio attorney general in 1937
director of law for the city of Cleveland 1942-1945
mayor of Cleveland 1945-1953
president of the National Conference of Mayors in 1953
appointed on October 13, 1953, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Robert A. Taft and served from November 10, 1953, to December 2, 1954
unsuccessful candidate for election to the vacancy in 1954
resumed the practice of law
died in Cleveland, Ohio, December 5, 1971
interment in Calvary Cemetery.

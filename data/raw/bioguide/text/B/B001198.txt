BUTMAN, Samuel, a Representative from Maine
born in Worcester, Worcester County, Mass., in 1788
moved to Maine in 1804, and settled in Dixmont, Penobscot County
engaged in agricultural pursuits
served as a captain in the War of 1812
member of the State constitutional convention in 1820
member of the house of representatives of Maine in 1822, 1826, and 1827
elected as an Adams candidate to the Twentieth Congress and as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1831)
county commissioner of Penobscot County in 1846
served in the State senate and was its president in 1853
died in Plymouth, Maine, October 9, 1864.

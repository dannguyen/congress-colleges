BRADLEY, William Warren (Bill), a Senator from New Jersey
born in Crystal City, Jefferson County, Mo., July 28, 1943
attended Crystal City public schools
graduated, Princeton University 1965
attended Oxford University, Oxford, England, as a Rhodes Scholar and received a graduate degree in 1968
represented the United States in 1964 Olympic Games (basketball) at Tokyo, Japan
served in the United States Air Force Reserve 1967-1978
author
professional basketball player 1967-1977
businessman
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate in 1978
reelected in 1984 and 1990 and served from January 3, 1979, to January 3, 1997
was not a candidate for reelection in 1996
was an unsuccessful candidate for the Democratic presidential nomination in 2000
senior advisor and vice chairman of the International Council of J.P. Morgan & Co., Inc. 1997-1999
essayist for CBS evening news
visiting professor, Stanford University, Notre Dame University and the University of Maryland 1997-1999
chief outside advisor, McKinsey & Company 2001-2004
managing director, Allen & Company LLC.

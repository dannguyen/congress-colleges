CADWALADER, John, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., April 1, 1805
was graduated from the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia in 1821
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1825 and commenced practice in Philadelphia
solicitor for the Bank of the United States in 1830
captain of a military company during the riots of 1844 in Philadelphia
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1857)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1856
resumed the practice of law in Philadelphia
appointed judge of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania in 1858 and served until his death in Philadelphia, Pa., January 26, 1879
interment in Christ Churchyard.

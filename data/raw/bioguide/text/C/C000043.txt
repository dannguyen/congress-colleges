CALHOON, John, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Henry County, Ky., in 1797
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
member of the State house of representatives in 1820, 1821, 1829, and 1830
unsuccessful candidate for election to the Twentieth Congress
received the credentials of an election as an Adams candidate to the Twentieth Congress, held November 5-7, 1827, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of United States Representative William S. Young, but, in order to avoid a contest, resigned and, together with his opponent, Thomas Chilton, petitioned the Governor for a new election
was again unsuccessful
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress and as a Whig to the Twenty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1839)
was not a candidate for reelection to the Twenty-sixth Congress
moved to St. Louis, Mo., in 1839
resumed the practice of law
returned to Kentucky
appointed judge of the fourteenth judicial district in January 1842
death date unknown.

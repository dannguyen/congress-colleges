CALKINS, William Henry, a Representative from Indiana
born in Pike County, Ohio, February 18, 1842
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
during the Civil War served in the Union Army from May 1861 to December 1865, except three months in 1863, attached to the Fourteenth Iowa Infantry and the Twelfth Indiana Cavalry
took up his residence in La Porte, Ind.
StateÂ’s attorney for the ninth Indiana judicial circuit 1866-1870
member of the State house of representatives in 1871
elected as a Republican to the Forty-fifth and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1877, to October 20, 1884, when he resigned
chairman, Committee on Elections (Forty-seventh Congress)
moved to Tacoma, Wash., and resumed the practice of law
appointed United States associate justice of the Territory of Washington in April 1889 and served until November 11, 1889, when the Territory was admitted as a State into the Union
died in Tacoma, Wash., on January 29, 1894
interment in Tacoma Cemetery.

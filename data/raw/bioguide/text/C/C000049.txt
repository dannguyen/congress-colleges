CALL, Jacob, a Representative from Indiana
born in Kentucky, birth date unknown
was graduated from an academy in Kentucky
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Vincennes and Princeton, Ind.
judge of the Knox County Circuit Court, 1817, 1818, and 1822-1824
elected as a Jackson Republican to the Eighteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of United States Representative William Prince (December 23, 1824-March 3, 1825)
died in Frankfort, Ky., April 20, 1826.

CAMPBELL, John Pierce, Jr., a Representative from Kentucky
born near Hopkinsville, Christian County, Ky., December 8, 1820
pursued an academic course
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1841 and commenced practice in Lexington, Mo.
member of the Missouri house of representatives 1848-1852
returned to Hopkinsville, Ky., and engaged in agricultural pursuits
elected as a candidate of the American Party to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1857)
declined to be a candidate for reelection
president of the Henderson & Nashville Railroad in 1870
organized the Mastodon Coal & Iron Co., which was succeeded by the St. Bernard Coal Co.
devoted the latter years of his life to his large landed estates
died in Hopkinsville, Ky., October 29, 1888
interment in Riverside Cemetery.

CARR, John, a Representative from Indiana
born in Uniontown, Perry County, Ind., April 9, 1793
moved with his parents to Clark County, Ind., in 1806
attended the public schools
fought in the Battle of Tippecanoe
appointed lieutenant in a company of United States Rangers, authorized by an act of Congress for defense of western frontiers, in 1812
brigadier general and major general of the Indiana Militia until his death
county clerk 1824-1830
presidential elector for Jackson and Calhoun in 1824
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-second, Twenty-third, and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1831-March 3, 1837)
chairman, Committee on Private Land Claims (Twenty-fourth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate in 1836 for reelection to the Twenty-fifth Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1841)
died in Charlestown, Clark County, Ind., January 20, 1845
interment in the Old Cemetery.

CARR, Nathan Tracy, a Representative from Indiana
born in Corning, Steuben County, N.Y., December 25, 1833
attended the common schools, and was graduated from Starkey Academy in 1851
moved to Midland County, Mich.
studied law
was admitted to the Midland County bar in 1858 and commenced practice at Vassar, Mich.
member of the State house of representatives 1858-1860
recorder of Midland County in 1861 and 1862
served as a lieutenant in the Second Regiment, Michigan Volunteer Infantry, in 1862
moved to Columbus, Ind., in 1867
prosecuting attorney for Bartholomew, Shelby, Jackson, and Brown Counties in 1870
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Michael C. Kerr and served from December 5, 1876, to March 3, 1877
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1876
resumed the practice of law in Columbus, Bartholomew County, Ind.
appointed judge of the ninth judicial circuit court of Indiana in 1878
died in Columbus, Ind., May 28, 1885
interment in the City Cemetery.

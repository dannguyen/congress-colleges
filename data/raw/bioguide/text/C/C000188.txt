CARROLL, James, a Representative from Maryland
born in Baltimore, Md., December 2, 1791
was graduated from old St. Marys College at Baltimore in 1808
studied law but did not practice
settled on a farm on West River
returned to Baltimore, Md., in 1831
judge of the orphans court
trustee of the poor
served as a director of the Baltimore & Ohio Railroad Company and the Chesapeake & Ohio Canal Company
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1841)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1840 to the Twenty-seventh Congress
unsuccessful candidate for Governor of Maryland in 1844
retired from political life
died in Baltimore, Md., January 16, 1873
interment in St. Pauls Burying Ground.

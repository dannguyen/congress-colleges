CASE, Walter, a Representative from New York
born in Pleasant Valley, Dutchess County, N.Y., in 1776
educated by private tutors
attended Newburgh Academy, and was graduated from Union College, Schenectady, N.Y., in 1799
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1802 and commenced practice in Newburgh
elected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1821)
affiliated with the Whig Party after its formation
resumed the practice of law
moved to New York City in 1844 and continued the practice of law until 1848, when he retired
died in Fishkill, Dutchess County, N.Y., October 7, 1859
interment in Fishkill Rural Cemetery.

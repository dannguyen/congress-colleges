CHAFFEE, Calvin Clifford, a Representative from Massachusetts
born at Saratoga Springs, N.Y., on August 28, 1811
attended the common schools
studied medicine, and was graduated from the medical school of Middlebury College, Middlebury, Vt., in 1835
settled in Springfield, Mass., where he began the practice of his profession
elected on the American Party ticket to the Thirty-fourth Congress and as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1858
librarian of the House of Representatives 1860-1862
settled in Washington, D.C., and engaged in the practice of medicine until 1876, when he moved to Springfield, Mass.
president of the Union Relief Association 1880-1893
died in Springfield, Hampden County, Mass., on August 8, 1896
interment in Springfield Cemetery.

CHALMERS, William Wallace, a Representative from Ohio
born in Strathroy, Ontario, Canada, November 1, 1861
moved with his parents to Kent County, near Grand Rapids, Mich., in 1865
attended the public schools, and Michigan State Normal School
was graduated from the University of Michigan at Ann Harbor in 1887, from Eureka (Ill.) College in 1889, and from Heidelberg University, Tiffin, Ohio, in 1904
teacher and principal of schools until 1890
superintendent of schools in Grand Rapids, Mich., 1890-1898 and in Toledo, Ohio, 1898-1905
president of Toledo University in 1904
engaged at different periods in farming, lumbering and, in the real-estate and insurance business at Toledo, Ohio
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1921-March 3, 1923)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1922 to the Sixty-eighth Congress
elected to the Sixty-ninth, Seventieth, and Seventy-first Congresses (March 4, 1925-March 3, 1931)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1930
died in Indianapolis, Ind., on October 1, 1944
interment in Crown Hill Cemetery.

CHAPIN, Chester William, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Ludlow, Mass., December 16, 1798
attended the common schools and Westfield Academy, Westfield, Mass.
engaged in mercantile pursuits
mail contractor, running post coaches and steamboats
member of the constitutional convention of Massachusetts in 1853
president and director of the Western Railroad Corporation 1854-1867
president of the Boston & Albany Railroad Co. 1868-1878, and one of the directors 1868-1880
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1877)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1876 to the Forty-fifth Congress
died in Springfield, Hampden County, Mass., on June 10, 1883
interment in Springfield Cemetery.

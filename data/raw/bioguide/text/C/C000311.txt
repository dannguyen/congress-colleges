CHAPMAN, Henry, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Newtown, Pa., February 4, 1804
attended Doylestown Academy and Doctor Gummeres private boys school near Burlington, N.J.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1825 and commenced practice in Doylestown
member of the State senate in 1843
judge of the fifteenth judicial district 1845-1849
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1858
judge of the Bucks County Court in 1861
retired in 1871
died at Frosterley, near Doylestown, Bucks County, Pa., April 11, 1891
interment in the graveyard of Doylestown Presbyterian Church.

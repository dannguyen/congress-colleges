CHARLES, William Barclay, a Representative from New York
born in Glasgow, Scotland, April 3, 1861
attended private schools and high schools in Stirling and Glasgow, Scotland
immigrated to the United States in 1884
spent two years ranching in Texas and Mexico
settled in Amsterdam, N.Y., in 1886 and engaged in textile manufacturing
member of the State assembly 1904-1906
director of the Amsterdam First National Bank
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1915-March 3, 1917)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1916
reengaged in the textile business until his retirement
died in Amsterdam, N.Y., November 25, 1950
interment in Green Hill Cemetery.

CHASE, Dudley, (uncle of Salmon Portland Chase and Dudley Chase Denison), a Senator from Vermont
born in Cornish, N.H., December 30, 1771
attended the common schools, and graduated from Dartmouth College, Hanover, N.H., in 1791
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1793 and practiced in Randolph, Vt.
prosecuting attorney for Orange County 1803-1812
member, State house of representatives 1805-1812, and served as speaker 1808-1812
delegate to the State constitutional conventions in 1814 and 1822
elected as a Democratic Republican to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1813, to November 3, 1817, when he resigned
chairman, Committee on Judiciary (Fourteenth Congress)
chief justice of the supreme court of Vermont 1817-1821
member, State house of representatives 1823-1824
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1825, to March 3, 1831
engaged in agricultural pursuits
died in Randolph Center, Vt., February 23, 1846
interment in Randolph Cemetery.

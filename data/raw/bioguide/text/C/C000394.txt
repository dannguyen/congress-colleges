CILLEY, Bradbury, (uncle of Jonathan Cilley and Joseph Cilley), a Representative from New Hampshire
born in Nottingham, Rockingham County, N.H., on February 1, 1760
attended the common schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
appointed by President John Adams as United States marshal for the district of New Hampshire on March 19, 1798, and served until May 3, 1802
elected as a Federalist to the Thirteenth and Fourteenth Congresses (March 4, 1813-March 3, 1817)
colonel and aide on the staff of Governor Gillman 1814-1816
retired from public life
died in Nottingham, N.H., December 17, 1831
interment in the General Joseph Cilley Burying Ground in Nottingham Square.

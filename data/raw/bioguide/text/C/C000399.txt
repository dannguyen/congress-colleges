CLAGETT, Clifton, a Representative from New Hampshire
born in Portsmouth, N.H., December 3, 1762
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Litchfield in 1787
elected as a Federalist to the Eighth Congress (March 4, 1803-March 3, 1805)
appointed a justice of the peace and quorum in 1808
appointed judge of probate for Hillsborough County in 1810 and served until his resignation in 1812, having been appointed to a judicial position
moved to Amherst in 1812
appointed a judge of the supreme court in 1812
member of the State house of representatives in 1816
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress and reelected to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1817-March 3, 1821)
appointed judge of probate August 5, 1823, and held the office until his death in Amherst, Hillsborough County, New Hampshire, January 25, 1829.

CLARK, Horace Francis, a Representative from New York
born in Southbury, Conn., November 29, 1815
was graduated from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass., in 1833
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in New York City in 1837, where he was prominent in financial, political, and railroad circles
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fifth Congress and reelected as an Anti-Lecompton Democrat to the Thirty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1861)
became director of the New York & Harlem Railroad, and subsequently was president of the Union Pacific, the Michigan Southern, and many other railroads
active manager of the Western Union Telegraph Co. and president of the Union Trust Co.
died in New York City on June 19, 1873
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

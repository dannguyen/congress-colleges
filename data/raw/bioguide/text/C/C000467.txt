CLARKE, Marian Williams, (wife of John Davenport Clarke), a Representative from New York
born at Standing Stone, Bradford County, Pa., July 29, 1880
moved with her parents to Cheyenne, Wyo., in 1881
attended the public schools and spent one year in the art school of the University of Nebraska at Lincoln
was graduated from Colorado College at Colorado Springs in 1902
resided in seven different States from 1881 to 1918
moved to Delaware County, N.Y., in 1918 and settled on a farm near Fraser
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of her husband, John Davenport Clarke, and served from December 28, 1933, to January 3, 1935
was a candidate for renomination in 1934 to the Seventy-fourth Congress, but withdrew her name before the primary election
returned to her farm, Arbor Hill, near Delhi, N.Y., where she resided until 1950
died in Cooperstown, N.Y., April 8, 1953
interment in Locust Hill Cemetery, Hobart, N.Y.

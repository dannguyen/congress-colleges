CLAY, Brutus Junius, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Richmond, Madison County, Ky., July 1, 1808
attended the common schools and was graduated from Centre College, Danville, Ky.
engaged in agricultural pursuits and stock raising
moved to Bourbon County in 1837 and continued former pursuits
member of State house of representatives in 1840
elected president of Bourbon County Agricultural Association in 1840 and served thirty years
president of the Kentucky Agricultural Association 1853-1861
again a member of the State house of representatives in 1860
elected as a Unionist to the Thirty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1863-March 3, 1865)
chairman, Committee on Agriculture (Thirty-eighth Congress)
was not a candidate for reelection
resumed former pursuits
died near Paris, Ky., October 11, 1878
interment in the family burial ground at Auvergne, near Paris, Ky.

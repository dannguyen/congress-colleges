CLAY, James Franklin, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Henderson, Henderson County, Ky. October 29, 1840
attended public and private schools at Henderson
was graduated from Georgetown College, Kentucky, in June 1860
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1862 and commenced practice in Henderson, Ky.
member of the State senate in 1870
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1885)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1884
resumed the practice of his profession in Henderson, Ky.
served as city attorney and as attorney for the St. Louis & Southern Railroad and the Ohio Valley Railway Co.
died in Henderson, Ky., on August 17, 1921
interment in Fernwood Cemetery.

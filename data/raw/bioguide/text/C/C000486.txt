born in Philadelphia, Pa., July 24, 1769
elected as a Republican to the Eighth, Ninth, and Tenth Congresses, and served from March 4, 1803, until his resignation after March 28, 1808
engaged in banking
chairman, Committee on Ways and Means (Ninth Congress)
one of the managers appointed by the House of Representatives in 1804 to conduct the impeachment proceedings against John Pickering, judge of the United States District Court for New Hampshire

died in Philadelphia, Pa., on August 27, 1811

CLAYTON, Augustin Smith, a Representative from Georgia
born in Fredericksburg, Va., November 27, 1783
moved with his parents to Richmond County, Ga., in 1784
attended Richmond Academy, and was graduated from Franklin College, Athens, Ga., in 1804
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1806 and commenced practice in Carnesville, Franklin County
moved to Athens
selected by the legislature in 1810 to compile the statutes of Georgia from 1800
member of the State house of representatives 1810-1812
clerk of the State house of representatives 1813-1815
served in the State senate in 1826 and 1827
judge of the superior court 1819-1825 and 1828-1831
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-second Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Wilson Lumpkin
reelected to the Twenty-third Congress and served from January 21, 1832, to March 3, 1835
resumed the practice of law in Athens, Ga., and died there June 21, 1839
interment in Oconee Cemetery.

CLOPTON, David, a Representative from Alabama
born in Putnam County, near Milledgeville, Ga., September 29, 1820
attended the county schools and Edenton (Ga.) Academy
was graduated from Randolph-Macon College, Boydton, Va., in 1840
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1841 and commenced practice in Milledgeville, Ga.
moved to Tuskegee, Ala., in 1844, and continued the practice of his profession
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-sixth Congress and served from March 4, 1859, to January 21, 1861, when he withdrew
during the Civil War enlisted as a private in the Confederate Army in the Twelfth Alabama Infantry for one year
elected as a Representative to the First and Second Confederate Congresses and served from 1862 to 1864
appointed judge of the supreme court of Alabama October 30, 1884, and served until his death
died in Montgomery, Ala., February 5, 1892
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

COKE, Richard, Jr., (uncle of Richard Coke [1829-1897]), a Representative from Virginia
born in Williamsburg, Va., November 16, 1790
completed preparatory studies, and was graduated from the College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Va.
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Gloucester County, Va.
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first and Twenty-second Congresses (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1833)
died on his plantation, Abingdon Place, in Gloucester County, Va., March 31, 1851
interment in the family burying ground on the estate.

CONNOLLY, Daniel Ward, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Cochecton, Sullivan County, N.Y., April 24, 1847
moved with his parents to Scranton, Pa., in 1849
attended the public schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar in June 1870 and commenced practice in Scranton
elected president judge of Lackawanna County in 1878 but did not serve because the State supreme court held that there was no vacancy
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1880 to the Forty-seventh Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1885)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1884 to the Forty-ninth Congress
appointed postmaster of Scranton, Pa., on May 2, 1885, and served until March 29, 1889
died in Scranton, Pa., December 4, 1894
interment in Forest Hill Cemetery.

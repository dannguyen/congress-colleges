CONRY, Michael Francis, a Representative from New York
born in Shenandoah, Schuylkill County, Pa., April 2, 1870
employed in the coal mines until crippled for life
attended the public schools
engaged in teaching for seven years
was graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor in 1896
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Scranton, Pa.
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1900 to the Fifty-seventh Congress
moved to New York City and resumed the practice of law
served two years as assistant corporation counsel of the city of New York
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-first and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1909, until his death
had been reelected to the Sixty-fifth Congress
died in Washington, D.C., March 2, 1917
interment in Calvary Cemetery, New York City.

CONSTABLE, Albert, a Representative from Maryland
born near Charlestown, Md., June 3, 1805
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1829 and settled in Bel Air, Harford County, Md.
moved to Baltimore and practiced law
later moved to Perryville, Cecil County, Md.
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1845-March 3, 1847)
judge of the circuit court of Maryland in 1851
died in Camden, N.J., September 18, 1855.

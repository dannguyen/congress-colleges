COOK, Samuel Ellis, a Representative from Indiana
born on a farm in Huntington County, Ind., September 30, 1860
attended the common schools in Whitley County and the normal schools at Columbia City, Ind., and Ada, Ohio
taught school and engaged in agricultural pursuits
studied law
was graduated from the law department of Valparaiso University, Indiana, in 1888
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice in Huntington, Ind.
prosecuting attorney for Huntington County 1892-1894
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1896
editorial writer for the Huntington News-Democrat 1896-1900
judge of the Huntington circuit court for the fifty-sixth judicial district 1906-1918
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1923-March 3, 1925)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1924 to the Sixty-ninth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Huntington, Ind., where he died February 22, 1946
interment in Mount Hope Cemetery.

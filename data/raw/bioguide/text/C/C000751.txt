COOPER, Henry, (brother of Edmund Cooper), a Senator from Tennessee
born in Columbia, Maury County, Tenn., on August 22, 1827
attended Dixon Academy, Shelbyville, Tenn., and graduated from Jackson (Tenn.) College in 1847
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1850 and commenced practice in Shelbyville
member, State house of representatives 1853-1855, 1857-1859
appointed judge of the seventh judicial circuit of Tennessee in April 1862 and resigned in January 1866
professor in the law school at Lebanon, Tenn. 1866-1867
moved to Nashville where he resumed the practice of law
member, State senate 1869-1870
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1871, to March 3, 1877
was not a candidate for renomination in 1876
was killed by bandits in Tierra Blanca, Guadelupe y Calvo, Mexico, on February 4, 1884, where he was engaged in mining operations
interment in Tierra Blanca.

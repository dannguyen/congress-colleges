CORNING, Erastus, (grandfather of Parker Corning), a Representative from New York
born in Norwich, Conn., December 14, 1794
moved to Troy, N.Y., and thence, in 1814, to Albany, where he established himself in iron manufacturing
served in the State senate 1842-1845
alderman of Albany
mayor 1834-1837
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Thirty-sixth Congress
member of the peace conference of 1861
elected to the Thirty-seventh and Thirty-eighth Congresses and served from March 4, 1861, to October 5, 1863, when he resigned
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1867
died in Albany, N.Y., April 9, 1872
interment in Rural Cemetery.

CRAIL, Joe, a Representative from California
born in Fairfield, Jefferson County, Iowa, December 25, 1877
attended the public schools and was graduated from Drake University, Des Moines, Iowa, in 1898
during the Spanish-American War enlisted as a private in the Twelfth Company, United States Volunteer Signal Corps
promoted to corporal and served in the American Army of Occupation in Cuba until its withdrawal
studied law at Iowa College of Law, Des Moines, Iowa
was admitted to the bar in 1903 and commenced practice in Fairfield, Iowa
moved to California in 1913, settled in Los Angeles, and practiced law until elected to Congress
served as chairman of the Republican State central committee for southern California 1918-1920
elected as a Republican to the Seventieth, Seventy-first, and Seventy-second Congresses (March 4, 1927-March 3, 1933)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1932, but was an unsuccessful candidate for nomination as United States Senator
resumed the practice of law
also engaged in banking
died in Los Angeles, Calif., March 2, 1938
interment in Inglewood Park Mausoleum, Inglewood, Calif.

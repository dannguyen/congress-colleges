CRAVENS, William Fadjo, (son of William Ben Cravens), a Representative from Arkansas
born in Fort Smith, Sebastian County, Ark., February 15, 1899
attended the public schools, the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville, the University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, Pa., and was graduated from the law school of Washington and Lee University, Lexington, Va., in 1920
was admitted to the bar in 1920 and commenced practice at Fort Smith, Ark.
during the First World War served as a seaman in the United States Navy
city attorney of Fort Smith, Ark., for ten years
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-sixth Congress, by special election, September 12, 1939, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of his father, William Ben Cravens
reelected to the four succeeding Congresses and served from September 12, 1939, to January 3, 1949
was not a candidate for renomination in 1948 to the Eighty-first Congress
died in Fort Smith, Ark., April 16, 1974
interment in Forest Park.

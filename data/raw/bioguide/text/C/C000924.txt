CROMER, George Washington, a Representative from Indiana
born near Anderson, Madison County, Ind., May 13, 1856
attended the common schools and Wittenberg College, Springfield, Ohio, and was graduated from the Indiana University at Bloomington in 1882
became editor of the Muncie (Ind.) Times in 1883
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1886 and commenced practice in Muncie, Delaware County, Ind.
prosecuting attorney for the forty-sixth judicial circuit of Indiana 1886-1890
member of the State Republican committee in 1892 and 1894
mayor of Muncie 1894-1898
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1899-March 3, 1907)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1906 to the Sixtieth Congress
resumed the practice of his profession in Muncie, Ind., until his death in that city November 8, 1936
interment in Beech Grove Cemetery.

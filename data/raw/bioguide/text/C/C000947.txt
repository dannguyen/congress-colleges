CROWNINSHIELD, Jacob, (brother of Benjamin Williams Crowninshield), a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Salem, Mass., March 31, 1770
engaged in mercantile pursuits
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1798 to the Sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Dwight Foster
member of the State senate in 1801
was tendered the position of Secretary of the Navy by President Jefferson, but never entered upon his duties on account of ill health
elected as a Republican to the Eighth, Ninth, and Tenth Congresses and served from March 4, 1803, until his death in Washington, D.C., on April 15, 1808
chairman, Committee on Commerce and Manufactures (Ninth Congress)
interment in Harmony Grove Cemetery, Salem, Mass.

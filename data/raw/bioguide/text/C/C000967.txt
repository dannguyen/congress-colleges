CULBRETH, Thomas, a Representative from Maryland
born in Kent County, Del., eight miles northeast of Greensboro, Md., April 13, 1786
attended the public schools and studied under private tutors
moved to Denton, Caroline County, Md., in 1806
was clerk in a store in Denton
member of the congressional committee at Hillsboro in 1810
member of the State house of delegates in 1812 and 1813
cashier of the State Bank at Denton in 1813
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress and reelected to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1817-March 3, 1821)
declined to be a candidate for reelection in 1820 to the Seventeenth Congress and for election in 1822 to the Eighteenth Congress
appointed chief judge of the Caroline County orphans court in 1822
clerk of the executive council of Maryland 1825-1838, and resided in Annapolis, Md.
returned to Denton, Md., 1838 and engaged in mercantile pursuits
soon afterward moved to Orrell Farm, near Greensboro, where he died April 16, 1843
interment in the family cemetery on the farm.

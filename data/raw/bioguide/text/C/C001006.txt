CURTIS, Carl Thomas, a Representative and a Senator from Nebraska
born near Minden, Kearney County, Nebr., March 15, 1905
attended the public schools, and Nebraska Wesleyan University at Lincoln
teacher in the Minden, Nebr., schools in 1927
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1930 and commenced practice in Minden
county attorney of Kearney County, Nebr. 1931-1934
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth Congress and to the seven succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1939, until his resignation December 31,1954
was not a candidate for reelection
elected as a Republican in 1954 to the United States Senate for the six-year term commencing January 3, 1955
subsequently appointed by the Governor, January 1, 1955, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Hazel H. Abel for the term ending January 3, 1955
reelected in 1960, 1966, and again in 1972 and served from January 1, 1955, to January 3, 1979
was not a candidate for reelection in 1978
chairman, Republican Conference (1975-1979)
practiced law in Lincoln, Nebr. where he was a resident until his death on January 24, 2000
interment in Minden Cemetery in Minden, Nebr.

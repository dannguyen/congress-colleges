CUTTING, Francis Brockholst, a Representative from New York
born in New York City August 6, 1804
attended Bensel School and was also tutored privately
studied law in the Litchfield (Conn.) Law School
was admitted to the bar in 1827 and commenced practice in New York City
member of the State assembly in 1836 and 1837
was not a candidate for reelection
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1836 to the Twenty-fifth Congress
member of the board of aldermen in 1843
city recorder
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1855)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1854
resumed the practice of law
died in New York City June 26, 1870
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

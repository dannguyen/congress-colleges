DAVIES, John Clay, a Representative from New York
born in Albany, N.Y., May 1, 1920
attended Camden (N.Y.) High School
attended the University of Alabama at Tuscaloosa, Ala., and Hamilton College, Clinton, N.Y.
editor of the Camden (N.Y.) Chronicle in 1940 and 1941
maintained publicity office in Albany 1941-1943
with public relations department, Westinghouse Electric Corp., in New York City 1943-1946
vice president of the Earle Ferris Co. Inc., in New York City 1946-1948
partner in public relations business, Utica, N.Y., 1948-1953
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-first Congress (January 3, 1949-January 3, 1951)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Eighty-second Congress in 1950
writer
public relations executive in San Juan, Puerto Rico
died on June 17, 2002, in San Juan, Puerto Rico.

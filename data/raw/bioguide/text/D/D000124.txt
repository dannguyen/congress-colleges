DAVIS, Lowndes Henry, a Representative from Missouri
born in Jackson, Cape Girardeau County, Mo., December 13, 1836
was graduated from Yale College in 1860 and from the Louisville University Law School in 1863
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Jackson, Mo.
State attorney for the tenth judicial district of Missouri 1868-1872
member of the State constitutional convention in 1875
member of the State house of representatives 1876-1878
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-sixth, Forty-seventh, and Forty-eighth Congresses (March 4, 1879-March 3, 1885)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of the Treasury (Forty-eighth Congress)
engaged in agricultural pursuits and in stock raising
died in Cape Girardeau, Mo., February 4, 1920
interment in Maple Hill Cemetery, Huntsville, Ala.

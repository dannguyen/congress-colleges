DAVIS, Timothy, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Gloucester, Mass., April 12, 1821
attended the public schools
served two years in a printing office
engaged in mercantile pursuits in Boston
member of the State house of representatives in 1870 and 1871
elected as the candidate of the American Party to the Thirty-fourth Congress and as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1860
appointed assistant appraiser in the Boston customhouse in 1861
engaged in the prosecution of claims against the Government
died in Boston, Mass., on October 23, 1888
interment in Oak Grove Cemetery.

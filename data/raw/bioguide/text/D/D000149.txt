DAWES, Rufus, (father of Vice President Charles Gates Dawes and Beman Gates Dawes), a Representative from Ohio
born in Malta, Morgan County, Ohio, July 4, 1838
attended the common schools, and was graduated from Marietta College, Ohio, in 1860
during the Civil War volunteered on April 25, 1861, and was chosen captain of Company K, Sixth Wisconsin Regiment, in the Army of the Potomac
appointed major June 21, 1862, lieutenant colonel March 24, 1863, colonel on July 6, 1864, and brevet brigadier general March 13, 1865
after the close of the war engaged in the wholesale lumber business in Marietta, Ohio
elected as a Republican to the Forty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1881-March 3, 1883)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1882 to the Forty-eighth Congress
resumed the wholesale lumber business in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio, and died there August 2, 1899
interment in Oak Grove Cemetery.

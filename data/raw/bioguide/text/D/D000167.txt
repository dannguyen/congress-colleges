DE ARMOND, David Albaugh, a Representative from Missouri
born in Blair County, Pa., March 18, 1844
attended the public schools and Williamsport Dickinson Seminary
moved to Davenport, Iowa, in 1866
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1867 and commenced practice in Davenport
moved to Missouri in 1869 and settled in Greenfield, Dade County
member of the Missouri state senate, 1879-1883
Missouri Supreme Court commissioner, 1884
judge of the twenty-second judicial circuit of Missouri, 1886-1890
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-second and to the nine succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1891-November 23, 1909)
one of the managers appointed by the House of Representatives in 1905 to conduct the impeachment proceedings against Charles Swayne, judge of the United States District Court for the Northern District of Florida
died in Butler, Bates County, Mo., November 23, 1909
interment in Oak Hill Cemetery.

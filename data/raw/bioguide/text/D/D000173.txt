DEAN, Josiah, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Raynham, Mass., March 6, 1748
attended the common schools
engaged in the rolling-mill and shipbuilding business
selectman in 1781
town clerk in 1805
served in the State senate 1804-1807
elected as a Republican to the Tenth Congress (March 4, 1807-March 3, 1809)
member of the State house of representatives in 1810 and 1811
resumed his former business pursuits
died in Raynham, Mass., October 14, 1818
interment in Pleasant Street Cemetery.

DEAN, Sidney, a Representative from Connecticut
born in Glastonbury, Conn., November 16, 1818
attended the common schools and Wilbraham and Suffield Academies
minister in the Methodist Episcopal Church from 1843 to 1853, when he retired from the ministry because of impaired health
engaged in manufacturing in Putnam, Conn.
member of the Connecticut house of representatives in 1854 and 1855
elected as the candidate of the American Party to the Thirty-fourth Congress and as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
chairman, Committee on Public Expenditures (Thirty-fourth Congress)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1858
in 1860 reentered the ministry, with pastorates in Pawtucket, Providence, and finally in Warren, R.I.
during the period 1865-1880 engaged as editor of the Providence Press, Providence Star, and Rhode Island Press
served in the Rhode Island senate in 1870 and 1871
engaged in literary pursuits and lecturing
died in Brookline, Norfolk County, Mass., October 29, 1901
interment in South Cemetery, Warren, R.I.

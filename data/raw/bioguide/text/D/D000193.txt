DE GRAFF, John Isaac, a Representative from New York
born in Schenectady, N.Y., October 2, 1783
attended the common schools and Union College, Schenectady, N.Y., in 1811
engaged in mercantile pursuits in Schenectady
served in the War of 1812
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twentieth Congress (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1829)
mayor of Schenectady 1832-1834 and again in 1836
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1837-March 3, 1839)
was not a candidate for renomination
engaged in mercantile pursuits
interested in the building of the Mohawk & Hudson Railroad
again served as mayor of Schenectady in 1842 and 1845
engaged in banking until his death in Schenectady, N.Y., July 26, 1848
interment in Vale Cemetery.

DEGETAU, Federico, a Resident Commissioner from Puerto Rico
born in Ponce, P.R., December 5, 1862
attended the common schools and Central College of Ponce
completed an academic course at Barcelona, Spain, and was graduated from the law department of Central University of Madrid
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Madrid, Spain
returned to Puerto Rico
one of the four commissioners sent by Puerto Rico to ask Spain for autonomy
settled in San Juan and continued the practice of law
member of the municipal council of San Juan in 1897
mayor of San Juan in 1898
deputy to the Spanish Cortes of 1898
appointed by General Henry secretary of the interior of the first American cabinet that was formed in Puerto Rico in 1899
appointed by General Davis a member of the insular board of charities
writer and author
first vice president of the municipal council of San Juan in 1899 and 1900
president of the board of education of San Juan in 1900 and 1901
elected as a Puerto Rican Republican a Resident Commissioner to the United States in 1900
reelected in 1902, and served from March 4, 1901, until March 3, 1905
was not a candidate for renomination in 1904
resumed the practice of law
died in Santurce, Puerto Rico, January 20, 1914
interment in the Cemetery of San Juan.

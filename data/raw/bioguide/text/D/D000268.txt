DERSHEM, Franklin Lewis, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near New Columbia, Union County, Pa., March 5, 1865
attended the common schools
was graduated from PalmÂ’s National Business College at Philadelphia in 1887
appointed postmaster at Kelly Point, Union County, Pa., on March 9, 1888, and served until January 13, 1891
engaged in agricultural pursuits, and was also interested in the hardware business 1891-1913
member of the board of trustees of Albright College, Myerstown, Pa.
member of the State house of representatives in 1907, 1908, and again in 1911 and 1912
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress (March 4, 1913-March 3, 1915)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914 to the Sixty-fourth Congress
appointed as an auditor in the Philadelphia division of the United States Bureau of Internal Revenue October 1, 1915, in which capacity he served until March 31, 1935
was engaged as an auditor and income-tax specialist in Lewisburg, Pa., where he died February 14, 1950
interment in Lewisburg Cemetery.

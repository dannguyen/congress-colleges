DICKEY, Oliver James, (son of John Dickey), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Old Brighton, Beaver County, Pa., April 6, 1823
completed preparatory studies
attended Beaver Academy and Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa.
studied law
was admitted to the bar at Lancaster, Lancaster County, Pa., in 1844 and practiced
district attorney of Lancaster County 1856-1859
during the Civil War served as lieutenant colonel of the Tenth Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteers
elected as a Republican to the Fortieth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Thaddeus Stevens and on the same day was elected to the Forty-first Congress
reelected to the Forty-second Congress and served from December 7, 1868, to March 3, 1873
was not a candidate for renomination in 1872
delegate to the State constitutional convention at Harrisburg in 1873
resumed the practice of law in Lancaster, Pa., and died there April 21, 1876
interment in Woodward Hill Cemetery.

DICKINSON, Lester Jesse, (cousin of Fred Dickinson Letts), a Representative and a Senator from Iowa
born in Derby, Lucas County, Iowa, October 29, 1873
attended the public schools
graduated from Cornell College, Mount Vernon, Iowa, in 1898, and from the law department of the University of Iowa at Iowa City in 1899
admitted to the bar in 1899 and commenced practice in Algona, Iowa
second lieutenant in the Fifty-second Infantry, Iowa National Guard, 1900-1902
city clerk of Algona 1900-1904
prosecuting attorney of Kossuth County 1909-1913
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-sixth and to the five succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1919-March 3, 1931)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1930, having become a candidate for Senator
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate in 1930 and served from March 4, 1931, to January 3, 1937
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1936 and for election in 1938
resumed the practice of law in Des Moines, Iowa, where he died on June 4, 1968
interment in Algona Cemetery, Algona, Iowa.

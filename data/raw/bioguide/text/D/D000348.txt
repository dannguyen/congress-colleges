DILLON, Charles Hall, a Representative from South Dakota
born near Jasper, Dubois County, Ind., December 18, 1853
attended the public schools
was graduated from the academic department of Indiana University at Bloomington in 1874 and from its law department in 1876
was admitted to the bar in 1876 and commenced practice in Jasper, Ind.
moved to Marion, Iowa, in 1881, to Mitchell, Dakota Territory (now South Dakota), in 1882 and to Yankton in 1894 and continued the practice of law
delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1900 and 1908
member of the State senate 1903-1911
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-third, Sixty-fourth, and Sixty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1913-March 3, 1919)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1918
resumed the practice of law in Yankton
moved to Vermillion, S.Dak., in 1922, having been elected associate justice of the State supreme court, and served until November 15, 1926, when he resigned
unsuccessful candidate for nomination as United States Senator in 1924
retired in 1926
died in Vermillion, S.Dak., September 15, 1929
interment in Yankton Cemetery, Yankton, S.Dak.

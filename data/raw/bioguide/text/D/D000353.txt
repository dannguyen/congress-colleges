DIMOND, Anthony Joseph, a Delegate from the Territory of Alaska
born in Palatine Bridge, Montgomery County, N.Y., November 30, 1881
attended the public schools and St. MaryÂ’s Catholic Institute, Amsterdam, N.Y.
taught school in Montgomery County, N.Y., 1900-1903
prospector and miner in Alaska 1904-1912
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1913 and commenced practice in Valdez, Alaska
United States Commissioner at Chisana, Alaska, in 1913 and 1914
special assistant United States attorney for the third judicial division of Alaska at Valdez in 1917
mayor of Valdez 1920-1922 and 1925-1932
member of the Alaska Territorial senate 1923-1926 and 1929-1932
elected as a Democrat a Delegate to the Seventy-third and to the five succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1933-January 3, 1945)
was not a candidate for renomination, having been confirmed as district judge for the third division of Alaska, in which capacity he was serving at the time of death
died in Anchorage, Alaska, May 28, 1953
interment in Anchorage Cemetery.

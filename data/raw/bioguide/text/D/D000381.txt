DOBBIN, James Cochrane, (grandson of James Cochran), a Representative from North Carolina
born in Fayetteville, N.C., January 17, 1814
attended the Fayetteville Academy and the William Bingham School, Hillsboro, N.C.
was graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1832
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1835 and commenced practice in Fayetteville
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1845-March 3, 1847)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1846
resumed the practice of law
member of the State house of commons in 1848, 1850, and 1852, serving as speaker in 1850
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1852
Secretary of the Navy in the Cabinet of President Pierce from March 7, 1853, to March 6, 1857
died in Fayetteville, Cumberland County, N.C., August 4, 1857
interment in Cross Creek Cemetery.

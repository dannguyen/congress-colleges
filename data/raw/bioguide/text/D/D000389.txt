DODD, Edward, a Representative from New York
born in Salem, Washington County, N.Y., August 25, 1805
attended the public schools
engaged in mercantile pursuits
moved to Argyle, N.Y., in 1835
county clerk of Washington County 1835-1844
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1846
elected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress and reelected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
chairman, Committee on District of Columbia (Thirty-fourth Congress)
United States marshal for the northern district of New York from April 1863 to April 1869
editor of the County Post for thirty years
trustee of the Argyle Academy for fifty-one years
president of the village of Argyle for eight years
member of the Republican State committee for many years
died in Argyle, N.Y., March 1, 1891
interment in Prospect Hill Cemetery.

DODGE, William Earle, a Representative from New York
born in Hartford, Conn., September 4, 1805
completed preparatory studies
moved to New York City in 1818
became a clerk
in 1826 established the house of Phelps, Dodge & Co., of which he was the head for forty years
delegate to the peace convention of 1861 held in Washington, D.C., in an effort to devise means to prevent the impending war
successfully contested as a Republican the election of James Brooks to the Thirty-ninth Congress and served from April 7, 1866, to March 3, 1867
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1866
resumed business interests
died in New York City February 9, 1883
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

DOVENER, Blackburn Barrett, a Representative from West Virginia
born in Tays Valley, Cabell County, Va. (now West Virginia), April 20, 1842
attended the common schools
taught school 1858-1861
at the age of nineteen raised a company and served as captain of Company A, Fifteenth Regiment, West Virginia Volunteer Infantry
became captain of an Ohio River steamboat in 1867
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1873 and commenced practice in Wheeling, W.Va.
member of the State house of delegates in 1883 and 1884
unsuccessful Republican candidate for election to the Fifty-second Congress
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth and to the five succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1907)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination
resumed the practice of law in Wheeling
lived in retirement at Glen Echo, Md., until his death on May 9, 1914
interment in Arlington National Cemetery.

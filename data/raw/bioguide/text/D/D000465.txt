DOWDNEY, Abraham, a Representative from New York
born in Youghal, Ireland, October 31, 1841
immigrated to the United States with his parents, who settled in New York City
attended private schools
engaged in the building and contracting business
served in the Civil War as captain in the One Hundred and Thirty-second Regiment, New York Volunteer Infantry, in 1862 and 1863
chairman of the public-school trustees of New York City 1882-1885
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth Congress and served from March 4, 1885, until his death in New York City December 10, 1886
interment in Calvary Cemetery, Long Island City, N.Y.

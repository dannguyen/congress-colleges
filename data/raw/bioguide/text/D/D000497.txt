DRIGGS, Edmund Hope, a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y., May 2, 1865
attended the public schools and Adelphi Academy in Brooklyn
became engaged in the casualty-insurance business
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-fifth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Francis H. Wilson
reelected to the Fifty-sixth Congress and served from December 6, 1897, to March 3, 1901
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1900 to the Fifty-seventh Congress
resumed the casualty-insurance business and also engaged in safety engineering
died in Brooklyn, N.Y., September 27, 1946
interment in Cypress Hills Cemetery.

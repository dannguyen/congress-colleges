DRUM, Augustus, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Greensburg, Pa., November 26, 1815
received private instruction and attended Greensburg Academy
was graduated from Jefferson College (now Washington and Jefferson), Canonsburg, Pa.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1836 and commenced practice in Greensburg
member of the State senate in 1852 and 1853
held several local offices
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1855)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1854 to the Thirty-fourth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Greensburg, Westmoreland County, Pa., and died there September 15, 1858
interment in St. Clair Cemetery.

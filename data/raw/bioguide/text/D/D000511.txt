DUDLEY, Charles Edward, a Senator from New York
born in Johnston Hall, Staffordshire, England, May 23, 1780
immigrated to the United States with his mother, who settled in Newport, R.I., in 1794
entered a counting room as clerk
moved to Albany, N.Y., where he engaged in the mercantile business
member of the State senate 1820-1825
mayor of Albany 1821-1824, 1828-1829
elected to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Martin Van Buren and served from January 15, 1829, to March 3, 1833
became interested in astronomical science
died in Albany, N.Y., January 23, 1841
interment in the Rural Cemetery.

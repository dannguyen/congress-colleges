DUNHAM, Ransom Williams, a Representative from Illinois
born in Savoy, Berkshire County, Mass., March 21, 1838
attended the common schools and the high school in Springfield, Mass., engaged as a clerk for a life insurance company 1855-1857
moved to Chicago in 1857
became a grain and provision commission merchant
president of the Board of Trade of Chicago in 1882
elected as a Republican to the Forty-eighth, Forty-ninth, and Fiftieth Congresses (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1889)
retired from active business pursuits
died in Springfield, Hampden County, Mass., on August 19, 1896, while en route to attend the centennial celebration of his native town, Savoy
interment in Mount Hope Cemetery, Chicago, Ill.

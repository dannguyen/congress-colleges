DUNLAP, William Claiborne, a Representative from Tennessee
born in Knoxville, Tenn., February 25, 1798
attended the Ebenezer Academy and Maryville College, Maryville, Tenn., 1813-1817
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Knoxville in 1819
served in the Indian campaign in 1818 and 1819
moved to Bolivar, Hardeman County, Tenn., in 1828
held a commission in the United States Volunteers in 1830
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1836 to the Twenty-fifth Congress
judge of the Eleventh Circuit Court of Tennessee from 1840 to 1849, when he resigned and resumed the practice of law
member of the State senate in 1851, 1853, and 1857
served in the State house of representatives 1857-1859
died near Memphis, Shelby County, Tenn., November 16, 1872
interment in Elmwood Cemetery, Memphis, Tenn.

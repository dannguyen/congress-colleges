DURAND, George Harman, a Representative from Michigan
born in Cobleskill, Schoharie County, N.Y., February 21, 1838
attended the common schools and Genesee Wesleyan Seminary at Lima, N.Y.
moved to Oxford, Oakland County, Mich., in 1856
taught school
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice at Flint, Genesee County, Mich., in 1858
member of the board of education
member of the board of aldermen 1862-1867
mayor of Flint in 1873 and 1874
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1877)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1876 to the Forty-fifth Congress
resumed the practice of his profession
appointed temporarily justice of the Michigan Supreme Court in 1892
president of the State board of law examiners for many years
appointed special assistant United States attorney in Chinese and opium smuggling cases in Oregon and served from 1893 to 1896
died in Flint, Mich., June 8, 1903
interment in Glenwood Cemetery.

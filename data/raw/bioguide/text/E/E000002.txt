EAGER, Samuel Watkins, a Representative from New York
born in Neelytown, Orange County, N.Y., on April 8, 1789
attended Montgomery Academy, Montgomery, N.Y., and was graduated from Princeton College in 1809
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1811 and commenced practice in Newburgh, N.Y.
moved to Montgomery, N.Y., in 1826, and continued the practice of his profession
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian candidate to the Twenty-first Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Hector Craig and served from November 2, 1830, to March 3, 1831
was not a candidate at the election held the same day for the Twenty-second Congress
returned to Newburgh in 1836 and engaged in literary pursuits
died in Newburgh, N.Y., December 23, 1860
interment in St. George Cemetery.

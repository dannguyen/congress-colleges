EDWARDS, Samuel, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Chester Township, Delaware County, Pa., March 12, 1785
attended the common schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1806 and commenced practice in Chester
served in the War of 1812
member of the State house of representatives 1814-1816
elected as Federalist to the Sixteenth and Seventeenth Congresses
elected as a Jackson Federalist to the Eighteenth Congress
and reelected as a Jacksonian to the Nineteenth Congress (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1827)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of Navy (Seventeenth and Eighteenth Congresses)
resumed the practice of his profession in Chester
inspector of customs 1838-1842
died in Chester, Pa., November 21, 1850
interment in Chester Rural Cemetery.

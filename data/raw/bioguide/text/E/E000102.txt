ELDER, James Walter, a Representative from Louisiana
born in Grand Prairie, Dallas County, Tex., October 5, 1882
attended the public schools, and Baylor University, Waco, Tex., 1895-1901
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1903 and commenced practice in Farmerville, Union Parish, La.
mayor of Farmerville, La.
moved to Monroe, Ouachita Parish, and continued the practice of his profession
member of the State senate 1908-1912
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress (March 4, 1913-March 3, 1915)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1914
returned to the practice of law in Farmerville, La., until January 1, 1925
moved to Ruston, La., and continued the practice of law until his death on December 16, 1941
interment in Greenwood Cemetery.

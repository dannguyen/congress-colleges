ELLIOTT, Robert Brown, a Representative from South Carolina
born in England., August 11, 1842
attended public school in England
journalist
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Columbia, S.C.
member of the State constitutional convention in 1868
member of the State house of representatives from July 6, 1868, to October 23, 1870
assistant adjutant general of South Carolina 1869-1871
elected as a Republican to the Forty-second and Forty-third Congresses and served from March 4, 1871, until his resignation, effective November 1, 1874
again a member of the State house of representatives 1874-1876, and served as speaker
unsuccessful candidate for election as attorney general of South Carolina in 1876
moved to New Orleans, La., in 1881 and practiced law until his death there on August 9, 1884
interment in St. Louis Cemetery No. 2.

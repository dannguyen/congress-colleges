ELLIS, Caleb, a Representative from New Hampshire
born in Walpole, Mass., April 16, 1767
was graduated from Harvard University in 1793
studied law and was admitted to the bar
moved to Newport, N.H., and then to Claremont
member of the New Hampshire house of representatives in 1803
elected as a Federalist to the Ninth Congress (March 4, 1805-March 3, 1807)
member of the GovernorÂ’s council 1809 and 1810
served in the State senate in 1811
presidential elector on the Clinton and Ingersoll ticket in 1812
appointed judge of the superior court of New Hampshire in 1813, which office he held until his death in Claremont, N.H., May 6, 1816
interment in Broad Street Cemetery.

ELLISON, Daniel, a Representative from Maryland
born in Russia, February 14, 1886
as an infant, was brought to the United States by his parents
attended the public schools of Baltimore, Md.
was graduated from Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, Md., in 1907 and from the law department of the University of Maryland at Baltimore in 1909
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice in Baltimore, Md.
served as a member of the Baltimore city council 1923-1942
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-eighth Congress (January 3, 1943-January 3, 1945)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1944 to the Seventy-ninth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Baltimore, Md.
member of the State senate 1946-1950
died in Baltimore, Md., August 20, 1960, interment in Hebrew Friendship Cemetery.

ENGLISH, William Hayden, (father of William Eastin English), a Representative from Indiana
born in Lexington, Scott County, Ind., August 27, 1822
pursued classical studies at Hanover (Ind.) College
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1846 and commenced practice at Lexington, Ind.
principal clerk of the State house of representatives in 1843
clerk in the United States Treasury Department at Washington, D.C., 1844-1848
secretary of the Indiana State constitutional convention in 1850
member of the State house of representatives in 1851 and 1852 and served as speaker
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1861)
chairman, Committee on Post Office and Post Roads (Thirty-fifth Congress)
Regent of the Smithsonian Institution 1853-1861
moved to Indianapolis, Ind., at the end of his congressional term
unsuccessful candidate for Vice President of the United States on the Democratic ticket in 1880
author of several books
died at his home in Indianapolis, Ind., February 7, 1896
interment in Crown Hill Cemetery.

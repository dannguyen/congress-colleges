EPPES, John Wayles, (son-in-law of Thomas Jefferson), a Representative and a Senator from Virginia
born at Eppington, Chesterfield County, Va., April 19, 1773
attended the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia
graduated from Hampden-Sydney College in Virginia in 1786
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1794 and commenced practice in Richmond, Va.
member, State house of delegates 1801-1803
elected as a Democratic Republican to the Eighth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1803-March 3, 1811)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Twelfth Congress
chairman, Committee on Ways and Means (Eleventh Congress)
engaged in agricultural pursuits
elected to the Thirteenth Congress (March 4, 1813-March 3, 1815)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Fourteenth Congress
chairman, Committee on Ways and Means (Thirteenth Congress)
elected to the United States Senate in 1815, but declined to accept the seat
again elected to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1817, until December 4, 1819, when he resigned because of ill health
chairman, Committee on Finance (Fifteenth Congress)
retired to his estate, Millbrooke, in Buckingham County, Va., where he died September 13, 1823
interment in the private cemetery of the Eppes family at Millbrook, near Curdsville, Va.

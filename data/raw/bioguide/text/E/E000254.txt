EVANS, Melvin Herbert, a Delegate from the Virgin Islands
born in Christiansted, St. Croix, V.I., August 7, 1917
attended the public schools
B.S., Howard University, Washington, D.C., 1940
M.D., Howard University College of Medicine, 1944
M.P.H., University of California, Berkeley, Calif., 1967
Virgin Islands Health Commissioner, 1959-1967
private practice of medicine, 1967-1969
appointed Governor of Virgin Islands, and served from 1969 until 1971
first elected Governor of Virgin Islands in 1970 and served from 1971 until 1975
Republican National Committeeman for United States, Virgin Islands, 1976-1980
delegate, Republican National Conventions, 1972 and 1976
elected as a Republican to the Ninety-sixth Congress (January 3, 1979-January 3, 1981)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1980 to the Ninety-seventh Congress
United States ambassador to Trinidad and Tobago, December 1, 1981, until his death
was a resident of Christiansted, St. Croix, V.I., until his death there on November 27, 1984
interment in Christiansted Cemetery.

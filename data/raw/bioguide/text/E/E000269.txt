EVERETT, William, (son of Edward Everett), a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Watertown, Middlesex County, Mass., October 10, 1839
attended the public schools of Cambridge and Boston
was graduated from Harvard University in 1859, from Trinity College, Cambridge University, England, in 1863, and from the law department of Harvard University in 1865
was admitted to the bar in 1866
studied for the ministry, and was licensed to preach in 1872 by the Suffolk Association of Unitarian Ministers
tutor in Harvard University 1870-1873
assistant professor of Latin 1873-1877
master of Adams Academy, Quincy, Mass., 1878-1893
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Henry Cabot Lodge and served from April 25, 1893, to March 3, 1895
was not a candidate for renomination in 1894
unsuccessful candidate for Governor of Massachusetts in 1897
master of school at Quincy, Mass., where he died February 16, 1910
interment in Mount Auburn Cemetery, Cambridge, Mass.

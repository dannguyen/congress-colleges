EVERHART, William, (father of James Bowen Everhart), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Chester County, Pa., May 17, 1785
attended the common schools and became a civil engineer
served in the War of 1812 as captain of a company of riflemen
was the only passenger saved from the packet ship Albion, wrecked off the coast of Ireland in 1822
upon his return to Pennsylvania he platted a large addition to the city of West Chester
was elected as a Whig to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1855)
was not a candidate for renomination
engaged in mercantile pursuits
died in West Chester, Pa., October 30, 1868
interment in Oakland Cemetery.

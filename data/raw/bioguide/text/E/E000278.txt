EWING, John Hoge, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Brownsville, Fayette County, Pa., October 5, 1796
attended the common schools and was graduated from Washington (now Washington and Jefferson) College, Washington, Pa., in 1814
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1818 and commenced practice in Washington, Pa.
engaged in agricultural pursuits
trustee of Washington College 1834-1887 and of Washington Female Seminary 1846-1887
member of the State house of representatives in 1835 and 1836
served in the State senate 1838-1842
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1845-March 3, 1847)
resumed agricultural pursuits
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1860
died in Washington, Pa., June 9, 1887
interment in Washington Cemetery.

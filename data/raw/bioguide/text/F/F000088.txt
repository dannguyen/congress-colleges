FERRARO, Geraldine Anne, a Representative from New York
born in Newburgh, Orange County, N.Y., August 26, 1935
graduated from Marymount School, Tarrytown, N.Y., 1952
B.A., Marymount College, New York, N.Y., 1956
J.D., Fordham University School of Law, New York, N.Y., 1960
lawyer, private practice
assistant district attorney, Queens County, N.Y., 1974-1978
elected as a Democrat to the Ninety-sixth and to the two succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1979-January 3, 1985)
was not a candidate for reelection to the Ninety-ninth Congress in 1984, but was the unsuccessful Democratic candidate for Vice President of the United States
fellow of the Institute of Politics, John F. Kennedy School of Government, Harvard University, Cambridge, Mass., 1988-1992
president, International Institute for WomenÂ’s Political Leadership
unsuccessful candidate for nomination to the United States Senate in 1992 and 1998
permanent member, United Nations Commission on Human Rights, 1993-1996
died on March 26, 2011, in Boston, Mass.

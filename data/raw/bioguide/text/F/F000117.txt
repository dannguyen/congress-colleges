FINCH, Isaac, a Representative from New York
born in Stillwater, Saratoga County, N.Y., October 13, 1783
moved with his parents to Peru, Clinton County, N.Y., in 1787
attended the public schools
studied law, but did not engage in extensive practice
settled near Jay, Essex County, N.Y., and became interested in agricultural pursuits
served as major in the Twenty-sixth Regiment of Infantry during the War of 1812
member of the State assembly 1822-1824
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1830
resumed agricultural pursuits
died in Jay, N.Y., June 23, 1845
interment in Central Cemetery.

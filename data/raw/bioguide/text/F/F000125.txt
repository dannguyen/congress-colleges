FINE, John, a Representative from New York
born in New York City August 26, 1794
received private instructions
was graduated from Columbia College at New York City in 1809
studied law in the Litchfield (Conn.) Law School
was admitted to the bar in 1815 and commenced practice in Ogdensburg, St. Lawrence County, N.Y.
treasurer of St. Lawrence County 1821-1833
judge of the court of common pleas for St. Lawrence County from 1824 until his resignation in March 1839
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1841)
again judge of the court of common pleas from February 16, 1843, until the court was abolished in 1847
unsuccessful candidate for judge of the State supreme court in 1847 and again in 1849
member of the State senate in 1848
resumed the practice of law
died in Ogdensburg, N.Y., January 4, 1867
interment in Ogdensburg Cemetery.

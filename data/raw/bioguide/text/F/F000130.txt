FINLEY, Charles, (son of Hugh Franklin Finley), a Representative from Kentucky
born in Williamsburg, Whitley County, Ky., March 26, 1865
attended the common and subscription schools, and Milligan College, Milligan, Tenn.
engaged in business as a coal operator, banker, and publisher
member of the State house of representatives 1894-1896
delegate to the Republican State convention in 1895
served as secretary of state of Kentucky 1896-1900
chairman of the Republican executive committee of the Eleventh Kentucky Congressional District 1912-1928
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-first Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of John M. Robsion
reelected to the Seventy-second Congress and served from February 15, 1930, to March 3, 1933
was not a candidate for renomination in 1932
retired from business activities
died in Williamsburg, Ky., March 18, 1941
interment in Highland Cemetery, Williamsburg, Ky.

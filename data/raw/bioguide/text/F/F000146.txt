FISHER, George, a Representative from New York
born in Franklin, Mass., March 17, 1788
attended the common schools and Brown University, Providence, R.I.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in Oswego County, N.Y., in 1816 and commenced practice in Oswego, N.Y.
appointed inspector of schools in 1818
trustee of the village of Oswego in 1828 and 1833
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian and presented credentials as a Member-elect to the Twenty-first Congress and served from March 4, 1829, to February 5, 1830, when the seat was awarded to Silas Wright, Jr., who contested the election
trustee of schools in 1830
continued the practice of law in Oswego, N.Y., until 1833
took his family to France, where he spent five years for the education of his children
returned to Oswego and engaged in real estate operations
served as president of the Northwestern Insurance Co. for several years
moved to New York City about 1856 and died there March 26, 1861.

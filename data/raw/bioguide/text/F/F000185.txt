FLANAGAN, De Witt Clinton, a Representative from New Jersey
born in New York City December 28, 1870
attended the Callison and Woodbridge private schools and Columbia College, New York City
pursued a commercial career, being interested in a number of industrial enterprises
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-seventh Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Joshua S. Salmon and served from June 18, 1902, to March 3, 1903
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1904
organized the Boston, Cape Cod & New York Canal Co., which built and operated the Cape Cod Canal
engaged in the agricultural and civic development of Baldwin County, Ala.
died in Utica, N.Y., January 15, 1946
interment in the family mausoleum, Woodlawn Cemetery, New York City.

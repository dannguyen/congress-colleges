FLANNERY, John Harold, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Pittston, Luzerne County, Pa., April 19, 1898
attended the public schools
was graduated from Wyoming Seminary, Kingston, Pa., in 1917 and from Dickinson School of Law, Carlisle, Pa., in 1920
during the First World War served as a private in the United States Army and was honorably discharged on December 14, 1918
was admitted to the bar in 1921 and commenced practice in Pittston, Pa.
solicitor for Pittston City, Pa., 1926-1930
served as assistant district attorney of Luzerne County, Pa., 1932-1936
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-fifth, Seventy-sixth, and Seventy-seventh Congresses and served from January 3, 1937, until his resignation on January 3, 1942, to become judge of the common pleas court of Luzerne County, Pa.
reelected in 1951 for a ten-year term and served until his death
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1944 and in 1960
died in Bethesda, Md., June 3, 1961
interment in Mount Olivet Catholic Cemetery, Pittston, Pa.

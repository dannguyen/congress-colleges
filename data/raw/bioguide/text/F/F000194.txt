FLEETWOOD, Frederick Gleed, a Representative from Vermont
born in St. Johnsbury, Caledonia County, Vt., September 27, 1868
attended the common schools of St. Johnsbury, and was graduated from St. Johnsbury Academy in 1886
also attended the University of Vermont at Burlington and was graduated from Harvard University in 1891
secretary of the commission on revision of Vermont statutes in 1893 and 1894
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Morrisville, Vt., in 1894
prosecuting attorney for Lamoille County 1896-1898
town clerk and treasurer of Morrisville, Vt., 1896-1900
member of the State house of representatives 1900-1902
secretary of state and insurance commissioner of Vermont 1902-1908
again secretary of state 1917-1919
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1923-March 3, 1925)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1924
resumed the practice of law
also engaged in banking
died in Morrisville, Vt., January 28, 1938
interment in Pleasant View Cemetery.

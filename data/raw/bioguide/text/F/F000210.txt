FLOOD, Henry De La Warr, (brother of Joel West Flood and uncle of Harry Flood Byrd), a Representative from Virginia
born in Eldon, Appomattox County, Va., September 2, 1865
attended the public schools of Appomattox and Richmond, Washington and Lee University, Lexington, Va., and the University of Virginia at Charlottesville
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1886 and commenced practice in Appomattox, Va.
member of the Virginia state house of delegates, 1887-1891
member of the Virginia state senate, 1891-1903
elected prosecuting attorney for Appomattox County, 1891, 1895, and 1899
unsuccessful candidate for election to the Fifty-fifth Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-seventh and to the ten succeeding Congresses and served until his death (March 4, 1901-December 8, 1921)
chair, Committee on Foreign Affairs (Sixty-second through Sixty-fifth Congresses), Committee on Territories (Sixty-second Congress)
author of the resolutions declaring a state of war to exist between the United States and the Imperial German Government and with the Imperial Austro-Hungarian Government
died on December 8, 1921, in Washington, D.C.
interment in a mausoleum on the courthouse green at Appomattox, Va.

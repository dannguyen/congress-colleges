FLOYD, John, a Representative from Georgia
born in Beaufort, Beaufort County, S.C., October 3, 1769
learned the carpenters trade
moved in 1791 with his father to Camden County, Ga., and engaged in boat building
served in the War of 1812 as brigadier general in the First (Floyds) Brigade of Georgia Militia from August 30, 1813, to March 8, 1814, and from October 17, 1814, to March 10, 1815, having participated in expeditions against the Creek Indians
member of the State house of representatives 1820-1827
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twentieth Congress (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1829)
died near Jefferson, Ga., June 24, 1839.

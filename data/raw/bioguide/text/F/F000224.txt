FLOYD, William, (grandfather of John Gelston Floyd), a Delegate and a Representative from New York
born in Brookhaven, Long Island, N.Y., December 17, 1734
pursued an academic course
served as major general in the State militia
Member of the Continental Congress 1774-1776 and 1779-1783
signed the Declaration of Independence
served in the State senate in 1777 and 1778
again served in the State senate 1784-1788
elected as an Anti-Administration candidate to the First Congress (March 4, 1789-March 3, 1791)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Second Congress in 1790
moved to Westernville, Oneida County in 1794
unsuccessful candidate for New York lieutenant governor in1795
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1801
again a member of the State senate in 1808
died in Westernville, N.Y., August 4, 1821
interment in Westernville Cemetery.

FLYNN, Joseph Vincent, a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y., September 2, 1883
attended the public schools and the BoysÂ’ High School of Brooklyn
was graduated from the College of the City of New York in 1904 and from the Brooklyn Law School of St. Lawrence University in 1906
was admitted to the bar in the latter year and commenced the practice of law in New York City
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-fourth and Sixty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1915-March 3, 1919)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1918
resumed the practice of law in New York City
delegate to the Democratic State conventions in 1925 and 1927
resided in Brooklyn, N.Y., until his death there February 6, 1940
interment in Calvary Cemetery, Long Island City, N.Y.

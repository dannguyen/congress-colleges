FOLGER, John Hamlin, (brother of Alonzo D. Folger), a Representative from North Carolina
born in Rockford, Surry County, N.C., December 18, 1880
attended the public schools, Guilford College, Greensboro, N.C., and studied law at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill
was admitted to the bar in 1901 and commenced practice in Dobson, Surry County, N.C.
mayor of Mount Airy, N.C., 1908-1912
member of the State house of representatives in 1927 and 1928
served in the State senate in 1931 and 1932
delegate to the Democratic State conventions 1924-1940
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1932 and 1944
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-seventh Congress in a special election to fill the vacancy caused by the death of his brother, Alonzo D. Folger
reelected to the Seventy-eighth, Seventy-ninth, and Eightieth Congresses and served from June 14, 1941, to January 3, 1949
was not a candidate for renomination in 1948
resumed the practice of law until his retirement in 1959
was a resident of Mount Airy, N.C.
died in Clemmons, N.C., July 19, 1963
interment in Oakdale Cemetery, Mount Airy, N.C.

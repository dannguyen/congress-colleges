FOLLETT, John Fassett, a Representative from Ohio
born near Enosburg, Franklin County, Vt., February 18, 1831
moved to Ohio in 1837 with his parents, who settled in Licking County
pursued classical studies, and was graduated from Marietta (Ohio) College in 1855
taught school two years
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1858 and practiced
member of the State house of representatives 1866-1868
served as speaker in 1868
moved to Cincinnati in 1868 and engaged in the practice of law
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1885)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1884 to the Forty-ninth Congress
resumed the practice of law
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1898 to the Fifty-sixth Congress
died in Cincinnati, Ohio, April 15, 1902
interment in Spring Grove Cemetery.

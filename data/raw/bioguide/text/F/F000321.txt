FOWLER, Hiram Robert, a Representative from Illinois
born near Eddyville, Pope County, Ill., February 7, 1851
attended the public schools of his native city, and was graduated from the Illinois Normal University at Normal in 1880
studied law at the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor and was graduated in 1884
was admitted to the bar in 1884 and commenced the practice of his profession in Elizabethtown, Ill.
StateÂ’s attorney of Hardin County 1888-1892
served in the State house of representatives 1893-1895
member of the State senate 1900-1904
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-second and Sixty-third Congresses (March 4, 1911-March 3, 1915)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914
resumed the practice of law in Elizabethtown, Ill.
moved to Harrisburg, Ill., in 1915 and continued practice until his death on January 5, 1926
interment in Sunset Hill Cemetery.

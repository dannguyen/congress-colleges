FREEMAN, James Crawford, a Representative from Georgia
born in Clinton (later Gray), Jones County, Ga., April 1, 1820
attended the common schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
moved to Griffin, Ga., in 1865 and continued in farming operations
engaged in mercantile pursuits and in banking
elected as a Republican to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1875)
moved to Atlanta, Ga., and again engaged in mercantile pursuits
died in Atlanta, Ga., September 3, 1885
interment in Oakland Cemetery.

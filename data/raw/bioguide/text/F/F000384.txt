FRIEDEL, Samuel Nathaniel, a Representative from Maryland
born in Washington, D.C., April 18, 1898
moved with his family to Baltimore, Md., when six months of age
attended the public schools and Strayer Business College
mailing clerk in a Baltimore store 1919-1923
founder and president of Industrial Loan Co., 1926-1956
member of the State house of delegates 1935-1939
member of the city council of Baltimore, 1939-1952, representing the first and later the fifth district
delegate, Democratic National Conventions, 1964 and 1968
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-third and to the eight succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1953-January 3, 1971)
chairman, Committee on House Administration (Ninetieth and Ninety-first Congresses), Joint Committee on the Library (Ninety-first Congress), Joint Committee on Printing (Ninety-first Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1970 to the Ninety-second Congress
died in Towson, Md., March 21, 1979
interment in the Hebrew Friendship Cemetery, Baltimore, Md.

FULLER, Thomas James Duncan, a Representative from Maine
born in Hardwick, Caledonia County, Vt., March 17, 1808
attended the common schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Calais, Maine
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-first and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1857)
chairman, Committee on Commerce (Thirty-third Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1856
appointed by President Buchanan as Second Auditor of the Treasury and served from April 15, 1857, to August 3, 1861
engaged in the practice of law before the United States Supreme Court and the Court of Claims in Washington, D.C.
died, while on a visit to his son, near Upperville, Fauquier County, Va., February 13, 1876
interment in Oak Hill Cemetery, Washington, D.C.

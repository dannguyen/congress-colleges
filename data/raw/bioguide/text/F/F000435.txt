FUSTER, Jaime B., a Resident Commissioner from Puerto Rico
born January 12, 1941, in Guayama, Puerto Rico
attended parochial schools
B.A., Notre Dame University, 1962
J.D., University of Puerto Rico Law School, 1965
LL.M., Columbia University Law School, 1966
Law and Humanities Fellow, Harvard University, 1973-1974
professor of law, 1966-1979, and dean of law, 1974-1978, University of Puerto Rico
United States Deputy Assistant Attorney General, 1980-1981
president, Catholic University of Puerto Rico, 1981-1984
elected as a Popular Democrat to the Ninety-Ninth Congress for a four-year term and reelected to the succeeding term until his resignation on March 4, 1992 (January 3, 1985-March 4, 1992)
associate justice, Supreme Court of Puerto Rico
died on December 3, 2007, in Guaynabo, P.R.

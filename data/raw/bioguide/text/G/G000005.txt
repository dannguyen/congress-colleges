GAILLARD, John, (uncle of Theodore Gaillard Hunt), a Senator from South Carolina
born in St. Stephens District, S.C., September 5, 1765
educated for the legal profession in England
member, State house of representatives 1794-1796
member, State senate 1796-1804, serving as president 1803-1804
elected as a Democratic Republican (later Crawford Republican) to the United States Senate in 1804 to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Pierce Butler
reelected in 1806, 1812, 1818, and 1824, and served from December 6, 1804, until his death on February 26, 1826
served as President pro tempore of the Senate during the Eleventh and Thirteenth through Nineteenth Congresses
died in Washington, D.C.
interment in the Congressional Cemetery.

GALLIVAN, James Ambrose, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Boston, Mass., October 22, 1866
attended the public schools
was graduated from the Boston Latin School in 1884 and from Harvard University in 1888
engaged in newspaper work in 1888
member of the State house of representatives in 1895 and 1896
served in the State senate in 1897 and 1898
street commissioner of Boston 1900-1914
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of James M. Curley
reelected to the Sixty-fourth and to the six succeeding Congresses and served from April 7, 1914, until his death in Arlington, Mass., April 3, 1928

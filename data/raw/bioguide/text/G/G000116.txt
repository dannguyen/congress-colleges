GEDDES, James, a Representative from New York
born near Carlisle, Pa., July 22, 1763
attended the public schools
moved to Onondaga County, N.Y., in 1794, and began the manufacture of salt at Liverpool, N.Y.
justice of the peace in 1800
member of the State assembly in 1804
associate justice of the county court in 1809
judge of the court of common pleas in 1809
elected as a Federalist to the Thirteenth Congress (March 4, 1813-March 3, 1815)
again a member of the State assembly in 1822
appointed chief engineer of the Ohio Canal in 1822
engineer on the Chesapeake & Ohio Canal in 1827
died in Geddes, N.Y., August 19, 1838
interment in Oakwood Cemetery, Syracuse, N.Y.

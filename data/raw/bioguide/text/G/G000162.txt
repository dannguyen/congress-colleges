GIBSON, James King, a Representative from Virginia
born in Abingdon, Washington County, Va., February 18, 1812
attended the common schools
moved to Huntsville, Limestone County, Ala., in 1833
returned to Abingdon, Va., the following year and engaged in mercantile pursuits
deputy sheriff of Washington County in 1834 and 1835
appointed postmaster of Abingdon on December 19, 1837, and served until July 26, 1849, when a successor was appointed
upon the readmission of the State of Virginia to representation was elected as a Conservative to the Forty-first Congress and served from January 28, 1870, to March 3, 1871
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1870
engaged in agricultural pursuits and banking
died in Abingdon, Va., March 30, 1879
interment in Sinking Spring Cemetery.

GILMAN, Charles Jervis, (grandnephew of John Taylor Gilman and Nicholas Gilman), a Representative from Maine
born in Exeter, Rockingham County, N.H., February 26, 1824
attended Phillips Exeter Academy, Exeter, N.H., and pursued classical studies
was graduated from Harvard Law School
was admitted to the bar in 1850 and commenced practice in Exeter, N.H.
member of the house of representatives of New Hampshire in 1851 and 1852
moved to Brunswick, Maine, and continued the practice of law
member of the house of representatives of Maine in 1854 and 1855
member of the State Whig committee
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1858
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1860
interested in introducing waterworks and other public improvements
died in Brunswick, Maine, on February 5, 1901
interment in Pine Grove Cemetery.

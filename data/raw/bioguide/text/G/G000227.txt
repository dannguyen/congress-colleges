GIST, Joseph, a Representative from South Carolina
born near the mouth of Fair Forest Creek, Union District, S.C., January 12, 1775
moved to Charleston with his parents in 1788
attended the common schools
was graduated from the College of Charleston
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1799 and began practice in Pinckneyville, S.C., in 1800
member of the State house of representatives, 1802-1817
member of the board of trustees of South Carolina College at Columbia 1809-1821
elected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress, reelected as a Jackson Republican to the Eighteenth Congress, and elected as a Jacksonian to the Nineteenth Congress (March 4, 1821-March 3, 1827)
was not a candidate for renomination
resumed the practice of law
died in Pinckneyville, S.C., on March 8, 1836
interment in the family burial ground.

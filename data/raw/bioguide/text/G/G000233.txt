GLASS, Presley Thornton, a Representative from Tennessee
born in Houston, Halifax County, Va., October 18, 1824
in 1828 moved with his parents to Weakley County, Tenn., where he attended Dresden Academy
elected colonel of militia when eighteen years of age
studied law
attended one course at Lexington (Ky.) Law School
was admitted to the bar in 1847 and commenced practice in Ripley, Tenn.
member of the State house of representatives in 1848 and again in 1882
during the Civil War served as commissary with the rank of major in the Confederate service
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth and Fiftieth Congresses (March 4, 1885-March 3, 1889)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1888
died in Ripley, Tenn., on October 9, 1902
interment in Maplewood Cemetery.

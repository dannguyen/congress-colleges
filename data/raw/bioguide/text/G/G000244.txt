GLOVER, John Milton, (nephew of John Montgomery Glover), a Representative from Missouri
born in St. Louis, Mo., June 23, 1852
attended the public schools of his native city and Washington University, St. Louis, Mo.
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in St. Louis
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth and Fiftieth Congresses (March 4, 1885-March 3, 1889)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1888, having become a candidate for the Democratic gubernatorial nomination, in which he was unsuccessful
reengaged in the practice of law in St. Louis, Mo., until 1909, when he moved to Denver, Colo., and continued the practice of his profession until incapacitated by ill health in 1926
died in Pueblo, Colo., October 20, 1929
interment in Bellefontaine Cemetery, St. Louis, Mo.

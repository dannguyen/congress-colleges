GOODHUE, Benjamin, a Representative and a Senator from Massachusetts
born in Salem, Mass., September 20, 1748
graduated from Harvard College in 1766
merchant
member, State house of representatives 1780-1782
member, State senate 1783, 1786-1788
member of the State constitutional convention in 1779 and 1780
elected to the First and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1789, until his resignation in June 1796
chairman, Committee on Commerce and Manufactures (Fourth Congress)
elected in 1796 as a Federalist to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of George Cabot
reelected and served from June 11, 1796, to November 8, 1800, when he resigned
died in Salem, Mass., on July 28, 1814
interment in Broad Street Cemetery.

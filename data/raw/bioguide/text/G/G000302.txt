GOODWIN, Philip Arnold, a Representative from New York
born in Athens, Greene County, N.Y., January 20, 1882
attended the public schools
moved to Coxsackie, N.Y., with his parents in 1896
was graduated from the high school at Coxsackie, N.Y., in 1900, and from Albany (N.Y.) Business College in 1902
engaged in the steel bridge construction business at Albany, N.Y., 1902-1916
owner and operator of a lumber business at Coxsackie, N.Y., from 1916 until his death
also interested in banking, in a milling and supply company, and in a securities company
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-third, Seventy-fourth, and Seventy-fifth Congresses and served from March 4, 1933, until his death in Coxsackie, N.Y., June 6, 1937
interment in Riverside Cemetery.

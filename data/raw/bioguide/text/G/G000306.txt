GOODWYN, Peterson, a Representative from Virginia
born at Martins, near Petersburg, Dinwiddie County, Va., in 1745
received his education from private tutors
completed preparatory studies
engaged in planting
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1776 and commenced practice in Petersburg, Va., and surrounding counties
during the Revolutionary War equipped his own company and rose from captain to major
was promoted to colonel for gallantry at the battles of Smithfield and Great Bridge
member of the State house of delegates 1789-1802
elected as a Republican to the Eighth and to the seven succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1803, until his death at his home, Sweden, in Dinwiddie County, Va., February 21, 1818
interment in the family burying ground on his estate.

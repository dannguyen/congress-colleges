GORE, Christopher, a Senator from Massachusetts
born in Boston, Mass., September 21, 1758
graduated from Harvard College in 1776
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Boston
member of the State constitutional convention in 1788
member, State house of representatives 1788-1789, 1808
United States attorney for the district of Massachusetts 1789-1796
commissioner to England 1796-1803
Charge dÂ’Affaires at London 1803-1804
member, State senate 1806-1807
Governor of Massachusetts 1809
appointed and subsequently elected to the United States Senate as a Federalist to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of James Lloyd and served from May 5, 1813, until May 30, 1816, when he resigned
overseer of Harvard University 1810-1815 and a fellow 1812-1820
died in Waltham, Mass., March 1, 1827
interment in Granary Burying Ground, Boston, Mass.

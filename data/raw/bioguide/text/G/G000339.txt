GOTT, Daniel, a Representative from New York
born in Hebron, near New London, Conn., July 10, 1794
attended the public schools
at the age of sixteen taught school
moved to Pompey, N.Y., in 1817
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1819 and commenced practice in Pompey, N.Y.
elected as a Whig to the Thirtieth and Thirty-first Congresses (March 4, 1847-March 3, 1851)
moved to Syracuse, N.Y., in 1853 and resumed the practice of his profession
died in Syracuse, N.Y., July 6, 1864
interment in Pompey Hill Cemetery, Pompey, N.Y.

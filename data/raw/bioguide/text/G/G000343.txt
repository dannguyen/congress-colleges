GOULD, Samuel Wadsworth, a Representative from Maine
born in Porter, Oxford County, Maine, January 1, 1852
moved with his parents to Hiram, Maine
attended the public schools and North Parsonsfield Seminary
was graduated from the University of Maine at Orono in 1877
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Skowhegan, Maine in 1879
postmaster of Skowhegan 1896-1900
attended all Democratic State conventions for more than forty years
secretary of the Democratic State committee 1882-1890
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1900, 1908, and 1912
unsuccessful candidate for Governor of Maine in 1902 and for election to the Sixty-first Congress in 1908
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-second Congress (March 4, 1911-March 3, 1913)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1912
resumed the practice of law in Skowhegan, Maine
interested in various business enterprises
president of the board of trustees of the University of Maine
died in Skowhegan, Maine, December 19, 1935
interment in Southside Cemetery.

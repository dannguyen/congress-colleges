GRAY, Hiram, a Representative from New York
born in Salem, Washington County, N.Y., July 10, 1801
attended Salem Academy
was graduated from Union College in 1821
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1823 and practiced in Elmira, N.Y., 1825-1828
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1837-March 3, 1839)
appointed by Gov. Silas Wright circuit judge and vice chancellor of the sixth judicial district of New York in 1846
elected justice of the supreme court of New York in 1847
reelected in 1851 and served until 1860
commissioner of appeals 1870-1875
resumed the practice of law
died in Elmira, N.Y., May 6, 1890
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

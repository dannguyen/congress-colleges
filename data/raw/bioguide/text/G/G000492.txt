GROOME, James Black, a Senator from Maryland
born in Elkton, Cecil County, Md., April 4, 1838
completed preparatory studies in the Tennent School, Hartsville, Pa.
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1861 and commenced practice in Elkton, Md.
member of the Maryland constitutional convention 1867
elected to the State house of delegates in 1871, 1872, and 1873
elected Governor in 1874 to fill a vacancy and served from 1874 to 1876
resumed the practice of law
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1879, to March 3, 1885
collector of customs for the port of Baltimore 1889-1893
died in Baltimore, Md., October 5, 1893
interment in Elkton Presbyterian Cemetery, Elkton, Md.

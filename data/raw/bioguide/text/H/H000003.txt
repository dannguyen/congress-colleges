HABERSHAM, Richard Wylly, (nephew of John Habersham and Joseph Habersham), a Representative from Georgia
born in Savannah, Ga., in December 1786
attended private schools, and was graduated from Princeton College in 1810
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Savannah, Ga.
appointed United States attorney and served until 1825, when he resigned
attorney general of Georgia
moved to Clarksville, Habersham County, in 1835
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses and served from March 4, 1839, until his death
died in Clarksville, Ga., December 2, 1842
interment in the Old Cemetery.

HAGAN, George Elliott, a Representative from Georgia
born in Sylvania, Screven County, Ga., May 24, 1916
attended the Screven County public schools and the University of Georgia
served five terms in the State house of representatives and one term in the State senate
at the outbreak of the Second World War resigned from the State house of representatives and served two years in the Army Signal Corps
secretary-treasurer and deputy director of the State Board of WorkmenÂ’s Compensation, 1946
member of National Council of State Governments for two terms
district director of Office of Price Stabilization for southern half of Georgia in 1951 and 1952 and deputy regional director, Atlanta Regional Office, in 1953
engaged in life insurance-estate planning, general farming and livestock raising
member of the board of trustees of Tift College
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-seventh and to the five succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1961-January 3, 1973)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1972 to the Ninety-third Congress
died on December 26, 1990, in Sylvania, Ga.
interment in Screven County Memorial Cemetery, Sylvania, Ga.

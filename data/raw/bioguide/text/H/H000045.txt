HALL, Bolling, (uncle of Dixon Hall Lewis), a Representative from Georgia
born in Dinwiddie County, Va., December 25, 1767
pursued classical studies
served in the Revolutionary War at the age of 16
moved to Hancock County, Ga., in 1792
held several local offices
member of the State house of representatives 1800-1802 and 1804-1806
elected as a Republican to the Twelfth, Thirteenth, and Fourteenth Congresses (March 4, 1811-March 3, 1817)
retired to private life
moved to Alabama in 1808 and engaged in planting near Montgomery
chairman of the reception committee to welcome General Lafayette in 1824
died on his plantation, Ellerslie, in Autauga (now Elmore) County, Ala., February 25, 1836
interment on his estate.

HALL, George, a Representative from New York
born in Cheshire, New Haven County, Conn., May 12, 1770
attended the common schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Onondaga County, N.Y.
moved to Onondaga, N.Y., in 1802 and continued the practice of law
postmaster of Onondaga Hollow in 1802
surrogate of Onondaga County 1800-1822
supervisor in 1811 and 1812
justice of the peace 1818-1822
member of the State assembly in 1816 and 1817
elected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1821)
unsuccessful candidate in 1820 to the Seventeenth Congress
resumed the practice of law
died in Onondaga Valley, N.Y., March 20, 1840
interment in Onondaga Valley Cemetery.

HALL, Leonard Wood, a Representative from New York
born in Oyster Bay, Nassau County, N.Y., October 2, 1900
attended the public schools
was graduated from the law department of Georgetown University, Washington, D.C., in 1920
was admitted to the bar in 1922 and commenced practice in New York City
member of the State assembly in 1927 and 1928 and 1934-1938
sheriff of Nassau County, N.Y., 1929-1931
delegate to the Republican State conventions 1930-1958 and to the Republican National Conventions in 1948, 1952, 1956, and 1968
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth Congress
reelected to the six succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1939, until December 31, 1952
did not seek reelection in 1952 to the Eighty-third Congress
elected surrogate of Nassau County, N.Y., in November 1952, resigning to become chairman of the Republican National Committee, serving from 1953 to 1957
President Eisenhowers personal representative at opening of the Brussels Worlds Fair in April 1958
resumed the practice of law in Garden City, N.Y., and New York City
resided in Locust Valley, N.Y.
died in Glen Cove, N.Y., June 2, 1979
interment in Memorial Cemetery of St. Johns Church, Laurel Hollow, Long Island, N.Y.

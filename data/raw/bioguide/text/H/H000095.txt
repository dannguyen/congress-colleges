HAMBLETON, Samuel, a Representative from Maryland
born at Waterloo farm, Talbot County, Md., January 8, 1812
educated by private tutors and attended Easton Academy
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1833 and commenced practice in Easton, Talbot County, Md.
member of the State house of delegates in 1834 and 1835
States attorney for Talbot County 1836-1844
served in the State senate 1844-1850
president of the Chesapeake & Ohio Canal in 1853 and 1854
again a member of the State house of delegates in 1853
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-first and Forty-second Congresses (March 4, 1869-March 3, 1873)
died in Easton, Md., December 9, 1886
interment in Spring Hill Cemetery.

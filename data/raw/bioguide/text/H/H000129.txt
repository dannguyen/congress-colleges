HAMMOND, John, a Representative from New York
born at Crown Point, Essex County, N.Y., August 17, 1827
attended the public schools and St. Albans Academy, St. Albans, Vt.
was graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, N.Y.
pioneer in California in 1849
volunteered as a private in the Civil War
promoted to captain of Cavalry and advanced to brigadier general
a manufacturer of iron for twenty-five years
president of the Crown Point Iron Co.
elected as a Republican to the Forty-sixth and Forty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1879-March 3, 1883)
was not a candidate for renomination
retired from business
died at Crown Point, N.Y., May 28, 1889
interment in Forest Dale Cemetery.

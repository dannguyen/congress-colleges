HAMMOND, Nathaniel Job, a Representative from Georgia
born in Elbert County, Ga., December 26, 1833
attended the common schools and was graduated from the University of Georgia at Athens in 1852
studied law, was admitted to the bar in 1853 and commenced practice in Atlanta, Ga.
solicitor general of the Atlanta circuit 1861-1865
reporter of the supreme court 1867-1872
attorney general 1872-1877
member of the State constitutional conventions in 1865 and 1877
elected a trustee of the University of Georgia in 1872
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1879-March 3, 1887)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1886
resumed the practice of law in Atlanta, Ga., and died there April 20, 1899
interment in Oakland Cemetery.

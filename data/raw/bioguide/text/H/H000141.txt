HAMPTON, Wade, (grandson of Wade Hampton [1752-1835]), a Senator from South Carolina
born in Charleston, S.C., March 28, 1818
received private instruction, graduated from the South Carolina College (now the University of South Carolina) at Columbia in 1836
studied law but never practiced
planter
member, State house of representatives 1852-1856
member, State senate 1858-1861
served in the Confederate Army during the Civil War, raising and commanding Hamptons Legion
three times wounded
made brigadier general in 1862, major general in 1863, and lieutenant general in 1865
Governor of South Carolina 1876-1879
elected in 1878 as a Democrat to the United States Senate
reelected in 1884 and served from March 4, 1879, until March 3, 1891
unsuccessful candidate for reelection
United States railroad commissioner 1893-1897
died in Columbia, S.C., April 11, 1902
interment in Trinity Cathedral Churchyard.

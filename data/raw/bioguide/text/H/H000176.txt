HANSON, Alexander Contee, (grandson of John Hanson), a Representative and a Senator from Maryland
born in Annapolis, Md., February 27, 1786
attended local private schools and graduated from St. Johns College, Annapolis, in 1802
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Annapolis, Md.
member, State house of delegates 1811-1815
established and edited the Federal Republican, an extreme Federalist newspaper, in Baltimore, and on June 22, 1812, four days after war was declared, a mob, irritated by his articles denouncing the administration, destroyed the office
when he issued the paper from another building in late July, he was seriously injured by a mob
moved the paper to Georgetown, D.C., where he published it unmolested
moved to Rockville, Md.
elected as a Federalist to the Thirteenth and Fourteenth Congresses and served from March 4, 1813, until his resignation in 1816
unsuccessful candidate in 1816 for election to the State house of delegates
elected as a Federalist to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Robert G. Harper and served from December 20, 1816, until his death on his estate, Belmont, near Elkridge, Howard County, Md., April 23, 1819
interment in the family burial ground.

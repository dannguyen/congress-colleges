HARALSON, Hugh Anderson, a Representative from Georgia
born near Penfield, Greene County, Ga., November 13, 1805
attended the common schools and was also instructed by private tutors
was graduated from Franklin College (now the University of Georgia) in 1825
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1825 and commenced practice in Monroe, Walton County, Ga.
moved to Lagrange, Troup County, Ga., in 1828, and continued the practice of law
also engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives in 1831 and 1832
served in the State senate in 1837 and 1838
served in the State militia as a major general 1838-1850
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-eighth, Twenty-ninth, Thirtieth, and Thirty-first Congresses (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1851)
chairman, Committee on Military Affairs (Twenty-eighth and Twenty-ninth Congresses)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1850
resumed the practice of law
died in Lagrange, Troup County, Ga., September 25, 1854
interment in Hill View Cemetery.

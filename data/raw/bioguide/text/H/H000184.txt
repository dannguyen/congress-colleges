HARDIN, Benjamin, (cousin of Martin Davis Hardin), a Representative from Kentucky
born at the Georges Creek settlement on the Monongahela River, Westmoreland County, Pa., February 29, 1784
moved with his parents to Washington County, Ky., in 1788
attended the schools of Nelson and Washington Counties, Ky.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1806 and commenced practice in Elizabethtown and Bardstown, Nelson County, Ky.
settled in Bardstown in 1808
member of the State house of representatives in 1810, 1811, 1824, and 1825
served in the State senate 1828-1832
elected as a Republican to the Fourteenth Congress (March 4, 1815-March 3, 1817)
reelected as a Republican to the Sixteenth and Seventeenth Congresses (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1823)
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
secretary of state of Kentucky 1844-1847
member of the State constitutional convention in 1849
died in Bardstown, Ky., September 24, 1852
interment in the family burying ground near Springfield, Ky.

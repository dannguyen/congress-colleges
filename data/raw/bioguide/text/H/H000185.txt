HARDIN, John J., (son of Martin Davis Hardin), a Representative from Illinois
born in Frankfort, Ky., January 6, 1810
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Transylvania University, Lexington, Ky.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in Kentucky in 1831 and commenced practice in Jacksonville, Morgan County, Ill.
served in the Illinois Militia during the Black Hawk War 1831-1832
was brigadier general in command during the Mormon trouble in Hancock County in 1844 and later attained the rank of major general
appointed prosecuting attorney of Morgan County in 1832
member of the State house of representatives 1836-1842
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1845)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1844
during the Mexican War recruited the First Regiment, Illinois Volunteer Infantry, of which he was commissioned colonel
was killed at the Battle of Buena Vista, Mexico, February 23, 1847
interment in City Cemetery (East), Jacksonville, Ill.

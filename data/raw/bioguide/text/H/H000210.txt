HARLAN, James, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Mercer County, Ky., June 22, 1800
attended the public schools
engaged in mercantile pursuits from 1817 to 1821
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1823 and commenced practice in Harrodsburg, Ky.
prosecuting attorney 1829-1844
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress and reelected as a Whig to the Twenty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1839)
secretary of state of Kentucky 1840-1844
member of the State house of representatives in 1845
attorney general of Kentucky from 1850 until his death in Frankfort, Franklin County, Ky., February 18, 1863.

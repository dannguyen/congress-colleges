HARPER, James Clarence, a Representative from North Carolina
born in Cumberland County, Pa., December 6, 1819
moved with his father to Darke County, Ohio, in 1831
attended the common schools
moved to Lenoir, Caldwell County, N.C., in 1840
land surveyor, civil engineer, and draftsman
laid out the town of Lenoir, N.C., in 1841
engaged in mercantile pursuits and subsequently became interested in the manufacture of cotton and woolen goods
held several local offices
colonel in the State militia
member of the State house of commons in 1865 and 1866
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-second Congress (March 4, 1871-March 3, 1873)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1872
engaged in agricultural pursuits and in road building
died near Patterson, Caldwell County, N.C., January 8, 1890
interment in the Cemetery at Harpers Chapel, Patterson, N.C.

HARRISON, Carter Henry, a Representative from Illinois
born near Lexington, Fayette County, Ky., February 15, 1825
educated by private tutors
was graduated from Yale College in 1845
traveled and studied in Europe 1851-1853
was graduated from the law department of Transylvania College, Lexington, Ky., in 1855
was admitted to the bar in 1855 and commenced practice in Chicago, Ill.
also engaged in the real estate business
unsuccessful candidate in 1872 for election to the Forty-third Congress
member of the board of commissioners of Cook County 1874-1876
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth and Forty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1879)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1878
mayor of Chicago 1879-1887 and declined a renomination
unsuccessful candidate for Governor of Illinois in 1884
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1880 and 1884
owner and editor of the Chicago Times 1891-1893
again elected mayor of Chicago in 1893 and served until his death in Chicago by assassination October 28, 1893
interment in Graceland Cemetery, Chicago, Ill.

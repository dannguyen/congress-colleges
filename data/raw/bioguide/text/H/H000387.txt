HAYDEN, Moses, a Representative from New York
born near Westfield, Hampden County, Mass., in 1786
completed preparatory studies and was graduated from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass., in 1804
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in York, Livingston County, N.Y.
was first judge of the court of common pleas of Livingston County 1821-1823
elected as an Adams-Clay Republican to the Eighteenth Congress and reelected as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth Congress (March 4, 1823-March 3, 1827)
member of the State senate from January 6, 1829, until his death in Albany, N.Y., February 13, 1830
interment in Mount Pleasant Cemetery, York, near Fowlerville, N.Y.

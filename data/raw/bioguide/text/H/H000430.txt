HEATH, James P., a Representative from Maryland
born in Delaware, December 21, 1777
completed preparatory studies
served in the Regular Army as lieutenant of Engineers 1799-1802
register in chancery in Annapolis, Md.
served throughout the War of 1812 as aide-de-camp to General Winder
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third Congress (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1835)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1834 to the Twenty-fourth Congress
died in Georgetown, D.C., June 12, 1854
interment in Oak Hill Cemetery, Washington, D.C.

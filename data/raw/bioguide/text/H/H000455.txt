HEINTZ, Victor, a Representative from Ohio
born on a farm near Grayville, White County, Ill., November 20, 1876
attended the public schools
was graduated from the University of Cincinnati in 1896 and from its law department in 1899
was admitted to the bar in 1898 and commenced practice in Cincinnati, Ohio
served six years in the Cavalry and Infantry of the Ohio National Guard
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1917-March 3, 1919)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1918
during the First World War absented himself from the House and was commissioned a captain in the One Hundred and Forty-seventh Regiment, United States Infantry, on August 4, 1917
went overseas June 22, 1918, and served until the end of the war
decorated with the Distinguished Service Cross with Oak Leaf Cluster, Silver Star Medal, Purple Heart, and the Croix de Guerre
vice president and secretary of Ohio Valley Real Estate Co.
resumed the practice of law until his retirement in 1961
died in Cincinnati, Ohio, December 27, 1968
interment in Armstrong Chapel Cemetery, Indian Hill, Cincinnati, Ohio.

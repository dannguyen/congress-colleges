HENDRICK, John Kerr, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Caswell County, N.C., October 10, 1849
moved with his parents to Logan County and later to Todd County, Ky., attended private schools and Bethel College, Russellville, Ky., moved to Crittenden County, Ky., in 1869 and engaged in teaching school
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1874 and commenced practice in Smithland, Ky.
prosecuting attorney of Livingston County, 1878-1886
member of the State Senate, 1887-1891
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1888
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1897)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1896
resumed the practice of law in Paducah, Ky., where he died June 20, 1921
interment in Maplelawn Cemetery.

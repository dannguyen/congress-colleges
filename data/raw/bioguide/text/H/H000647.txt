HITCHCOCK, Peter, a Representative from Ohio
born in Cheshire, Conn., October 19, 1781
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Yale College in 1801
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1804 and commenced practice in Cheshire
moved to Geauga County, Ohio, in 1806
member of the State house of representatives in 1810
member of the State senate 1812-1815 and served as speaker in 1815
commissioned lieutenant colonel of the Fourth Regiment, Ohio State Militia, in 1814
commissioned major general, Fourth Division, Ohio State Militia, in 1816
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress (March 4, 1817-March 3, 1819)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1818
judge of the supreme court of Ohio 1819-1832 and served a portion of that time as chief justice
again a member of the State senate in 1833 and 1834
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1850
died in Painesville, Lake County, Ohio, March 4, 1853
interment in Welton Cemetery, Burton, Ohio.

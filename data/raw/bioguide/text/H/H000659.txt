HOBART, Aaron, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Abington, Mass., June 26, 1787
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Brown University, Providence, R.I., in 1805
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1809 and commenced practice in Abington
moved to Hanover in 1811
member of the State house of representatives in 1814 and served in the State senate in 1819
moved to East Bridgewater in 1824
elected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Zabdiel Sampson
reelected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress, elected as an Adams-Clay Republican to the Eighteenth Congress, and reelected as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth Congress, and served from November 24, 1820, to March 3, 1827
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1826
executive councilor 1827-1831
judge of probate 1843-1858
died in East Bridgewater, Mass., September 19, 1858
interment in Central Cemetery.

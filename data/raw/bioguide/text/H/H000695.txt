HOGE, John, (brother of William Hoge), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Hogestown, Pa., September 10, 1760
pursued English studies
served in the Revolutionary War as ensign in the Ninth Pennsylvania Regiment
moved to what is now Washington, Pa., in 1782, which he and his brother William founded
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1790
member of the State senate 1790-1795
elected as a Republican to the Eighth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of his brother, William Hoge, and served from November 2, 1804, to March 3, 1805
died at Meadow Lands, near Washington, Pa., August 4, 1824
interment in the City Cemetery, Washington, Pa.

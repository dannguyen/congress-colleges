HOGE, Joseph Pendleton, a Representative from Illinois
born in Steubenville, Ohio, December 15, 1810
attended the common schools and was graduated from Jefferson College
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1836
moved to Illinois and located in Galena in 1836 and practiced law
held several local offices
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-eighth and Twenty-ninth Congresses (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1847)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1846
resumed the practice of law in Galena
moved to California in 1853 and continued the practice of his profession
unsuccessful candidate for election to the United States Senate in 1869
president of the State constitutional convention in 1878 and of the board of freeholders in 1880
judge of the superior court from January 1, 1889, until his death in San Francisco, Calif., August 14, 1891
interment in Laurel Hill Cemetery.

HOGG, Samuel, a Representative from Tennessee
born in Halifax, N.C., April 18, 1783
attended the public schools in Caswell County
taught school for a short time
studied medicine in Gallatin, Sumner County, Tenn., about 1804
moved to Lebanon County, Tenn., after a short time
surgeon in the First Regiment of Tennessee Volunteer Infantry from November 21, 1812, to April 22, 1813
hospital surgeon on the staff of Maj. Gen. Andrew Jackson in the expedition against the Creek Indians from February 22 to May 25, 1814
also hospital surgeon on the staff of Maj. Gen. William Carroll from November 13, 1814, to May 13, 1815
member of the State house of representatives
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress (March 4, 1817-March 3, 1819)
engaged in the practice of medicine in Lebanon, Tenn., until 1828, in Nashville 1828-1836 and 1838-1840, and in Natchez 1836-1838
president of the State Medical Society of Tennessee in 1840
died in Rutherford County, Tenn., May 28, 1842
interment in Nashville City Cemetery.

HOLBROOK, Edward Dexter, a Delegate from Idaho
born in Elyria, Lorain County, Ohio, May 6, 1836
attended the common schools and Oberlin (Ohio) College
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1859 and commenced practice in Elyria
Ohio, Weaverville, Calif., and Placerville, Idaho
lawyer, private practice
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-ninth and succeeding Congress (March 4, 1865-March 3, 1869)
censured by the House of Representatives on February 4, 1869, for use of unparliamentary language
was not a candidate for reelection
shot by Charles H. Douglas in Idaho City, Idaho, on June 17, 1870, and died from his wounds in that city the next day
interment in the Masonic Burial Ground.

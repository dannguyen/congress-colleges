HOLLADAY, Alexander Richmond, a Representative from Virginia
born in Prospect Hill, Spotsylvania County, Va., September 18, 1811
attended the public schools, received special training under John Lewis of Spotsylvania County, and attended the University of Virginia at Charlottesville
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Spotsylvania, Orange, and Louisa Counties
member of the State house of delegates 1845-1847
held several local offices
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1853)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of the Navy (Thirty-first Congress)
declined to be a candidate for renomination
moved to Richmond, Va., in 1853 and practiced law
president of the Virginia Board of Public Works 1857-1861
died in Richmond, Va., January 29, 1877
interment in family burial ground called Prospect Hill in Spotsylvania County, Va.

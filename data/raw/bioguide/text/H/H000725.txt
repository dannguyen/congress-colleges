HOLLINGS, Ernest Frederick, a Senator from South Carolina
born in Charleston, Charleston County, S.C., January 1, 1922
attended the public schools of Charleston
graduated, The Citadel 1942 and University of South Carolina Law School 1947
admitted to the bar in 1947 and commenced law practice in Charleston
served in the United States Army 1942-1945
elected to the South Carolina general assembly in 1948, 1950, and 1952
speaker pro tempore, South Carolina house of representatives
elected lieutenant governor of South Carolina 1954
elected governor of South Carolina 1958, serving from 1959 to 1963
presidential appointee to several federal commissions
elected in a special election on November 8, 1966, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to complete the unexpired term of Olin D. Johnston
reelected in 1968, 1974, 1980, 1986, 1992 and 1998 and served from November 9, 1966, to January 3, 2005
chair, Committee on the Budget (Ninety-sixth Congress), Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation (One Hundredth through One Hundred Third Congresses
One Hundred Seventh Congress [January 3-20, 2001
June 6, 2001-January 3, 2003])
unsuccessful candidate for the Democratic nomination for President of the United States in 1984
was not a candidate for reelection to the Senate in 2004.

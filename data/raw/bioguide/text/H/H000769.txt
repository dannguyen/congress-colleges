HOPKINS, Albert Cole, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Villanovia, near Jamestown, Chautauqua County, N.Y., September 15, 1837
attended the public schools
was graduated from Alfred University, Alfred, N.Y.
taught school
engaged in mercantile pursuits in Troy, Pa., where he remained until 1867
moved to Lock Haven, Clinton County, Pa., and engaged in the lumber business
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-second and Fifty-third Congresses (March 4, 1891-March 3, 1895)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1894
resumed lumber manufacturing pursuits
State forestry commissioner 1899-1904
died in Lock Haven, Pa., June 9, 1911
interment in Highland Cemetery.

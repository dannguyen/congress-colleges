HOPKINS, Benjamin Franklin, a Representative from Wisconsin
born in Hebron, N.Y., April 22, 1829
attended the common schools and became a telegraph operator
moved to Madison, Wis., in 1849
private secretary to Governor Bashfourd in 1856 and 1857
served in the State senate in 1862 and 1863
member of the State assembly in 1866
elected as a Republican to the Fortieth and Forty-first Congresses and served from March 4, 1867, until his death in Madison, Dane County, Wis., January 1, 1870
chairman, Committee on Public Buildings and Grounds (Forty-first Congress)
interment in Forest Hill Cemetery.

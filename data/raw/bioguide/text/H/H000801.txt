HOSKINS, George Gilbert, a Representative from New York
born in Bennington, N.Y., December 24, 1824
completed preparatory studies
engaged in mercantile pursuits
for a number of years town clerk of Bennington and justice of the peace
postmaster of Bennington, N.Y., 1849-1853 and 1861-1866
member of the State assembly in 1860, 1865, and 1866, and served as speaker in 1865
removed to Attica, N.Y., in 1867
commissioner of public accounts 1868-1870
appointed collector of internal revenue for the twenty-ninth district of New York May 1, 1871, and served until March 4, 1873
elected as a Republican to the Forty-third and Forty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1877)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1876 to the Forty-fifth Congress
Lieutenant Governor of New York 1880-1883
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1880
died in Attica, N.Y., June 12, 1893
interment in Forest Hill Cemetery.

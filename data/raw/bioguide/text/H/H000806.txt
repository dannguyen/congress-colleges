HOSTETTER, Jacob, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near York, Pa., May 9, 1754
attended the common schools
was a clockmaker
member of the general assembly of Pennsylvania 1797-1802
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Jacob Spangler
reelected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress and served from November 16, 1818, until March 3, 1821
moved to Ohio and settled in Columbiana, where he died June 29, 1831.

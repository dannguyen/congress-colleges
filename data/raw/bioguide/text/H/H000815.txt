HOUGHTON, Sherman Otis, a Representative from California
born in New York City on April 10, 1828
completed preparatory studies and attended Collegiate Institute, New York
during the Mexican War enlisted in the First Regiment, New York Volunteers, in June 1846
honorably discharged at Monterey, Calif., in October 1848
proceeded to the gold mines and thence to San Jose
deputy clerk of the State supreme court in 1854
mayor of San Jose, Calif., in 1855 and 1856
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1857 and commenced practice in San Jose
during the Civil War was commissioned captain and promoted to lieutenant colonel, and served successively as inspector and ordnance officer
elected as a Republican to the Forty-second and Forty-third Congresses (March 4, 1871-March 3, 1875)
chairman, Committee on Coinage, Weights, and Measures (Forty-third Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Forty-fourth Congress
appointed commissioner to investigate the affairs of the United States Mint at San Francisco in 1881
moved to Los Angeles in 1886 and continued the practice of law
died in Compton, Los Angeles County, Calif., August 31, 1914
interment in Rosedale Cemetery, Los Angeles, Calif.

HOWE, John W., a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Maine in 1801
studied law
was admitted to the bar
moved to Smethport, Pa., and then to Franklin, Pa., in 1829 and commenced the practice of law
justice of the peace
elected as a Free Soil candidate to the Thirty-first Congress and reelected as a Whig to the Thirty-second Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1853)
moved to Meadville, Pa., and later to Rochester, N.Y., where he died December 1, 1873
interment in Greendale Cemetery, Meadville, Crawford County, Pa.

HUBBS, Orlando, a Representative from North Carolina
born in Commack, Suffolk County, N.Y., February 18, 1840
attended the district schools and the local academy at Commack
went to Northport in 1856 and learned the trade of a carriage and wagon builder and subsequently became employed as a shipÂ’s joiner at Hunters Point, N.Y.
moved to New Bern, N.C., in 1865 and became engaged in mercantile pursuits
took an active part in organizing the Republican Party in North Carolina
sheriff of Craven County 1871-1881
elected as a Republican to the Forty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1881-March 3, 1883)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1882
returned to New York in 1890 and settled in Central Islip
engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the New York assembly 1902-1908
served in the State senate in 1910 and 1911
resided in Smithtown Branch, Suffolk County, N.Y., until his death on December 5, 1930
interment in Commack Cemetery, Commack, N.Y.

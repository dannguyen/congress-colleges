HULL, Merlin, a Representative from Wisconsin
born in Warsaw, Kosciusko County, Ind., December 18, 1870
attended Gale College, Galesville, Wis., De Pauw University, Greencastle, Ind., and Columbian (now George Washington) University, Washington, D.C.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1894 and commenced practice in Black River Falls, Wis.
publisher of the Jackson County Journal 1904-1926 and of the Banner-Journal 1926-1953
also engaged in agricultural pursuits
district attorney of Jackson County 1907-1909
member of the Wisconsin assembly 1909-1915, serving as speaker in 1913
secretary of state of Wisconsin 1917-1921
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-first Congress (March 4, 1929-March 3, 1931)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1930 and unsuccessful Independent candidate for reelection to the Seventy-second Congress
resumed former business pursuits
elected as a Progressive to the Seventy-fourth and to the five succeeding Congresses, and as a Republican to the Eightieth and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1935, until his death in La Crosse, Wis., May 17, 1953
interment in Oak Grove Cemetery.

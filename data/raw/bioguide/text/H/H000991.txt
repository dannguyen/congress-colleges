HUNTER, William, a Senator from Rhode Island
born in Newport, R.I., November 26, 1774
attended Rogers School in Newport and graduated from Rhode Island College (later Brown University), Providence, R.I., in 1791
went abroad to study medicine, but preferred to study law at the Inner Temple, London
returned to Newport, R.I., in 1793
admitted to the bar in 1795 and commenced practice in Newport
member of the State general assembly 1799-1812
elected as a Federalist to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Christopher G. Champlin
reelected in 1814 and served from October 28, 1811, to March 3, 1821
chairman, Committee on Commerce and Manufactures (Fourteenth Congress)
member, State house of representatives 1823-1825
resumed the practice of law in Newport
appointed by President Andrew Jackson Charge dÂ’Affaires to Brazil 1834, elevated to Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary and served until 1845
died in Newport, R.I., December 3, 1849
interment in Trinity Church Graveyard.

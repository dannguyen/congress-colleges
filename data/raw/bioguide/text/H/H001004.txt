HURLEY, Denis Michael, a Representative from New York
born in the city of Limerick, Ireland, March 14, 1843
immigrated to the United States in 1850 with his parents, who settled in Brooklyn, N.Y.
moved to New York City in 1854
was educated in the public schools
returned to Brooklyn in 1866
learned the carpenters trade
engaged in the building contractors business
delegate to the Republican State conventions from 1879 to 1899
unsuccessful Republican candidate for member of the State assembly in 1880
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth and Fifty-fifth Congresses and served from March 4, 1895, until his death
unsuccessful in 1898 for reelection to the Fifty-sixth Congress
died at Hot Springs, Va., February 26, 1899
interment in Holy Cross Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

HYNES, William Joseph, a Representative from Arkansas
born in County Clare, Ireland, March 31, 1843
immigrated to the United States in 1854 and settled in New York
attended the public schools of Massachusetts
learned the art of printing
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1870 and commenced practice in Little Rock, Ark.
elected as a Liberal Republican to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1875)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Forty-fourth Congress
moved to Chicago in 1876 and resumed the practice of his profession
retired from the practice of law in 1910 and moved to Los Angeles, Calif., where he remained until his death, April 2, 1915
interment in Calvary vault.H

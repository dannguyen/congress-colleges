IHRIE, Peter, Jr., a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Easton, Pa., February 3, 1796
completed preparatory studies
was graduated from Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa., in 1815
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1818 and commenced practice in Easton, Pa.
charter member of the board of trustees of Lafayette College in 1826
member of the State house of representatives in 1826 and 1827
brigadier general of State militia in 1845
elected as a Jacksonian candidate to the Twenty-first Congress to fill in part the vacancies caused by the resignation of George Wolf and Samuel D. Ingraham
reelected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-second Congress and served from October 13, 1829, to March 3, 1833
member of the board of directors of the Easton Bank
died in Easton, Pa., on March 29, 1871
interment in Easton Cemetery.

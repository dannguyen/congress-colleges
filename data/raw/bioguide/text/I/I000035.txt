IRVINE, William, a Delegate and a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in County Fermanagh, Ulster, Ireland, on November 3, 1741
pursued classical studies and was graduated from the Dublin University
studied medicine and was admitted to practice
served as surgeon on a British man-of-war
immigrated to the United States and settled in Carlisle, Pa., in 1763
delegate to the State Revolutionary conventions 1764-1766
colonel of the Sixth Pennsylvania Regiment in the Revolutionary Army
captured in Canada June 16, 1776, and remained a prisoner of war until exchanged May 6, 1778
appointed brigadier general May 12, 1779, and served until the close of the war
Member of the Continental Congress 1787-1788
commanded the State troops during the Whisky Rebellion in 1794
elected as an Anti-Administration candidate to the Third Congress (March 4, 1793-March 3, 1795)
moved to Philadelphia, where he was superintendent of military stores 1801-1804
died in Philadelphia, Pa., July 29, 1804
interment in Gloria Dei (Old Swedes) Church Burial Ground, Philadelphia, Pa.

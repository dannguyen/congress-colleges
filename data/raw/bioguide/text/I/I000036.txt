IRVINE, William, a Representative from New York
born in Whitneys Point, Broome County, N.Y., February 14, 1820
attended the common schools
moved to Greene County, N.Y., in 1841
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1849 and commenced practice in Corning, Steuben County, N.Y.
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1859-March 3, 1861)
during the Civil War assisted in raising the Tenth Regiment, New York Volunteer Cavalry, of which he became lieutenant colonel November 25, 1861
brevetted colonel and brigadier general of Volunteers March 13, 1865
adjutant general on the staff of Governor Fenton in 1865 and 1866
moved to California and continued the practice of his profession until his death in San Francisco, Calif., November 12, 1882
interment in the Elmira Cemetery, Elmira, N.Y.

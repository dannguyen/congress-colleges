IRWIN, Thomas, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., February 22, 1785
attended the common schools and Franklin College, Lancaster, Pa.
became editor of the Philadelphia Repository in 1804
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1808 and commenced practice in Uniontown, Pa.
appointed Indian agent at Natchitoches, La., where he also engaged in the practice of law for two years
returned to Uniontown, Pa., in 1811 and resumed the practice of law
member of the State house of representatives 1824-1828
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1830
appointed a United States judge for the western district of Pennsylvania April 14, 1831, and served until February 8, 1859, when a successor was appointed
lived in retirement until his death in Pittsburgh, Pa., May 14, 1870
interment in Allegheny Cemetery.

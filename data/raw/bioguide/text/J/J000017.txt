JACKSON, James, (father of Jabez Y. Jackson and grandfather of James Jackson [1819-1887]), a Representative and a Senator from Georgia
born in Moreton-Hampstead, Devonshire, England, September 21, 1757
emigrated to Georgia in 1772 and located in Savannah
served in the Revolution with the Georgia State forces
studied law and built a lucrative practice in Savannah
several times elected to the state legislature
elected governor of Georgia in 1788 but declined
planter
elected to the First Congress (March 4, 1789-March 3, 1791)
contested the election of Anthony Wayne in the Second Congress and the seat was declared vacant by the House of Representatives March 21, 1792
elected to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1793, until his resignation in 1795
again a member of the State legislature
Governor of Georgia 1798-1801
was again elected as a Democratic Republican to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1801, until his death in Washington, D.C., March 19, 1806
interment in the Congressional Cemetery.

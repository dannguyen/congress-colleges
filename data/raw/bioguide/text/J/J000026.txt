JACKSON, Samuel Dillon, a Senator from Indiana
born near Zanesville, Allen County, Ind., May 28, 1895
attended the public schools of Fort Wayne, Ind.
graduated from the Indiana University Law School at Indianapolis in 1917, and admitted to the bar the same year
during the First World War, served as a captain of Infantry 1917-1919
engaged in the practice of law at Fort Wayne, Ind., in 1919
prosecuting attorney of Allen County, Ind., 1924-1928
unsuccessful Democratic candidate for election in 1928 to the Seventy-first Congress
attorney general of Indiana 1940-1941
appointed on January 28, 1944, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Frederick Van Nuys and served from January 28, 1944, to November 13, 1944, when a duly elected successor qualified
was not a candidate for election to fill the vacancy
unsuccessful Democratic candidate for Governor of Indiana in 1944
resumed the practice of law
died in Fort Wayne, Ind., March 8, 1951
interment in Lindenwood Cemetery.

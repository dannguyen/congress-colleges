JARNAGIN, Spencer, a Senator from Tennessee
born in Grainger County, Tenn., in 1792
pursued classical studies and graduated from Greenville College in 1813
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1817 and commenced practice in Knoxville
member, State senate 1833-1835
trustee of the East Tennessee College 1836-1851
moved to Athens, Tenn., in 1837 and continued the practice of his profession
presidential elector on the Whig ticket in 1840
Whig nominee for United States Senator in 1841 but the general assembly adjourned without electing a Senator
elected as a Whig to the United States Senate and served from October 17, 1843, to March 3, 1847
chairman, Committee on Revolutionary Claims (Twenty-eighth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Senate and for member of the supreme court of Tennessee
moved to Memphis and continued the practice of law
died in Memphis, Tenn., June 25, 1853
interment in Elmwood Cemetery.

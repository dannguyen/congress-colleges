JENISON, Edward Halsey, a Representative from Illinois
born in Fond du Lac, Wis., July 27, 1907
attended the public schools and the University of Wisconsin at Madison
engaged in newspaper work 1925-1937, and as a publisher since 1938 of the Paris, Ill., Daily Beacon-News
served as lieutenant commander in the United States Navy, attached to the Deputy Chief of Naval Operations for Air, with service in the Pacific and Atlantic Forces, from April 1943 to September 1946
elected as a Republican to the Eightieth, Eighty-first, and Eighty-second Congresses (January 3, 1947-January 3, 1953)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1952 to the Eighty-third Congress, for election in 1954 to the Eighty-fourth Congress, and for election in 1962 to the Eighty-eighth Congress
resumed the publishing business
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1956 and 1968
director, Department of Finance, State of Illinois, from June 15, 1960, to January 20, 1961
member, house of representatives, Illinois General Assembly, one term, 1965-1966
delegate, Illinois Sixth Constitutional Convention, 1969-1970
died June 24, 1996.

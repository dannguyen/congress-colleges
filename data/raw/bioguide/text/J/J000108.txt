JOELSON, Charles Samuel, a Representative from New Jersey
born in Paterson, Passaic County, N.J., January 27, 1916
attended the public schools, graduated from Montclair Academy
Cornell University, B.A., 1937
law school of Cornell University, LL.B., 1939
was admitted to the bar in 1940 and commenced the practice of law in Paterson, N.J.
enlisted in the United States Navy in 1942 and served in the Far Eastern Branch of the Division of Naval Intelligence
city counsel of Paterson, N.J., 1949-1952
deputy attorney general of the States criminal investigation division, 1954-1956, and Passaic County Prosecutors Office, 1956-1958
director of criminal investigation for the State of New Jersey, 1958-1960
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-seventh and to the four succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1961, until his resignation September 4, 1969, to become a judge of the Superior Court of New Jersey
was a resident of Paramus, N.J., until his death on August 17, 1999.

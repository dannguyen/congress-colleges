JOHNS, Kensey, Jr., a Representative from Delaware
born in New Castle, New Castle County, Del., December 10, 1791
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Princeton College in 1810
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1813 and commenced practice in New Castle
elected as an Adams to the Twentieth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Louis McLane
reelected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress and served from October 2, 1827, to March 3, 1831
was not a candidate for renomination in 1830 to the Twenty-second Congress
appointed chancellor of Delaware in 1832 and served until his death in New Castle, Del., on March 28, 1857
interment in the Presbyterian Cemetery.

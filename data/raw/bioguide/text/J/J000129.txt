JOHNSON, Francis, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Caroline County, Va., June 19, 1776
pursued preparatory studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
moved to Woodford County, Ky., in 1796 and to Bowling Green in 1807
member of the State house of representatives in 1812, 1813, and 1815
elected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of David Walker
reelected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress
elected as an Adams-Clay Republican to the Eighteenth Congress and as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth Congress
and served from November 13, 1820, to March 3, 1827
chairman, Committee on the Post Office and Post Roads (Seventeenth and Eighteenth Congresses)
moved to Louisville, Ky., in 1829 and resumed the practice of law
served as Commonwealth attorney for the fifth district
unsuccessful Republican candidate for Governor
died in Louisville, Ky., May 16, 1842
interment in the old family burial ground, later a municipal playground.

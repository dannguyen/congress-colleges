JOHNSON, Philip, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Polkville, Knowlton Township, Warren County, N.J., January 17, 1818
moved to Mount Bethel, Pa., in 1839
attended the common schools and Lafayette College, Easton, Pa., 1842-1844
was a plantation tutor in Mississippi 1844-1846
returned to Pennsylvania
studied law
attended Union Law School in Easton, Pa.
was admitted to the bar in 1848 and commenced practice in Easton
county court clerk 1848-1853
member of the State house of representatives in 1853 and 1854
revenue commissioner of the third judicial district in 1859 and 1860
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-seventh, Thirty-eighth, and Thirty-ninth Congresses and served from March 4, 1861, until his death in Washington, D.C., January 29, 1867
interment in Easton Cemetery, Easton, Pa.

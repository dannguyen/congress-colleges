JOHNSON, Timothy Peter (Tim), a Senator and a Representative from South Dakota
born in Canton, S.Dak., December 28, 1946
attended public schools
B.A., University of South Dakota 1969
M.A., 1970
post-graduate studies, Michigan State University 1970-1971
J.D., University of South Dakota 1975
budget advisor, Michigan Senate 1971-1972
began the practice of law in Vermillion, S.D., in 1975
member, South Dakota House of Representatives 1979-1982
member South Dakota Senate 1983-1986
Clay County deputy StateÂ’s attorney 1985
elected as a Democrat to the One Hundredth and to the four succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1987-January 2, 1997)
not a candidate for reelection to the House of Representatives in 1996
elected to the U.S. Senate in 1996
reelected in 2002, and again in 2008, and served from January 3, 1997, to January 3, 2015
chair, Committee on Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs (One Hundred Twelfth and One Hundred Thirteenth Congresses)
was not a candidate for reelection in 2014.

JOHNSON, Tom Loftin, a Representative from Ohio
born in Georgetown, Scott County, Ky., July 18, 1854
moved to Indiana in boyhood
attended the public schools
employed in a rolling mill
clerk in a street-railway office in Louisville, Ky., 1869-1875
later became secretary of the company
invented several street-railway devices
purchased a street railway in Indianapolis, Ind.
later acquired large street-railway interests in Cleveland, Detroit, and Brooklyn
settled in Cleveland, Ohio
became interested in rolling mills and iron manufacturing
unsuccessful Democratic candidate for election in 1888 to the Fifty-first Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-second and Fifty-third Congresses (March 4, 1891-March 3, 1895)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1894 to the Fifty-fourth Congress
mayor of Cleveland, Ohio, 1901-1909
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1909
unsuccessful Democratic candidate for Governor of Ohio in 1903
died in Cleveland, Ohio, April 10, 1911
interment in Green-Wood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

JOHNSTON, Rienzi Melville, (cousin of Benjamin Edward Russell), a Senator from Texas
born in Sandersville, Washington County, Ga., September 9, 1849
attended the public schools
during the Civil War served in the Confederate Army
moved to Austin, Tex., in 1878 and engaged in journalism
moved to Houston in 1883 and established the Houston Post
member of the Democratic National Committee 1900-1912
appointed on January 4, 1913, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Joseph W. Bailey and served from January 4, to January 29, 1913, when a successor was elected and qualified
resumed his former activities as editor and president of the Houston Post
elected to the State senate in 1916
relinquished the active management of his newspaper business in 1919 and lived in retirement until his death in Houston, Tex., February 28, 1926
interment in Glenwood Cemetery.

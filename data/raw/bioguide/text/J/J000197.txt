JOHNSTON, Rowland Louis, a Representative from Missouri
born in Louisiana, Pike County, Mo., April 23, 1872
attended the public schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1894 and commenced practice in St. Louis, Mo.
member of the State house of representatives 1892-1896
served as prosecuting attorney of St. Louis County 1904-1908
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1908
assistant circuit attorney for the city of St. Louis 1920-1926
member of the State militia
during the Spanish-American War served as a recruiting officer
moved to Rolla, Mo., in 1926 and continued the practice of law
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-first Congress (March 4, 1929-March 3, 1931)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1930 to the Seventy-second Congress and for election in 1932 to the Seventy-third Congress
resumed the practice of law in Rolla, Mo., until his death there on September 22, 1939
remains were cremated and the ashes deposited in the mausoleum at Oak Grove Cemetery, St. Louis, Mo.

JUDSON, Andrew Thompson, a Representative from Connecticut
born in Eastford, Windham County, Conn., November 29, 1784
received a limited schooling
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1806
moved to Montpelier, Vt., where he began the practice of law
returned to Connecticut and settled in Canterbury in 1809
StateÂ’s attorney for Windham County 1819-1833
member of the State house of representatives 1822-1825
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress and served from March 4, 1835, until July 4, 1836, when he resigned
appointed by President Jackson United States judge for the district of Connecticut June 28, 1836, and served until his death in Canterbury, Conn., March 17, 1853
interment in Hyde Cemetery.

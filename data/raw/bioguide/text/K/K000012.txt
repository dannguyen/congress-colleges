KARST, Raymond Willard, a Representative from Missouri
born in South St. Louis, Mo., December 31, 1902
attended Wyman grade school and St. Louis Academy
was graduated from the law school of St. Louis University in 1927
was admitted to the bar in 1926 and commenced the practice of law in St. Louis, Mo.
member of the State house of representatives in 1935 and 1936
provisional city judge and judge of Court of Criminal Correction 1936-1940
served as a captain, Ordnance Department, United States Army, 1942-1945
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-first Congress (January 3, 1949-January 3, 1951)
was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1950 to the Eighty-second Congress
appointed general counsel with Economic Stabilization Agency and later acting administrator
in 1955 resumed the practice of law in Clayton, Mo.
chairman of the board, Karst Enterprises
was a resident of Kirkwood, Mo., until his death there October 4, 1987.

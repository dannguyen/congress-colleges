KEMP, Bolivar Edwards, a Representative from Louisiana
born on the Kemp homestead near Amite, St. Helena Parish, La., December 28, 1871
was privately tutored and also attended the public schools of Amite, La., and the University of Louisiana at Baton Rouge
was graduated in law from Tulane University at New Orleans in 1897
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice at Amite, La.
was active in the development of agricultural and trucking industries and also interested in banking
member of the board of supervisors of the University of Louisiana since 1910
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-ninth and to the four succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1925, until his death in Amite, La., on June 19, 1933
chairman, Committee on Territories (Seventy-third Congress)
interment in Amite Cemetery.

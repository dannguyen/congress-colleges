KERR, James, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Reedsville, Mifflin County, Pa., October 2, 1851
resided in Blair County until 1864
moved to Clearfield in 1867
pursued an academic course
justice of the peace, 1878
prothonotary for Clearfield County, Pa., 1880–1883
engaged in the coal and lumber business
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-first Congress (March 4, 1889–March 3, 1891)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination to the Fifty-Second Congress in 1890
elected Clerk of the United States House of Representatives for the Fifty-second Congress and for the succeeding Congress (December 8, 1891–December 2, 1895)
resumed business interests
died on October 31, 1908, in New York City, N.Y.
interment in Hillcrest Cemetery, Clearfield, Pa.

KEY, Philip, (cousin of Philip Barton Key and great-grandfather of Barnes Compton), a Representative from Maryland
born probably on his fathers estate near Leonardtown, St. Marys County, Md. in 1750
pursued an academic course in England
returned to Maryland and engaged in farming
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
served in the Maryland house of delegates in 1773
member of the committee of correspondence, St. Marys County, in 1774
again a member of the house of delegates 1779-1790
elected as a Pro-Administration candidate to the Second Congress (March 4, 1791-March 3, 1793)
member of the State house of delegates in 1795 and 1796 and served as speaker
died in St. Marys County, Md., January 4, 1820
interment probably in the churchyard at Chaptico, Md.

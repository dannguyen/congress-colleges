KILGORE, Constantine Buckley, a Representative from Texas
born in Newnan, Coweta County, Ga., February 20, 1835
moved with his parents to Rusk County, Tex., in 1846
received a common-school and academic training
studied law
during the Civil War entered the Confederate Army as a private and by 1862 had attained the rank of adjutant general of EctorÂ’s brigade, Army of the Tennessee
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Rusk County, Tex.
elected justice of the peace in 1869
member of the State constitutional convention in 1875
elected to the State senate in 1884 for a term of four years
was chosen president of that body in 1885 for two years
resigned from the State senate in 1886, having been elected to Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Fiftieth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1895)
appointed by President Cleveland United States judge for the southern district of Indian Territory March 20, 1895, and served until his death in Ardmore, Indian Territory (now Oklahoma), September 23, 1897
interment in White Rose Cemetery, Wills Point, Tex.

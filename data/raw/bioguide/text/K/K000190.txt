KINDNESS, Thomas Norman, a Representative from Ohio
born in Knoxville, Knox County, Tenn., August 26, 1929
graduated from Glendale High School, Glendale, Calif., 1947
A.B., University of Maryland, College Park, Md., 1951
LL.B., George Washington University, Washington, D.C., 1953
lawyer, private practice
assistant counsel Champion International Corp., 1957-1973
mayor of Hamilton, Ohio, 1964-1967
member, Hamilton, Ohio, City Council, 1964-1969
member of the Ohio state house of representatives, 1971-1974
delegate, Ohio State Republican conventions, 1971-1974
elected as a Republican to the Ninety-fourth and to the five succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1975-January 3, 1987)
one of the managers appointed by the House of Representatives in 1986 to conduct the impeachment proceedings against Harry E. Claiborne, judge of the United States District Court for Nevada
was not a candidate for reelection in 1986, but was an unsuccessful candidate for the United States Senate
unsuccessful candidate for nomination to the One Hundred Second Congress in 1990
died on January 8, 2004, in Exeter, England.

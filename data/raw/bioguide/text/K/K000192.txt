KING, Adam, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in York, Pa., January 20, 1783
pursued academic studies
studied medicine in the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia and commenced practice in York
edited and published the York Gazette 1818-1835
clerk of the courts of York County 1818-1826
elected to the Twentieth Congress and reelected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first and Twenty-second Congresses (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1833)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1832 to the Twenty-third Congress
resumed the practice of medicine
died in York, York County, Pa., May 6, 1835
interment in Prospect Hill Cemetery.

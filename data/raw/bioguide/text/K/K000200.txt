KING, Edward John, a Representative from Illinois
born in Springfield, Mass., July 1, 1867
moved to Illinois with his parents, who settled in Galesburg, Knox County, in 1880
attended the public schools, and Knox College at Galesburg, Ill.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1893 and commenced practice in Galesburg, Ill.
city attorney in 1893 and 1894
member of the State house of representatives 1907-1914
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fourth and to the six succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1915, until his death
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of Agriculture (Sixty-seventh through Sixty-ninth Congresses)
had been reelected to the Seventy-first Congress
died in Washington, D.C., February 17, 1929
interment in Hope Abbey Mausoleum, Hope Cemetery, Galesburg, Ill.

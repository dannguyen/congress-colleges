KINKAID, Moses Pierce, a Representative from Nebraska
born near Morgantown, Monongalia County, W.Va., January 24, 1856
attended the public schools
was graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor in 1876
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Henry County, Ill., from 1876 until 1880 and in Pierre, S.Dak., in 1880 and 1881
moved to OÂ’Neill, Nebr., and continued the practice of law
member of the State senate in 1883
district judge 1887-1900
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1902 to the Fifty-seventh Congress
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-eighth and to the nine succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1903, until his death in Washington, D.C., July 6, 1922
chairman, Committee on Irrigation of Arid Lands (Sixty-sixth and Sixty-seventh Congresses)
interment in Prospect Hill Cemetery, O'Neill, Holt County, Nebr.

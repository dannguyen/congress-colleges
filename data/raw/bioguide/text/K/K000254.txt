KITTERA, Thomas, (son of John Wilkes Kittera), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Lancaster, Pa., March 21, 1789
was graduated from the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia in 1805
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1808 and commenced practice in Philadelphia
deputy attorney general of Pennsylvania in 1817 and 1818
deputy attorney general of Philadelphia 1824-1826
member of the select council and its president 1824-1825
elected as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Joseph Hemphill and served from October 10, 1826, to March 3, 1827
at the same election was an unsuccessful candidate for election to the Twentieth Congress
died in Philadelphia, Pa., on June 16, 1839
interment in St. Paul's Protestant Episcopal Church Cemetery.

KLINE, Isaac Clinton, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Mount Pleasant, Westmoreland County, Pa., August 18, 1858
attended the public schools, the State normal school, Bloomsburg, Pa., and Bucknell Academy, Lewisburg, Pa.
was graduated from Lafayette College, Easton, Pa., in 1893
taught school five years before entering college
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1894 and commenced practice in Sunbury
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1912 to the Sixty-third Congress
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1921-March 3, 1923)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1922 to the Sixty-eighth Congress
resumed the practice of his profession in Sunbury, Pa.
died in De Land, Fla., December 2, 1947
interment in Pomfret Manor, Sunbury, Pa.

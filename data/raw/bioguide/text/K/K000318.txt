KORNEGAY, Horace Robinson, a Representative from North Carolina
born in Asheville, Buncombe County, N.C., March 12, 1924
educated in the public schools of Greensboro, N.C.
attended Georgia School of Technology and graduated from Wake Forest College (N.C.), B.S., 1947, LL.B., 1949
was admitted to the bar and entered the practice of law in Greensboro, N.C., in 1949
served in the United States Army, One Hundredth Infantry, from December 14, 1942, to February 1, 1946, with service in the European Theater
assistant district solicitor, 1951-1953
elected district solicitor (prosecuting attorney), for the twelfth district of North Carolina in 1954 and again in 1958
delegate to 1964 Democratic National Convention
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-seventh and to the three succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1961-January 3, 1969)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1968 to the Ninety-first Congress
vice president and counsel, January 1969-June 1970, president, June 1970-February 1981, and chairman, February 1982-December 1986, the Tobacco Institute, Inc.
resumed the practice of law in Greensboro, N.C., in January 1987
died on January 21, 2009, in Greensboro, N.C.

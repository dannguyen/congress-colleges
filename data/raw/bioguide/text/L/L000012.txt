LADD, Edwin Fremont, a Senator from North Dakota
born in Starks, Somerset County, Maine, December 13, 1859
attended the public schools and Somerset Academy, Athens, Maine, and graduated from the University of Maine at Orono in 1884
chemist of the New York State Experiment Station, Geneva, N.Y., 1884-1890
dean of the school of chemistry and pharmacy and professor of chemistry at the North Dakota Agricultural College, Fargo, N.Dak.
chief chemist of the North Dakota Agricultural Experiment Station 1890-1916
editor of the North Dakota Farmer at Lisbon 1899-1904
administrator of the State pure-food laws, for which he actively crusaded 1902-1921
president of the North Dakota Agricultural College 1916-1921
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate in 1920 and served from March 4, 1921, until his death in Johns Hopkins Hospital, Baltimore, Md., June 22, 1925
chairman, Committee on Public Roads and Surveys (Sixty-eighth Congress)
interment in Glenwood Cemetery, Washington, D.C.

LAMAR, Henry Graybill, a Representative from Georgia
born in Clinton, Jones County, Ga., July 10, 1798
pursued an academic course
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Macon, Ga.
judge of the State superior court
member of the State house of representatives
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of George R. Gilmer
reelected to the Twenty-second Congress and served from December 7, 1829, to March 3, 1833
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1832 to the Twenty-third Congress
unsuccessful candidate for Governor in 1857
associate justice of the State supreme court
died in Macon, Ga., September 10, 1861
interment in Rose Hill Cemetery.

LAW, Lyman, (son of Richard Law and father of John Law), a Representative from Connecticut
born in New London, Conn., August 19, 1770
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Yale College in 1791
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1793 and commenced practice in New London
member of the State house of representatives in 1801, 1802, 1806, 1809, 1810, 1819, and 1826, and served as speaker in 1806, 1809, and 1810
elected as a Federalist to the Twelfth, Thirteenth, and Fourteenth Congresses (March 4, 1811-March 3, 1817)
resumed the practice of his profession
died in New London, Conn., February 3, 1842
interment in the Second Burial Ground
reinterment in Cedar Grove Cemetery in 1851.

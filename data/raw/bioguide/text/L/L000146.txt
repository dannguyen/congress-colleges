LAWSON, Thomas Graves, a Representative from Georgia
born near Eatonton, Putnam County, Ga., on May 2, 1835
attended private schools and was graduated from Mercer University, Macon, Ga., in 1855
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1857 and commenced practice in Eatonton, Ga.
during the Civil War served two years in the Confederate Army
member of the State house of representatives 1861-1866, 1889, and 1890
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1877
member of the board of trustees of Mercer University and the Eatonton Male and Female Academy
judge of the superior courts of Ocmulgee circuit 1879-1887
engaged in agricultural pursuits near Eatonton, Ga., 1888-1891
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-second, Fifty-third, and Fifty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1891-March 3, 1897)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1896
resumed agricultural pursuits in Putnam County, Ga.
died in Eatonton, Ga., April 16, 1912
interment in Pine Grove Cemetery.

LAY, George Washington, a Representative from New York
born in Catskill, N.Y., July 26, 1798
pursued classical studies and was graduated from Hamilton College at Clinton, N.Y., in 1817
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Batavia, N.Y., in 1820
treasurer of Genesee County, N.Y., 1825-1831
elected as an Anti-Masonic candidate to the Twenty-third Congress and reelected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
member of the state assembly in 1840
Chargé dAffaires to Sweden from May 12, 1842, to October 29, 1845
died in Batavia, Genesee County, N.Y., October 21, 1860
interment in Batavia Cemetery.

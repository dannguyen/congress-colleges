LEIPER, George Gray, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., February 3, 1786
attended the common schools
was graduated from the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia in 1803
moved to Lapidea, Delaware County, Pa., in 1810 and engaged in logging
also operated bark mills and stone quarries
served as first lieutenant of the Delaware County Fencibles in 1814 and was called into active service near Brandywine Creek
member of the State house of representatives in 1822 and 1823
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of the Treasury (Twenty-first Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1830
resumed the management of his quarry properties
appointed associate judge of the courts of Delaware County on February 25, 1843
reappointed on February 16, 1848, and served until December 1, 1851, when the office became elective
died at his home, Lapidea, on Crum Creek, Delaware County, Pa., November 18, 1868
interment in the Ridley Presbyterian Church Cemetery, Ridley Township, Delaware County, Pa.

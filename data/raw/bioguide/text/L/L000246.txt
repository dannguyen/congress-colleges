LEONARD, George, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Norton, Mass., July 4, 1729
was graduated from Harvard College in 1748
register of probate 1749-1783
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice at Norton in 1750
member of the provincial assembly 1764-1766
executive councilor 1770-1775
judge of the probate court 1784-1790
judge of the common pleas court 1785-1798 and chief justice 1798-1804
elected as a Pro-Administration candidate to the First and Second Congresses (March 4, 1789-March 3, 1793)
member of the state senate in 1792 and 1793
elected as a Federalist to the Fourth Congress (March 4, 1795-March 3, 1797)
served in the state house of representatives in 1801and 1802
died in Raynham, Mass., July 26, 1819
interment in the local cemetery at Norton, Mass.

LEWIS, Joseph Horace, a Representative from Kentucky
born near Glasgow, Barren County, Ky., October 29, 1824
attended the common schools
was graduated from Centre College, Danville, Ky., in 1843
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1845 and commenced practice in Glasgow, Ky.
member of the State house of representatives 1850-1855
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1857 to the Thirty-fifth Congress and in 1861 to the Thirty-seventh Congress
during the Civil War commanded the Sixth Kentucky Regiment in the Confederate Army, the Second Brigade and the First Brigade in BatesÂ’ division
returned to Glasgow at the close of the Civil War and resumed the practice of law
again a member of the State house of representatives in 1869 and 1870
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-first Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Jacob S. Golladay
reelected to the Forty-second Congress and served from May 10, 1870, to March 3, 1873
was not a candidate for renomination in 1872
resumed the practice of his profession
elected judge of the Kentucky Court of Appeals in 1874
reelected to subsequent terms and served until 1898
moved to a farm in Scott County, near Georgetown, where he died on July 6, 1904
interment in Glasgow Cemetery.

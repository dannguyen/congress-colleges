LITTLE, Joseph James, a Representative from New York
born in Bristol, England, June 5, 1841
immigrated to the United States in 1846 with his parents, who settled in Morris, Otsego County, N.Y.
attended the common schools
apprenticed to the local printer and entered a New York book-printing office to complete his trade
served in the Union Army 1862-1864 as corporal, first sergeant, and first lieutenant
established a printing business in 1867 at New York City
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-second Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Roswell P. Flower and served from November 3, 1891, to March 3, 1893
was not a candidate for renomination in 1892 to the Fifty-third Congress
served as commissioner of education and president of the board of education of New York City
engaged in the printing and publishing business until his death in New York City on February 11, 1913
interment in Kensico Cemetery, Valhalla, N.Y.

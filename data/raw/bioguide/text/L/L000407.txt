LOGUE, James Washington, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., February 22, 1863
attended the public schools and was graduated from La Salle College, Philadelphia, Pa.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1888 and commenced the practice of his profession in Philadelphia
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress (March 4, 1913-March 3, 1915)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914 to the Sixty-fourth Congress
unsuccessful candidate for Lieutenant Governor of Pennsylvania in 1918
resumed the practice of law in Philadelphia
member of the speakersÂ’ bureau of the Council of National Defense during the First World War
secretary of the board of inspectors of the Eastern Penitentiary in 1923
died in Philadelphia, Pa., August 27, 1925
interment in Holy Sepulchre Cemetery.

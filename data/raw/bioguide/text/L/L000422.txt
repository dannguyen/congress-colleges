LONG, John Benjamin, a Representative from Texas
born in Douglass, Nacogdoches County, Tex., September 8, 1843
moved with his parents to Rusk, Cherokee County, Tex., in 1846
educated in private schools
during the Civil War served in the Confederate Army in Company C, Third Texas Cavalry
studied law
was admitted to the bar but never practiced
became a planter
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-second Congress (March 4, 1891-March 3, 1893)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1892
engaged in the newspaper business in Rusk, Tex., 1886-1905
member of the State house of representatives in 1913 and 1914
died in Rusk, Tex., April 27, 1924
interment in Cedar Hill Cemetery.

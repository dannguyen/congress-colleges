LOVE, Thomas Cutting, a Representative from New York
born in Cambridge, N.Y., November 30, 1789
attended the common schools
served as a Volunteer in the War of 1812
wounded and taken prisoner at the Battle of Fort Erie on September 17, 1814
taken to Quebec and kept imprisoned until the close of the war
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
moved to Batavia, N.Y., and later to Buffalo
judge of Erie County in 1828 and 1829
district attorney 1829-1835 and surrogate 1841-1845
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1837)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1836
resumed the practice of law until 1847 when he retired from active practice
died in Buffalo, N.Y., September 17, 1853
interment in Forest Lawn Cemetery.

LOVERING, William Croad, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Woonsocket, R.I., February 25, 1835
moved with his parents to Taunton, Mass., in 1837
attended the Cambridge High School and the Hopkins Classical School, Cambridge, Mass.
left school in 1859 for employment in his fathers mill
during the Civil War served as quartermaster of Engineers in the Second Massachusetts Brigade, consisting of the Second and Third Regiments
engaged in cotton manufacturing in Taunton
first president of the Taunton Street Railway
president of the American Liability Insurance Co.
interested in several other business enterprises
president of the New England Cotton Manufacturers Association for two years
member of the State senate in 1874 and 1875
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1880
presided at the Republican State convention in 1892
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fifth and to the six succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1897, until his death in Washington, D.C., February 4, 1910
interment in Mount Pleasant Cemetery, Taunton, Mass.

LUCAS, John Baptiste Charles, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Pont-Audemer, Normandy, France, August 14, 1758
attended the Honfleur and Paris Law Schools, and was graduated from the law department of the University of Caen in 1782
practiced law in France until 1784
immigrated to the United States, settled near Pittsburgh, Pa., and engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives 1792-1798
judge of the common pleas court in 1794
elected as a Republican to the Eighth and Ninth Congresses and served from March 4, 1803, until his resignation in 1805, before the assembling of the Ninth Congress
moved to St. Louis, La. (now Missouri), having been appointed district judge for the northern district of Louisiana (which became Missouri Territory in 1812), and served from 1805 until 1820, when he resigned
also served as commissioner of land claims of northern Louisiana 1805-1812
resumed agricultural pursuits
died near St. Louis, Mo., August 17, 1842
interment in Calvary Cemetery.

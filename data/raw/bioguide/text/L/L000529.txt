LYMAN, Theodore, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Waltham, Mass., August 23, 1833
was educated by private tutors
studied in Europe 1847-1849
was graduated from Harvard University in 1855 and from the Lawrence Scientific School of Harvard University in 1858
served during the Civil War as lieutenant colonel and volunteer aide-de-camp on the staff of Major General Meade from September 2, 1863, to April 20, 1865
member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and of the National Academy of Sciences
trustee of the Peabody Education Fund
one of the State fishery commissioners 1865-1882
overseer of Harvard University 1868-1880
elected as an Independent Republican to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1885)
retired on account of ill health
died in Nahant, Mass., September 9, 1897
interment in Mount Auburn Cemetery, Cambridge, Mass.

LYNCH, John, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Providence, R.I., November 1, 1843
moved to Pennsylvania in 1856 with his parents, who settled in Wilkes-Barre
attended the public schools and Wyoming Seminary, Kingston, Pa.
worked on a farm and in the coal mines
taught school
studied law
was admitted to the bar November 1, 1868, and commenced practice in Wilkes-Barre, Pa.
elected as a Democrat to the Fiftieth Congress (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1889)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1888 to the Fifty-first Congress
resumed the practice of law in Wilkes-Barre
judge of the court of common pleas 1892-1910
died in Atlantic City, N.J., August 17, 1910

MAGNER, Thomas Francis, (uncle of John Francis Carew), a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y. March 8, 1860
attended the public schools
was graduated from St. Xavier College in 1880 and from Columbia University, New York City, in 1882
taught in a public school in Brooklyn
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1883 and commenced practice in Brooklyn, N.Y., the same year
member of the State assembly in 1888
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-first, Fifty-second, and Fifty-third Congresses (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1895)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1894
resumed the practice of law
corporation counsel of the Borough of Brooklyn 1913-1917
continued the practice of his profession in Brooklyn, N.Y., until his death there on December 22, 1945
interment in Holy Cross Cemetery.

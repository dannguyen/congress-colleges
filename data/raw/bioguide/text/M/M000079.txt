MALLARY, Rollin Carolas, a Representative from Vermont
born in Cheshire, New Haven County, Conn., May 27, 1784
was graduated from Middlebury (Vt.) College in 1805
moved to Poultney, Rutland County, Vt.
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Castleton, Vt., in 1807
elected trustee of the Rutland County Grammar School in 1807
secretary to the Governor and council in 1807, 1809-1812, and 1815-1819
StateÂ’s attorney for Rutland County 1811-1813, 1815, and 1816
moved to Poultney in 1818
as a Republican, successfully contested the election of Orsamus C. Merrill to the Sixteenth Congress
elected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress, reelected as an Adam-Clay Republican to the Eighteenth Congress, elected as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth and Twentieth Congresses, and reelected as an Anti-Jacksonian candidate to the Twenty-first and Twenty-second Congresses and served from January 13, 1820, until his death in Baltimore, Md., April 15, 1831
chairman, Committee on Manufactures (Nineteenth through Twenty-first Congresses)
interment in East Poultney Cemetery, East Poultney, Vt.

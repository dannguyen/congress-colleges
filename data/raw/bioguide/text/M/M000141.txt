MARR, Alem, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Upper Mount Bethel, Northampton County, Pa., June 18, 1787
moved to Northumberland County in 1795 with his parents, who settled near Milton, Pa.
attended the common schools and was graduated from Princeton College in 1807
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1813 and commenced practice in Danville, Montour County, Pa.
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
was not a candidate for renomination and retired to his farm near Milton, Northumberland County, where he died March 29, 1843
interment in Milton Cemetery.

MARSH, George Perkins, (son of Charles Marsh), a Representative from Vermont
born in Woodstock, Windsor County, Vt., March 15, 1801
was graduated from Dartmouth College, Hanover, N.H., in 1820
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1825 and commenced practice in Burlington, Vt.
member of the GovernorÂ’s council in 1835
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-eighth and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1843, until his resignation in 1849, having been appointed by President Taylor as Minister Resident to Turkey, in which office he served from May 29, 1849, to December 19, 1853
charged with a special mission to Greece in 1852
fish commissioner of Vermont in 1857 and railroad commissioner 1857-1859
appointed by President Lincoln as Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary to Italy on March 20, 1861, and served until his death in Vallombrosa, Italy, July 23, 1882
interment in English Cemetery, Rome, Italy.

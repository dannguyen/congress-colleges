MARTIN, John Mason, (son of Joshua Lanier Martin), a Representative from Alabama
born in Athens, Limestone County, Ala., January 20, 1837
attended the common schools, the high school in Green Springs, Ala., and the University of Alabama at Tuscaloosa
was graduated from Centre College, Danville, Ky., in 1856
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1858 and commenced practice in Tuscaloosa, Ala.
member of the State senate 1871-1876 and served as president pro tempore 1873-1876
professor of equity jurisprudence in the University of Alabama 1875-1886
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1885-March 3, 1887)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1886 to the Fiftieth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Birmingham, Ala.
died in Bowling Green, Warren County, Ky., June 16, 1898
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Tuscaloosa, Ala.

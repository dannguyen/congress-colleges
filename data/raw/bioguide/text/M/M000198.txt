MARTIN, Robert Nicols, a Representative from Maryland
born in Cambridge, Dorchester County, Md., January 14, 1798
attended the public schools
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced at Princess Anne, Md., 1819-1827
elected as an Adams candidate to the Nineteenth Congress (March 4, 1825-March 3, 1827)
settled in Baltimore and resumed the practice of law
appointed by Governor Pratt chief justice of the western judicial district in 1845, in which capacity he served until the office was vacated by the constitution of 1851
again engaged in the practice of his profession in Baltimore
judge of the superior court of Baltimore 1859-1867
professor of international law in the University of Maryland at Baltimore 1867-1870
died at Saratoga Springs, N.Y., July 20, 1870
interment in Christ Protestant Episcopal Church Cemetery, Cambridge, Md.

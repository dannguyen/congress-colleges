MASON, Harry Howland, a Representative from Illinois
born on a farm in McLean County, near Farmer City, De Witt County, Ill., December 16, 1873
moved to Delavan, Tazewell County, with his parents and attended the public schools
engaged in newspaper work
moved to Pawnee, Sangamon County, Ill., in 1903 and engaged in the newspaper publishing business
secretary to Congressman J. Earl Major 1930-1933
treasurer of Sangamon County in 1933 and 1934
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-fourth Congress (January 3, 1935-January 3, 1937)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1936
resumed the newspaper publishing business in Pawnee, Ill.
died March 10, 1946, in Springfield, Ill.
interment in Prairie Rest Cemetery, Delavan, Ill.

MASON, James Brown, a Representative from Rhode Island
born in Thompson, Windham County, Conn., in January 1775
pursued classical studies
was graduated from Brown University, Providence, R.I., in 1791
studied medicine and was admitted to practice
moved to Charleston, S.C., and practiced 1795-1798
returned to Providence, R.I., and engaged in mercantile pursuits 1798-1819
member of the State house of representatives 1804-1814 and served as speaker from February 1812 to May 1814
elected as a Federalist to the Fourteenth and Fifteenth Congresses (March 4, 1815-March 3, 1819)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1818 to the Sixteenth Congress
served as a trustee of Brown University 1804-1819
died in Providence, R.I., August 31, 1819
interment in North Burial Ground.

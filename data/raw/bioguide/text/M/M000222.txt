MASON, Joseph, a Representative from New York
born in Plattsburg, Clinton County, N.Y., March 30, 1828
moved with his parents to Hamilton, Madison County, N.Y., in 1840
attended Hamilton Academy and Madison College (later Colgate University), Hamilton, N.Y.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1849 and practiced in Hamilton, N.Y.
elected justice of the peace in 1849 and served in that capacity until 1904
elected county judge and surrogate of Madison County for the term commencing January 1, 1864, and served four years
collector of internal revenue 1871-1876
served as city attorney for many years
elected as a Republican to the Forty-sixth and Forty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1879-March 3, 1883)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1882
resumed the practice of law in Hamilton, N.Y., and died there May 31, 1914
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

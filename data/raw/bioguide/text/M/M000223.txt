MASON, Moses, Jr., a Representative from Maine
born in Dublin, Cheshire County, N.H., June 2, 1789
moved with his parents to Bethel, Oxford County, Maine, in 1799
attended the common schools
studied medicine
and commenced practice in Bethel in 1813
appointed first postmaster of Bethel April 1, 1815, serving until December 27, 1833
justice of the peace 1821-1866
county commissioner 1831-1834
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
executive councilor 1843-1845
trustee of the State insane hospital in 1844
selectman of Bethel for fourteen years
president of GouldÂ’s Academy 1854-1856
died in Bethel, Maine, June 25, 1866
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

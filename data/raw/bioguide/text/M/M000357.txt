McCONNELL, William John, a Senator from Idaho
born in Commerce, Oakland County, Mich., September 18, 1839
pursued an academic course
moved to California in 1860 and engaged in mining, the cattle business, merchandising, and banking
resided in Oregon in 1862 and 1863 and taught school in Yamhill County
moved to Idaho in 1863
deputy United States marshal 1865-1867
returned to Oregon and was engaged in the cattle business
member, Oregon State senate 1882, and served as president
returned to Idaho in 1886
member of the constitutional convention of Idaho in 1890
upon the admission of Idaho as a State into the Union was elected as a Republican to the United States Senate and served from December 18, 1890, to March 3, 1891
was not a candidate for renomination
Governor of Idaho 1892-1896
appointed Indian inspector by President William McKinley in 1897 and served until 1901
appointed by President William Taft an inspector in the Immigration Service in 1909 and served until his death in Moscow, Idaho, on March 30, 1925
interment in Moscow Cemetery.

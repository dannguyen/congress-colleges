McCREERY, William, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Omagh, County Tyrone, Ireland, May 17, 1786
immigrated to the United States in 1791 with his parents, who settled near Fairfield, Westmoreland County, Pa.
attended private school
moved to Paris, Washington County, Pa., in 1812 and engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives 1824-1827
constructor of the Pennsylvania State Canal and of the State highway 1826-1831
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1830 to the Twenty-second Congress
served as collector of internal revenue at Pittsburgh 1831-1833
again a member of the State house of representatives 1833-1836
superintendent of the Pennsylvania State Canal in 1835, residing in Allegheny City, Allegheny County, Pa.
acting president of the Pennsylvania Board of Canal Appraisers at the time of his death
died in Fairfield, Pa., on September 27, 1841
interment in Up-the-Valley United Presbyterian Church Cemetery.

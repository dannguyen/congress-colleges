McCULLOCH, John, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in McCulloch Mills, Pa., November 15, 1806
attended the common schools and was graduated from Jefferson College, Canonsburg, Pa., in 1825
studied medicine and was graduated from the medical department of the University of Pennsylvania in 1829
commenced practice in Green Tree, Huntingdon County, Pa.
moved to Petersburg in 1830, where he engaged in the practice of his profession until 1852
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1855)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1854
resumed the practice of medicine in Huntingdon, Pa.
affiliated with the Republican Party upon its formation in 1856
member of the State constitutional convention in 1874
died in Huntingdon, Pa., May 15, 1879
interment in Riverside Cemetery.

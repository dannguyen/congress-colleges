McCULLOGH, Welty, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Greensburg, Westmoreland County, Pa., October 10, 1847
attended the common schools and Washington and Jefferson College, Washington, Pa.
served as second clerk under Capt. W.B. Coulter, provost marshal of twenty-first district of Pennsylvania, during the Civil War
graduated from Princeton College in June 1870
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1872 and commenced practice in Greensburg
assistant solicitor for the Baltimore & Ohio Railroad
elected as a Republican to the Fiftieth Congress (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1889)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1888
continued the practice of law until his death in Greensburg, Pa., August 31, 1889
interment in the new St. Clair Cemetery.

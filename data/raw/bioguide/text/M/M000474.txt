McILVAINE, Abraham Robinson, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Ridley, Delaware County, Pa., August 14, 1804
attended the common schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits in Chester County, Pa.
member of the State house of representatives in 1836 and 1837
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-eighth, Twenty-ninth, and Thirtieth Congresses (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1849)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of War (Twenty-eighth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1848
resumed agricultural interests and also engaged in the iron business
died on his estate, Springton Manor Farm in Chester County, Pa., August 22, 1863
interment in Caln Orthodox Quaker Meeting Burial Ground near Downingtown
reinterment in Northwood Cemetery, Downingtown, Pa.

McLEAN, Finis Ewing, (brother of John McLean and uncle of James David Walker), a Representative from Kentucky
born near Russellville, Logan County, Ky., February 19, 1806
attended the country schools and Lebanon Academy in Logan County
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Elkton, Ky., in 1827
also engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives in 1837
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1851)
resumed the practice of law and also engaged in agricultural pursuits
moved to Andrew County, Mo., in 1860 and engaged in farming until 1865
moved to Greencastle, Ind., in 1865, in which city he died April 12, 1881
interment in Forest Hill Cemetery.

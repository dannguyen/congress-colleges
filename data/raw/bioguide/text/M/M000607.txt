McVEAN, Charles, a Representative from New York
born near Johnstown, N.Y., in 1802
pursued an academic course
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Johnstown
editor of a newspaper in Canajoharie 1827-1831
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third Congress (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1835)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1834
district attorney of Montgomery County 1836-1839
moved to New York City in 1839
resumed the practice of his profession
appointed surrogate of New York County January 24, 1844, and served until 1848
appointed United States attorney for the southern district of New York September 1, 1848
died in New York City, December 22, 1848

MEACHAM, James, a Representative from Vermont
born in Rutland, Rutland County, Vt., August 16, 1810
was graduated from Middlebury (Vt.) College in 1832
taught in the seminary at Castleton, Vt., and in the local academy at St. Albans, Vt.
attended Andover (Vt.) Theological Seminary, where he studied for the ministry and was ordained as a Congregational minister in 1838, assuming his duties as pastor in New Haven, Vt., and served from 1839 to 1846
tutor and professor at Middlebury College 1846-1850
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of George P. Marsh
reelected as a Whig to the Thirty-second and Thirty-third Congresses, and elected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress and served from December 3, 1849, until his death in Rutland, Vt., August 23, 1856
chairman, Committee on District of Columbia (Thirty-fourth Congress)
interment in West Cemetery, Middlebury, Vt.

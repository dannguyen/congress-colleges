MERRICK, William Duhurst, (father of William Matthew Merrick), a Senator from Maryland
born in Annapolis, Md., October 25, 1793
completed preparatory studies and graduated from Georgetown University, Washington, D.C.
held several local offices
served in the War of 1812
register of wills of Charles County, Md., 1825-1832
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Port Tobacco, Md.
member, State house of delegates 1832-1838
elected as a Whig to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Joseph Kent
reelected in 1839 and served from January 4, 1838, to March 3, 1845
chairman, Committee on the District of Columbia (Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses), Committee on Post Office and Post Roads (Twenty-seventh and Twenty-eighth Congresses)
member of the State constitutional convention in 1850
member, State house of delegates from January 1856, until his death in Washington, D.C., February 5, 1857
interment in Mount Olivet Cemetery.

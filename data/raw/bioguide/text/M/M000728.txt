MILLER, Homer Virgil Milton, a Senator from Georgia
born in Pendleton District, S.C., April 29, 1814
moved with his parents to Rabun County, Ga., in 1820
attended the common schools and graduated from the Medical College of South Carolina in 1835
continued medical studies in Paris and commenced practice in Cassville, Ga., in 1838
unsuccessful Whig candidate for election to the Twenty-ninth Congress in 1844
served during the Civil War in the Confederate Army as a surgeon and as medical director, surgeon of posts, and inspector of hospitals in Georgia
resumed the practice of medicine in Rome, Ga.
member of the State reconstruction convention in 1867
member of the faculty of the Atlanta Medical College
upon the readmission of Georgia to representation was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate on July 28, 1868
qualified on February 24, 1871, and served until March 3, 1871
trustee of the University of Georgia at Athens
died in Atlanta, Fulton County, Ga., May 31, 1896
interment in Myrtle Hill Cemetery, Rome, Ga.

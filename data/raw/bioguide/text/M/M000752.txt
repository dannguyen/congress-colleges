MILLER, Samuel Franklin, a Representative from New York
born in Franklin, Delaware County, N.Y., May 27, 1827
was graduated from the Delaware Literary Institute and Hamilton College, Clinton, N.Y., in 1852
studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1853, but did not engage in extensive practice
engaged in farming and lumbering
member of the State assembly in 1854
served as a colonel in the State militia
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1863-March 3, 1865)
member of the State constitutional convention in 1867
district collector of internal revenue 1869-1873
member of the State board of charities 1869-1877
elected to the Forty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1877)
continued agricultural pursuits and lumbering
died in Franklin, N.Y., on March 16, 1892
interment in Ouleout Valley Cemetery.

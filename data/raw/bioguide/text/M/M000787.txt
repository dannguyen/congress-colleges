MILTON, John Gerald, a Senator from New Jersey
born in Jersey City, N.J., January 21, 1881
attended the public schools
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1903 and commenced practice in Jersey City, N.J.
appointed on January 18, 1938, as a Democrat to the United States Senate, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of A. Harry Moore and served from January 18, 1938, to November 8, 1938, when a successor was elected
was not a candidate to fill the vacancy
resumed the practice of law
resided in Jersey City, N.J., where he died April 14, 1977
interment in Holy Cross Cemetery, North Arlington, N.J.

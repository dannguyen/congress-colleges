MINSHALL, William Edwin, Jr., a Representative from Ohio
born in East Cleveland, Cuyahoga County, Ohio, October 24, 1911
attended the public schools of East Cleveland, the University School, Shaker Heights, Ohio, and the University of Virginia at Charlottesville
was graduated from the Cleveland Law School in 1940
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced the practice of law in Cleveland, Ohio
member of the State house of representatives in 1939 and 1940
enlisted in December 1940 as a private in the United States Army and served in the European Theater, G-2 section, Headquarters III Corps, and was discharged as a lieutenant colonel in March 1946
awarded Bronze Star
special assistant attorney general of Ohio, 1948-1952
general counsel, Maritime Administration, Washington, D.C., in 1953 and 1954
elected as a Republican to the Eighty-fourth Congress and to the nine succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1955, until his resignation December 31, 1974
was not a candidate for reelection in 1974 to the Ninety-fourth Congress
was a resident of Delray Beach, Fla., until his death on October 15, 1990
interment in Arlington National Cemetery, Arlington, Va.

MITCHELL, Charles F., a Representative from New York
born in Bucks County, Pa., February 18, 1806
attended public schools
moved to Lockport, N.Y., in 1829
appointed one of the firemen of the village May 21, 1829
engaged in grain milling and other businesses
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-fifth and Twenty-sixth Congresses (March 4, 1837-March 3, 1841)
relocated first to Flemingsburg, Ky. and later to Cincinnati, Ohio, where he died on September 27, 1865
interment in Spring Grove Cemtery, Cincinnati, Ohio.

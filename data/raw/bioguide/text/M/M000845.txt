MOLLOHAN, Robert Homer, (father of Alan Bowlby Mollohan), a Representative from West Virginia
born in Grantsville, Calhoun County, W.Va., September 18, 1909
attended the public schools, Glenville College, and Shepherd College 1929-1931
deputy collector of internal revenue at Parkersburg, W.Va., in 1933 and chief of miscellaneous tax division and cashier 1935-1938
district manager of Works Progress Administration in 1939
State director for the Census Bureau in 1940
superintendent of State Industrial School for Boys 1941-1948
clerk of the United States Senate District of Columbia Committee in 1949 and 1950
United States marshal for the northern district of West Virginia in 1950
again served as clerk of the Senate District of Columbia Committee 1950-1952
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-third and Eighty-fourth Congresses (January 3, 1953-January 3, 1957)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1956, but was an unsuccessful candidate for Governor
unsuccessful candidate in 1958 for election to the Eighty-sixth Congress
engaged in general insurance business
elected to the Ninety-first and to the six succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1969-January 3, 1983)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1982
resumed insurance business
was a resident of Fairmont, W.Va., until his death on August 3, 1999.

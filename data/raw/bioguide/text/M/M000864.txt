MONTGOMERY, Daniel, Jr., a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Londonderry, Chester County, Pa., October 30, 1765
moved to Danville, Pa.
chief promoter of turnpike roads in the section around Danville
elected as a member of the State house of representatives in 1800
lieutenant colonel of the Eighty-first Pennsylvania Militia in 1805
appointed major general of the Ninth Division of Militia on July 27, 1809
elected as a Republican to the Tenth Congress (March 4, 1807-March 3, 1809)
appointed canal commissioner in 1828
died in Danville, Montour County, Pa., December 30, 1831.

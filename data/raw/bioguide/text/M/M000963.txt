MORPHIS, Joseph Lewis, a Representative from Mississippi
born near Pocahontas, McNairy County, Tenn., April 17, 1831
pursued elementary studies
engaged in planting
member of the State house of representatives in 1859
entered the Confederate Army as captain in August 1861 and served until the close of the Civil War
moved with his family to Pontotoc, Miss., in 1863
member of the State constitutional convention in 1865
member of the State house of representatives 1866-1868
upon the readmission of the State of Mississippi to representation was elected as a Republican to the Forty-first and Forty-second Congresses and served from February 23, 1870, to March 3, 1873
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1872
appointed by President Hayes as United States marshal of the northern district of Mississippi and served from 1877 to 1885
licensed as an Indian trader on the Osage Reservation in 1890 and engaged in that occupation until 1901
lived in retirement until his death in Cleveland, Pawnee County, Okla., July 29, 1913
interment in Woodland Cemetery.

MORRISON, James Lowry Donaldson, a Representative from Illinois
born in Kaskaskia, Ill., April 12, 1816
appointed midshipman in the Navy in 1832 and served until December 31, 1839, when he resigned
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Belleville, Ill.
member of the State house of representatives in 1844
raised a company and served in the Mexican War as lieutenant colonel of BissellÂ’s regiment of Illinois Volunteers from July 1, 1846, to July 1, 1847
was presented a sword by the Illinois legislature for services at Buena Vista
member of the State senate in 1848
unsuccessful Whig candidate for Lieutenant Governor in 1852
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Lyman Trumbull and served from November 4, 1856, to March 3, 1857
at the same election was not the nominee for the Thirty-fifth Congress
was an unsuccessful candidate for the Democratic nomination for Governor of Illinois in 1860
died in St. Louis, Mo., on August 14, 1888
interment in Calvary Cemetery.

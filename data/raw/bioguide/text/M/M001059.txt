MUDD, Sydney Emanuel, (father of Sydney Emanuel Mudd [1885-1924]), a Representative from Maryland
born at Gallant Green, Charles County, Md., February 12, 1858
attended Georgetown University, Washington, D.C., and was graduated from St. Johns College, Annapolis, Md., in 1878
studied law privately and also attended the law department of the University of Virginia at Charlottesville
was admitted to the bar in 1880 and practiced
member of the State house of delegates in 1879 and 1881
successfully contested as a Republican the election of Barnes Compton to the Fifty-first Congress and served from March 20, 1890, to March 3, 1891
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1890 to the Fifty-second Congress
elected to the State house of delegates in 1895 and served as speaker
moved to La Plata in 1896
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1896
elected to the Fifty-fifth and to the six succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1897-March 3, 1911)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of Justice (Sixtieth and Sixty-first Congresses)
died in Philadelphia, Pa., October 21, 1911
interment in St. Ignatius Catholic Church Cemetery, Chapel Point, near La Plata, Charles County, Md.

MULKEY, William Oscar, a Representative from Alabama
born in Brundidge, Pike County, Ala., July 27, 1871
attended the public schools and was graduated from State Normal College, Troy, Ala., in 1892
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1893 and commenced practice in Troy, Geneva County, Ala., in 1894
member of the State constitutional convention in 1901
served in the State house of representatives in 1911
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Henry D. Clayton and served from June 29, 1914, to March 3, 1915
was not a candidate for renomination in 1914
resumed the practice of law
died in Geneva, Ala., June 30, 1943
interment in Geneva Cemetery.

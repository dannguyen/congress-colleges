MURRAY, Thomas, Jr., (cousin of John Murray), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Potts Grove, East Chillisquaque Township, Northumberland County, Pa., in 1770
attended private schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the state house of representatives in 1813
served in the state senate in 1814
elected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of William Cox Ellis and served from October 9, 1821, to March 3, 1823
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1822 to the Eighteenth Congress
died in East Chillisquaque Township, Northumberland County, Pa., August 26, 1823
interment in Chillisquaque Cemetery, near Potts Grove, Pa.

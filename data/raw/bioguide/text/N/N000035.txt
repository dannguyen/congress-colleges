NELSON, Hugh, (son of Thomas Nelson, Jr.), a Representative from Virginia
born in Yorktown, York County, Va., September 30, 1768
completed preparatory studies
was graduated from the College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Va., in 1780
served in the State senate 1786-1791
member of the State house of delegates 1805-1809, 1828-1829
judge of the general court
elected as a Republican to the Twelfth and to the five succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1811, until his resignation on January 14, 1823, having received an appointment in the diplomatic service
chairman, Committee on the Judiciary (Fourteenth, Fifteenth, and Seventeenth Congresses)
appointed by President James Monroe United States Minister to Spain on January 15, 1823, and served until November 23, 1824
died at his home, Belvoir, Albemarle County, Va., March 18, 1836
interment in Belvoir Cemetery, Cismont, Albemarle County, Va.

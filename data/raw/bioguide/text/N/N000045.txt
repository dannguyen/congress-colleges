NELSON, William, a Representative from New York
born in Hyde Park, Dutchess County, N.Y., June 29, 1784
attended the common schools and was graduated from Poughkeepsie Academy
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Peekskill, Westchester County, N.Y., in 1807
for thirty years served as district attorney for Putnam, Rockland, and Westchester Counties
member of the State assembly in 1820 and 1821
served in the State senate 1824-1827
judge of the court for the correction of errors 1824-1827
elected as a Whig to the Thirtieth and Thirty-first Congresses (March 4, 1847-March 3, 1851)
resumed the practice of his profession
died in Peekskill, N.Y., October 3, 1869
interment in Hillside Cemetery.

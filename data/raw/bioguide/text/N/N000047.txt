NES, Henry, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in York, Pa., May 20, 1799
completed preparatory studies and was graduated from Princeton College
studied medicine and practiced in York, Pa.
elected as an Independent Democrat to the Twenty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1845)
elected as a Whig to the Thirtieth and Thirty-first Congresses and served from March 4, 1847, until his death in York, Pa., September 10, 1850
chairman, Committee on Invalid Pensions (Thirtieth Congress), Committee on Revisal and Unfinished Business (Thirtieth Congress)
interment in Prospect Hill Cemetery.

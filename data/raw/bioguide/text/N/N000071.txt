NEWNAN, Daniel, a Representative from Georgia
born in Salisbury, Rowan County, N.C., about 1780
completed preparatory studies
attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1796 and 1797
commissioned ensign and second lieutenant in the Fourth United States Infantry, March 3, 1799
promoted to first lieutenant the following November and resigned January 1, 1801
engaged in planting
commanded the Georgia Volunteers in the Creek War 1812-1814
major general of the third division of state militia in 1817
superintendent of the state penitentiary 1823-1825
secretary of state of Georgia 1825-1827
the city of Newnan, Ga., was named for him in 1828
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-second Congress (March 4, 1831-March 3, 1833)
unsuccessful for reelection in 1832 to the Twenty-third Congress
died near Rossville, Ga., January 16, 1851
interment in Newnan Springs (Ga.) Churchyard.

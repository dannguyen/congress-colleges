NICHOLAS, Robert Carter, (nephew of John Nicholas and Wilson Cary Nicholas), a Senator from Louisiana
born in Hanover, Hanover County, Va., in 1793
served in the War of 1812 as captain and major
attended the College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Va.
moved to Louisiana and became a sugar planter in Terrebonne Parish in 1820
elected as a Jacksonian (later Democrat) to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Senator-elect Charles E.A. Gayarre and served from January 13, 1836, to March 3, 1841
secretary of State of Louisiana 1843-1846, when he resigned
died in Terrebonne Parish, La., on December 24, 1857
interment in the Burthe vault, St. Louis Cemetery, New Orleans, La.

NICHOLSON, Donald William, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Wareham, Plymouth County, Mass., August 11, 1888
attended the public schools and took college extension courses
engaged as a salesman
during the First World War served in the United States Army 1917-1919, with overseas service
selectman, assessor, and overseer of the poor, Town of Wareham, Mass., 1920-1925
delegate to all Republican state conventions 1924-1947
served in the Massachusetts state house of representatives in 1925 and 1926
member of the Massachusetts state senate 1926-1947, serving as president in 1946 and 1947
elected as a Republican to the Eightieth Congress by special election, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of United States Representative Charles L. Gifford, and reelected to the five succeeding Congresses (November 18, 1947-January 3, 1959)
was not a candidate for renomination to the Eighty-sixth Congress in 1958
retired and resided at Wareham, Mass., until his death February 16, 1968
interment in Center Cemetery.

NIEDRINGHAUS, Frederick Gottlieb, (uncle of Henry Frederick Niedringhaus), a Representative from Missouri
born in Luebbecke, Westphalia, North Germany, on October 21, 1837
attended the common schools
learned the glazing, painting, and tinning trades
immigrated to the United States in November 1855 and settled in St. Louis, Mo.
began the stamping of tinware in 1862
invented what is called granite ironware in 1874 and established an extensive business
became interested in various other business enterprises in St. Louis
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-first Congress (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1891)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1890
resumed his former business pursuits
died in St. Louis, Mo., November 25, 1922
interment in Bellefontaine Cemetery.

NORRIS, Moses, Jr., a Representative and a Senator from New Hampshire
born in Pittsfield, N.H., November 8, 1799
attended the public schools and the Pittsfield Academy, and graduated from Dartmouth College, Hanover, N.H., in 1828
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1832 and commenced practice in Barnstead
returned to Pittsfield in 1834
member, State house of representatives 1837-1840, 1842
member, State council 1841-1842
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-eighth and Twenty-ninth Congresses (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1847)
member, State house of representatives 1847-1848, and served as speaker
elected to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1849, until his death
chairman, Committee on Claims (Thirty-first Congress), Committee on Patents and the Patent Office (Thirty-second Congress), Committee on the District of Columbia (Thirty-third Congress)
died in Washington, D.C., January 11, 1855
interment in Floral Park Cemetery, Pittsfield, N.H.

O'CONNOR, James, a Representative from Louisiana
born in New Orleans, La., April 4, 1870
attended the public schools and was graduated from the law department of Tulane University, New Orleans, La., in 1900
member of the State constitutional conventions in 1898 and 1913
served in the State house of representatives 1900-1912
assistant city attorney of Orleans Parish from 1918 until his resignation in 1919, having been elected to the United States House of Representatives
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Albert Estopinal
reelected to the Sixty-seventh and to the four succeeding Congresses and served from June 5, 1919, to March 3, 1931
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1930
resumed the practice of law

died in Covington, La., January 7, 1941

OLIN, Abram Baldwin, (son of Gideon Olin), a Representative from New York
born in Shaftsbury, Bennington County, Vt., September 21, 1808
attended the common schools, and was graduated from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass., in 1835
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1838 and commenced practice in Troy, N.Y.
recorder of the city of Troy 1844-1852
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth, Thirty-sixth, and Thirty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1863)
appointed by President Lincoln an associate justice of the supreme court of the District of Columbia and served from March 11, 1863, until he voluntarily retired January 13, 1879
died near Sligo, Montgomery County, Md., July 7, 1879
interment in the Danforth family lot adjacent to West Lawn Cemetery, Williamstown, Berkshire County, Mass.

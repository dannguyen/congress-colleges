OLIVER, Andrew, a Representative from New York
born in Springfield, N.Y., January 16, 1815
was graduated from Union College, Schenectady, N.Y., in 1835
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Penn Yan, Yates County, N.Y., in 1838
judge of the court of common pleas 1843-1847
judge of the surrogate and county courts in 1846
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third and Thirty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1857)
chairman, Committee on Invalid Pensions (Thirty-fourth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate on the American Party ticket for reelection in 1856 to the Thirty-fifth Congress
engaged in agricultural pursuits and also in the practice of law
again served as county judge and surrogate 1872-1877
died in Penn Yan, N.Y., March 6, 1889
interment in Lake View Cemetery.

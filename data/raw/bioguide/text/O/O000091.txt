O'NEALL, John Henry, a Representative from Indiana
born in Newberry, Newberry County, S.C., October 30, 1838
was left an orphan when eight years of age and was reared by his grandfather, who resided in Daviess County, Ind.
attended country schools and was graduated from Indiana University at Bloomington in 1862
was graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor in 1864
was admitted to the bar the same year and practiced in Terre Haute and later in Washington, Ind.
served in the State legislature in 1866
appointed prosecuting attorney for the eleventh judicial circuit in 1873
elected to the office in 1874, but resigned before his term was completed
elected as a Democrat to the Fiftieth and Fifty-first Congresses (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1891)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1890
resumed the practice of law in Washington, Ind.
school trustee of Washington for fifteen years
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1896
city attorney of Washington 1899-1907
organized the Federal Trust Co. in 1899 and was its president until 1902, when it was made a national bank
died in Washington, Daviess County, Ind., July 15, 1907

OSBORNE, John Eugene, a Representative from Wyoming
born in Westport, Essex County, N.Y., June 19, 1858
attended the common schools and was graduated from the high school at Westport
studied medicine and was graduated from the University of Vermont at Burlington in 1880
moved to Rawlins, Wyo., and engaged in the practice of medicine
later engaged in raising livestock on the open range
member of the Wyoming Territorial legislature 1883-1885
served as chairman of the Territorial penitentiary building commission in 1888, and as mayor of the city of Rawlins the same year
Governor of Wyoming 1893-1895
was renominated but declined
chairman of the Wyoming delegation to the Democratic National Convention in 1896
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1897-March 3, 1899)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1898
member of the Democratic National Committee 1900-1920
First Assistant Secretary of State 1913-1917
engaged in banking and stock raising
died in Rawlins, Wyo., on April 24, 1943
interment in Cedar Hill Cemetery, Princeton, Ky.

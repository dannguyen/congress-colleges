PARKER, James, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Boston, Mass., in 1768
completed preparatory studies
studied medicine and began practice in Gardiner, Maine (then a part of Massachusetts)
member of the Massachusetts senate in 1811 and 1812
elected as a Republican to the Thirteenth Congress (March 4, 1813-March 3, 1815)
elected to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1821)
resumed the practice of medicine
died in Gardiner, Kennebee County, Maine, November 9, 1837
interment in Oak Grove Cemetery.

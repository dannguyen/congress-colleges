PARKS, Gorham, a Representative from Maine
born in Westfield, Mass., May 27, 1794
attended the common schools and was graduated from Harvard University in 1813
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1819 and practiced
moved to Bangor, Maine, in 1823 and continued the practice of law
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
United States marshal for the district of Maine 1838-1841
United States attorney for the district of Maine 1843-1845
United States consul at Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, from 1845-1849
died in Bay Ridge, Kings County, N.Y., November 23, 1877
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

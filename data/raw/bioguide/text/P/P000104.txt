PATMAN, William Neff, (son of John William Wright Patman), a Representative from Texas
born in Texarkana, Bowie County, Tex., March 26, 1927
attended the public schools of Texarkana and Washington, D.C.
graduated from Kemper Military School, Boonville, Mo., 1944
B.B.A., University of Texas, Austin, 1953, and LL.B., 1953
served in the United States Marine Corps, private first class, 1945-1946, and United States Air Force Reserve, captain, 1953-1966
diplomatic courier, United States Foreign Service, 1949-1950
admitted to the Texas bar in 1953 and was legal examiner, Texas Railroad Commission, 1953-1955
commenced the private practice of law in 1955
city attorney, Ganado, 1955-1960
served in the Texas senate, 1961-1980
delegate, Texas State Democratic conventions, 1960-1978
elected as a Democrat to the Ninety-seventh and Ninety-eighth Congresses (January 3, 1981-January 3, 1985)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Ninety-ninth Congress
died on December 9, 2008, in Houston, Tex.

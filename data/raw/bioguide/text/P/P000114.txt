PATTERSON, Francis Ford, Jr., a Representative from New Jersey
born in Newark, N.J., July 30, 1867
moved with his parents to Woodbury, N.J., in 1874
attended the public schools
employed in a newspaper office at the age of thirteen
moved to Camden, N.J., in 1882
connected with the Camden Courier 1883-1890
New Jersey editor of the Philadelphia Record 1890-1894
owner and publisher of the Camden Post-Telegram 1894-1923
president of the West Jersey Trust Co. 1916-1925
director of the West Jersey Title Co. 1920-1925
member of the State house of assembly in 1900
county clerk of Camden County 1900-1920
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1920
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of William J. Browning
reelected to the Sixty-seventh, Sixty-eighth, and Sixty-ninth Congresses and served from November 2, 1920, to March 3, 1927
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1926
engaged in banking, serving as president of the West Jersey Parkside Trust Co., of Camden, N.J., until his death in Merchantville, N.J., on November 30, 1935
interment in Colestown Cemetery, Cherry Hill, N.J.

PAYNE, William Winter, a Representative from Alabama
born at Granville, near Warrenton, Fauquier County, Va., January 2, 1807
completed preparatory studies
studied law but never practiced
moved to Franklin County, Ala., in 1825 and engaged in planting
member of the State house of representatives in 1831
moved to Sumter County, Ala.
again a member of the State house of representatives 1834-1838 and in 1840
unsuccessful candidate for the State senate in 1839
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-seventh, Twenty-eighth, and Twenty-ninth Congresses (March 4, 1841-March 3, 1847)
chairman, Committee on Elections (Twenty-eighth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1846 to the Thirtieth Congress
returned to Virginia in 1847 and engaged in planting near Warrenton
chairman of the Democratic State convention in 1859
died in Warrenton, Va., September 2, 1874
interment in the City Cemetery.

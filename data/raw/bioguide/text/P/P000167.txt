PEARSON, John James, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Darby, Delaware County, Pa., October 25, 1800
moved with his parents to Mercer, Pa., in 1805
attended private schools and a grammar boarding school
studied law
was admitted to the bar in August 1822 and commenced practice in Mercer County
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of John Banks and served from December 5, 1836, to March 3, 1837
was not a candidate for renomination in 1836
resumed the practice of law
member of state senate 1838-1842
appointed president judge of Dauphin and Lebanon Counties, Pa., April 7, 1849, and served until January 1, 1882
died in Harrisburg, Pa., May 30, 1888
interment in Mount Kalmia Cemetery.

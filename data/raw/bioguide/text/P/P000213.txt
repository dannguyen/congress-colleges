PENNINGTON, Alexander Cumming McWhorter, (cousin of William Pennington), a Representative from New Jersey
born in Newark, N.J., July 2, 1810
completed preparatory studies
attended the United States Military Academy, West Point, N.Y., 1826-1828
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1833 and commenced practice in Newark
member of the state general assembly in 1837 and 1838
alderman of Newark 1837-1840
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-third Congress and reelected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1857)
chairman, Committee on Foreign Affairs (Thirty-fourth Congress)
moved to New York City, where he died January 25, 1867
interment in Mount Pleasant Cemetery, Newark, N.J.

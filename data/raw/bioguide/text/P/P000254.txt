PETERS, Mason Summers, a Representative from Kansas
born near Kearney, Clay County, Mo., September 3, 1844
attended the William Jewell College, Liberty, Mo.
taught in the grammar schools of Clay County, Mo., 1867-1870
clerk of the court of Clinton County, Mo., 1870-1874
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1875 and commenced practice in Plattsburg, Mo.
moved to Wyandotte County, Kans., in 1886
organized the Union Live Stock Commission Co. in 1895
elected as a Populist to the Fifty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1897-March 3, 1899)
was unsuccessful for reelection in 1898 to the Fifty-sixth Congress
resumed his business and professional pursuits in Kansas City, Kans.
died in Kansas City, Mo., February 14, 1914
interment in Forest Hill Cemetery.

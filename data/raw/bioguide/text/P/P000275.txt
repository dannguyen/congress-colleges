PETTIS, Spencer Darwin, a Representative from Missouri
born in Culpeper County, Va., in 1802
completed preparatory studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar about 1824 and commenced practice in Fayette, Howard County, Mo.
held various local offices
appointed secretary of state on July 22, 1826, and served until December 31, 1828, when he resigned
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first and Twenty-second Congresses and served from March 4, 1829, until his death
during the campaign of 1830 his feeling regarding the United States bank issue precipitated a quarrel and subsequently a duel with Maj. Thomas Biddle, in which both fell mortally wounded
Mr. Pettis died the next day, August 28, 1831, in St. Louis
interment in the Old City Cemetery, St. Louis, Mo.

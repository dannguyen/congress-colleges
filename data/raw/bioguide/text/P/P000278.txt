PETTIT, John Upfold, a Representative from Indiana
born in Fabius, Onondaga County, N.Y., September 11, 1820
received an academic education
attended Hamilton College, Clinton, N.Y., and was graduated from Union College, Schenectady, N.Y., in 1839
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1841 and commenced practice in Wabash, Wabash County, Ind.
American consul to Maranham, Brazil, 1850-1853
elected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress and reelected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth, and Thirty-sixth Congresses (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1861)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Post Office Department (Thirty-fourth Congress)
member of the state house of representatives in 1865 and served as speaker
judge of the twenty-seventh judicial district of Indiana 1872-1880
died in Wabash, Ind., March 21, 1881
interment in Falls Cemetery.

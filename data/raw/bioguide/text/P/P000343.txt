PIERSON, Job, a Representative from New York
born in East Hampton, Suffolk County, N.Y., September 23, 1791
attended the common schools
was graduated from Williams College in 1811
studied law in Salem and Schaghticoke
was admitted to the bar in 1815 and commenced practice in Rensselaer County
district attorney 1824-1833
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-second and Twenty-third Congresses (March 4, 1831-March 3, 1835)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1834 to the Twenty-fourth Congress
resumed the practice of law
surrogate of Rensselaer County 1835-1840
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1848, 1852, and 1856
died in Troy, N.Y., April 9, 1860
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

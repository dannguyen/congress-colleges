PILE, William Anderson, a Representative from Missouri
born near Indianapolis, Ind., February 11, 1829
completed preparatory studies
studied theology and became a minister in the Methodist Episcopal Church and a member of the Missouri conference
during the Civil War entered the Union Army
commissioned chaplain of the First Regiment, Missouri Light Artillery, June 12, 1861
lieutenant colonel of the Thirty-third Regiment, Missouri Infantry, September 5, 1862
colonel December 23, 1862
brigadier general of Volunteers December 26, 1863
brevetted major general April 9, 1865
elected as a Republican to the Fortieth Congress (March 4, 1867-March 3, 1869)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Post Office Department (Fortieth Congress)
unsuccessful for reelection in 1868 to the Forty-first Congress
Governor of New Mexico in 1869 and 1870
Minister Resident to Venezuela from 1871 to 1874, when he resigned
died in Monrovia, Calif., July 7, 1889
interment in Live Oak Cemetery.

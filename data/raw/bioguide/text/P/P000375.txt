PLANT, David, a Representative from Connecticut
born in Stratford, Conn., March 29, 1783
attended the Episcopal Academy, Cheshire, Conn.
was graduated from Yale College in 1804
studied law in the Litchfield (Conn.) Law School
was admitted to the bar in 1804 and commenced practice in Stratford
judge of the probate court of Fairfield County
member of the state house of representatives 1817-1820 and served as speaker
served in the state senate in 1821 and 1822
lieutenant governor of Connecticut 1823-1827
elected as an Adams candidate to the Twentieth Congress (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1829)
did not seek renomination in 1828
resumed the practice of law
died in Stratford, Conn., October 18, 1851
interment in the Congregational Burying Ground.

POFF, Richard Harding, a Representative from Virginia
born in Radford, Montgomery County, Va., October 19, 1923
attended the Christiansburg, Va., public schools
undergraduate work at Roanoke College, Salem, Va.
during the Second World War, served as a bomber pilot with the Eighth Air Force in England
flew thirty-five successful missions over Europe
awarded the Distinguished Flying Cross
was inactivated from the service as a first lieutenant serving from February 1943 to August 1945
University of Virginia at Charlottesville, LL.B., 1948
was admitted to the bar in June 1947, and commenced practice in Radford, Va., in 1948
delegate Republican National Convention, 1968
elected as a Republican to the Eighty-third Congress
reelected to the nine succeeding Congresses and served from January 3, 1953, until his resignation August 29, 1972, to become a justice of the Supreme Court of Virginia
died on June 28, 2011, in Tullahoma, Tenn.

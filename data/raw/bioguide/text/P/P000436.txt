PORTER, Alexander, a Senator from Louisiana
born in County Donegal, Ireland, June 24, 1785
immigrated to the United States in 1801 with an uncle, who settled in Nashville, Tenn.
received a limited schooling
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1807 and commenced practice in the Attakapas region of the Territory of Orleans
delegate to the convention which framed the first State constitution in 1812
member, lower branch of the Louisiana State legislature 1816-1818
judge of the State supreme court 1821-1833
elected as a Whig to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Josiah S. Johnson and served from December 19, 1833, until January 5, 1837, when he resigned due to ill health
continued the practice of law in Attakapas
planter
again elected to the United States Senate for the term beginning March 4, 1843, but did not take his seat due to ill health
died in Attakapas, La., January 13, 1844
interment on Oakland plantation in Franklin, La.

PORTER, Augustus Seymour, (nephew of Peter Buell Porter), a Senator from Michigan
born in Canandaigua, N.Y., January 18, 1798
attended Canandaigua Academy, Canandaigua, N.Y.
graduated from Union College, Schenectady, N.Y., in 1818
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Detroit, Mich.
recorder of Detroit 1830
mayor of Detroit 1838
elected as a Whig to the United States Senate on January 20, 1840, for the term beginning March 4, 1839, and served until March 3, 1845
was not a candidate for renomination
chairman, Committee on Roads and Canals (Twenty-seventh and Twenty-eighth Congresses), Committee on Enrolled Bills (Twenty-seventh Congress)
moved to his fatherÂ’s residence in Niagara Falls, N.Y., in 1848
died at Niagara Falls, N.Y., September 18, 1872
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

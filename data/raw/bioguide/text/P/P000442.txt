PORTER, James, a Representative from New York
born in Williamstown, Mass., April 18, 1787
was graduated from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass., in 1810
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Skaneateles, N.Y.
member of the State assembly in 1814 and 1815
elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress (March 4, 1817-March 3, 1819)
was not a candidate for renomination
resumed the practice of law
surrogate of Onondaga County 1822-1824
moved to Albany, N.Y., and served as register of the court of chancery until his death there February 7, 1839
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

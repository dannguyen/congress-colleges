PORTER, Peter Augustus, (grandson of Peter Buell Porter), a Representative from New York
born at Niagara Falls, N.Y., October 10, 1853
taught by private teachers
attended St. PaulÂ’s Schools, Concord, N.H., 1865-1871 and was graduated from Yale College in 1874
engaged in banking and was an extensive landowner
president of the village of Niagara Falls, N.Y., in 1878
member of the State assembly in 1886 and 1887
elected as an Independent Republican to the Sixtieth Congress (March 4, 1907-March 3, 1909)
declined to be a candidate for renomination
engaged in the study and writing of history of the Niagara frontier
died in Buffalo, N.Y., December 15, 1925
interment in Oakwood Cemetery, Niagara Falls, N.Y.

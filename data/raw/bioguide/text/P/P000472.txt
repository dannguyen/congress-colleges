POTTS, David, Jr., a Representative from Pennsylvania
born at Warwick Furnace, about eight miles from Pottstown, Chester County, Pa., November 27, 1794
completed preparatory studies in Pottstown
became an ironmaster
owner and manager of Warwick Furnace
member of the State house of representatives 1824-1826
elected as an Anti-Masonic candidate to the Twenty-second and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1831-March 3, 1839)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1838
resumed his former business pursuits
died at Warwick Furnace (now Warwick), Chester County, Pa., June 1, 1863
interment in Coventry Cemetery, near Warwick.

PRATT, Charles Clarence, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in New Milford, Susquehanna County, Pa., April 23, 1854
attended the rural schools in his community, Sedgwick Institute, Great Barrington, Mass., and was graduated from the State normal school at Bloomsburg, Pa.
became engaged in the lumber and oil businesses at New Milford in 1879
served as assessor, school director, and justice of the peace
colonel on the respective staffs of Governors Stone, Pennypacker, and Tener 1899-1907
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-first Congress (March 4, 1909-March 3, 1911)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1910 to the Sixty-second Congress
resumed his former business pursuits, residing in Binghamton, N.Y., during the winters and in New Milford, Pa., during the summers
died in Binghamton, N.Y., January 27, 1916
interment in New Milford Cemetery, New Milford, Pa.

PRINGEY, Joseph Colburn, a Representative from Oklahoma
born in Somerset, Somerset County, Pa., May 22, 1858
attended the common schools
moved to Missouri in 1870
attended a business college in Sedalia, Mo.
moved to Chandler, Lincoln County, Okla., in 1891
engaged in agricultural pursuits and in the loan and insurance business
member of the Territorial senate in 1893
member of the board of regents of the University of Oklahoma at Norman in 1893 and 1894
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1900
county clerk of Lincoln County, Okla., 1912-1920
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1921-March 3, 1923)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1922 to the Sixty-eighth Congress
acting postmaster of Chandler, Okla., in 1923 and 1924
resumed agricultural pursuits
died in Chandler, Okla., on February 11, 1935
interment in Oak Park Cemetery.

PUGH, Samuel Johnson, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Greenup County, Ky., January 28, 1850
moved with his parents to Lewis County in 1852
attended Chandlers Select School, Rands Academy, and Centre College, Danville, Ky.
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Vanceburg, Lewis County, Ky.
city attorney in 1872 and 1873
master commissioner of the circuit court 1874-1880
county attorney 1878-1886
county judge 1886-1890
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1890 and 1891
member of the State senate in 1893 and 1894
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth, Fifty-fifth, and Fifty-sixth Congresses (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1901)
resumed the practice of law in Vanceburg, Ky., and died there April 17, 1922
interment in Greenlawn Cemetery.

PURVIANCE, Samuel Dinsmore, a Representative from North Carolina
born on Masonboro Sound at Castle Fin House, near Wilmington, New Hanover County, N.C., January 7, 1774
attended a private school
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced at Fayetteville, N.C.
also owned and operated a large plantation
member of the State house of commons in 1798 and 1799
member of the State senate from Cumberland County in 1801
trustee of Fayetteville Academy in 1803
elected as a Federalist to the Eighth Congress (March 4, 1803-March 3, 1805)
continued the practice of law in Fayetteville
died on the Red River about 1806, while on an exploring expedition into the West.

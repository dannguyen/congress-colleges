RAMSTAD, James (Jim), a Representative from Minnesota
born in Jamestown, Stutsman County, N.D., May 6, 1946
B.A., University of Minnesota, 1968
J.D., George Washington University Law School, Washington, D.C., 1973
United States Army Reserve, 1968-1975
staff for the speaker of the Minnesota state house of representatives, 1969-1970
staff, United States Representative Thomas S. Kleppe of North Dakota, 1970
adjunct professor, American University, Washington, D.C., 1974-1978
deputy campaign manager for United States Representative William E. Frenzel of Minnesota, 1978
member of the Minnesota state senate, 1981-1990
elected as a Republican to the One Hundred Second and to the eight succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1991-January 3, 2009)
was not a candidate for reelection to the One Hundred Eleventh Congress in 2008.

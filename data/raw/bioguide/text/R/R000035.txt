RANDALL, Benjamin, a Representative from Maine
born in Topsham, Maine (then a district of Massachusetts), November 14, 1789
pursued an academic course
was graduated from Bowdoin College, Brunswick, Maine, in 1809
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1812 and commenced practice in Bath, Maine
served in the State militia in Colonel ReedÂ’s regiment stationed at Coxes Head in September 1814
member of the Maine senate in 1833, 1835, and 1838
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1843)
resumed the practice of law
appointed collector of customs for the port of Bath in 1849 and served until his death in Bath, Maine, October 11, 1859
interment in Maple Grove Cemetery.

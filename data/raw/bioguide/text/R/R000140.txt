REGAN, Kenneth Mills, a Representative from Texas
born in Mount Morris, Ogle County, Ill., March 6, 1893
attended the public schools and Vincennes (Ind.) University
during the First World War served as a flyer in the United States Army Signal Corps
in 1920 engaged in the real estate business and as an oil operator in Pecos, Tex.
alderman of the city of Pecos
mayor of Pecos 1929-1932
member of the State senate 1933-1937
during the Second World War served as an intelligence officer in the Air Corps and was discharged with the rank of captain
moved to Midland, Tex., and continued oil operations
elected as a Democrat to the Eightieth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Robert Ewing Thomason
reelected to the Eighty-first, Eighty-second, and Eighty-third Congresses, and served from August 23, 1947, to January 3, 1955
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1954 to the Eighty-fourth Congress
representative of Texas railroads in Washington, D.C.
died in Santa Fe, N.Mex., on August 15, 1959
interment in Resthaven Memorial Park, Midland, Tex.

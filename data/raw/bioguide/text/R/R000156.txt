REILLY, Thomas Lawrence, a Representative from Connecticut
was born in New Britain, Hartford County, Conn., September 20, 1858
attended the common schools and was graduated from the Connecticut State Normal School in 1876
assistant town clerk of New Britain in 1876
moved with his parents to Meriden, Conn., in 1877
studied law for a year
employed as a bookkeeper for several years
engaged as a newspaper correspondent until 1886
one of the founders of the Meriden Journal in 1886 and became the city editor
member of the Meriden Board of Education 1896-1903
chairman of the town committee in 1900
mayor of Meriden 1906-1912
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-second and Sixty-third Congresses (March 4, 1911-March 3, 1915)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914 to the Sixty-fourth Congress
employed in the Internal Revenue Service in 1916 and 1917
elected sheriff of New Haven County in 1918
reelected and served until his death in New Haven, Conn., July 6, 1924
interment in Sacred Heart Cemetery, Meriden, Conn.

REYBURN, William Stuart, (son of John Edgar Reyburn), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., December 17, 1882
attended the Hill School, Pottstown, Pa.
was graduated from Yale University in 1904 and from the law department of Georgetown University, Washington, D.C.
was admitted to the bar in 1908 and commenced practice in Washington, D.C.
member of President Tafts party which visited the Philippines, Japan, and China in 1905
served in the State house of representatives from 1909 until May 25, 1911, when he resigned
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-second Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Joel Cook and served from May 23, 1911, to March 3, 1913
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1912
resumed the practice of his profession in Washington, D.C., and subsequently retired from active business pursuits
resided in Aiken, Aiken County, S.C., and later moved to his estate Black Hill, Old Lyme, Conn.
died in New Haven, Conn., on July 25, 1946
interment in Laurel Hill Cemetery, Philadelphia, Pa.

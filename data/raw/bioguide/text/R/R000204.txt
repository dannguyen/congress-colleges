RICE, Thomas, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Pownalborough (now Wiscasset), Maine (then a part of Massachusetts), March 30, 1768
was graduated from Harvard University in 1791
studied law
was admitted to the bar in Suffolk County, Mass., in 1794 and commenced practice in Winslow, Maine, the following year
appointed in 1807 by the supreme judicial court of Maine one of the examiners of counselors and attorneys for Kennebec County
member of the State house of representatives in 1814
elected as a Federalist to the Fourteenth and Fifteenth Congresses (March 4, 1815-March 3, 1819)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1818 to the Sixteenth Congress
resumed the practice of law
died in Winslow, Kennebec County, Maine, August 25, 1854
interment in Pine Grove Cemetery, Waterville, Maine.

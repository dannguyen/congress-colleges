RICHARDS, Jacob, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Chester, Delaware County, Pa., in 1773
was graduated from the University of Pennsylvania at Philadelphia in 1794
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1795 and commenced practice in Philadelphia
elected as a Republican to the Eighth, Ninth, and Tenth Congresses (March 4, 1803-March 3, 1809)
was commissioned as colonel of militia in Delaware County, Pa.
engaged in the practice of law until his death near Chester, Pa., July 20, 1816.

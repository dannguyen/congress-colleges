RICHARDSON, John Peter, a Representative from South Carolina
born at Hickory Hill, S.C., April 14, 1801
was graduated from South Carolina College at Columbia in 1819
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Fulton, S.C.
member of the State house of representatives, 1825-1833
judge of the circuit court
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Richard I. Manning
reelected as a Democrat to the Twenty-fifth Congress and served from December 19, 1836, to March 3, 1839
Governor of South Carolina 1840-1842
died in Fulton (later Pinewood), Sumter County, S.C., January 24, 1864
interment in Richardson Cemetery, Clarendon County, S.C.

RIKER, Samuel, a Representative from New York
born in Newtown, Long Island, N.Y., April 8, 1743
attended the common schools
member of the Newtown committee of correspondence in 1774
was supervisor of Suffolk County in 1783
lieutenant of Light Horse in the Revolution
member of the State assembly in 1784
elected as a Republican to the Eighth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of John Smith and served from November 5, 1804, to March 3, 1805
elected to the Tenth Congress (March 4, 1807-March 3, 1809)
died in Newtown, Long Island, N.Y., May 19, 1823
interment in the Dutch Reformed Cemetery.

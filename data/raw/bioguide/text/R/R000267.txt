RIPLEY, Thomas C., a Representative from New York
born in Schaghticoke, Rensselaer County, N.Y, on January 2, 1807
graduated, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, 1828
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Harts Falls, N.Y.
farmer
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-ninth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of United States Representative Richard P. Herrick (December 7, 1846-March 3, 1847)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1846
relocated to Saginaw, Mich. in 1854
member of the Michigan House of Representatives (1873-1874)
died on February 12, 1897, in Saginaw, Mich.
interment in Oakwood Cemetery, Saginaw, Mich.

ROBBINS, Gaston Ahi, a Representative from Alabama
born in Goldsboro, Wayne County, N.C., September 26, 1858
moved to Randolph County, N.C.
attended Trinity College at Durham and was graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1879
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1880 and commenced practice in Selma, Ala.
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-third Congress (March 4, 1893-March 3, 1895)
presented credentials as a Member-elect to the Fifty-fourth Congress and served from March 4, 1895, to March 13, 1896, when he was succeeded by William F. Aldrich, who contested his election
presented credentials to the Fifty-sixth Congress and served from March 4, 1899, to March 8, 1900, when he was again succeeded by William F. Aldrich, who contested his election
resumed the practice of law in New York City, where he died on February 22, 1902
interment in Oakwood Cemetery, Statesville, N.C.

ROBBINS, George Robbins, a Representative from New Jersey
born near Allentown, Monmouth County, N.J., September 24, 1808
received a good literary education
was graduated from the Jefferson Medical College at Philadelphia in 1837 and commenced the practice of medicine in Falsington, Bucks County, Pa.
moved to Hamilton Square, N.J., the same year and continued the practice of medicine
elected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress and reelected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
was not a candidate for renomination
resumed the practice of his profession
died in Hamilton Square, N.J., February 22, 1875
interment in the Presbyterian Church Cemetery.

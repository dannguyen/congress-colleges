ROBERTS, Anthony Ellmaker, (grandfather of Robert Grey Bushong), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Barneston Station, Chester County, Pa., on October 29, 1803
received a limited schooling
engaged in mercantile pursuits in New Holland, Lancaster County, Pa., 1816-1839
moved to Lancaster, Pa., in 1839
sheriff of Lancaster County 1839-1842
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1842 to the Twenty-eighth Congress
was appointed United States marshall for the eastern district of Pennsylvania on May 16, 1850, and served until March 29, 1853
elected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress and reelected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1859)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1858
was active in organization of the Republican Party in Pennsylvania
engaged in operating his real estate holdings in Lancaster and was executor for various estates
died in Lancaster, Pa., on January 23, 1885
interment in the Lancaster Cemetery.

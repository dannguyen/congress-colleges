RODGERS, Robert Lewis, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in El Dorado, Butler County, Kans., June 2, 1875
raised on a farm near Jamestown, Mercer County, Pa.
attended district school and Fredonia (Pa.) Institute
during the War with Spain enlisted in Company K, Fifteenth Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry
taught in the district schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
moved to Erie, Erie County, Pa., in 1914 and engaged in the insurance, real estate, and mortgage business
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1939-January 3, 1947)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1946 to the Eightieth Congress
resided in Erie, Pa., until his death there May 9, 1960
interment in Rocky Glen Cemetery, Adamsville, Pa.

RODMAN, William, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Bensalem Township, near Bristol, Bucks County, Pa., October 7, 1757
completed preparatory studies
served in the Revolutionary War as a private and subsequently as brigade quartermaster
commanded a company during the Whisky Rebellion in 1794
justice of the peace 1791-1800
member of the State senate 1804-1808
elected as a Republican to the Twelfth Congress (March 4, 1811-March 3, 1813)
died at Flushing near Bristol, Bucks County, Pa., July 27, 1824
interment in the Episcopal Cemetery (later known as the St. James Burying Ground).

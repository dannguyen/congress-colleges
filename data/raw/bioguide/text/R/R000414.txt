ROMAN, James Dixon, a Representative from Maryland
born in Chester County, Pa., August 11, 1809
attended the common schools and a private school at West Nottingham (now Nottingham)
moved to Cecil County, Md.
studied law in Frederick, Md.
was admitted to the bar in 1834 and commenced practice in Hagerstown, Md.
member of the State senate in 1847
elected as a Whig to the Thirtieth Congress (March 4, 1847-March 3, 1849)
presidential elector on the Whig ticket in 1848 and on the Democratic ticket in 1856
again resumed the practice of law in Hagerstown
president of the Old Hagerstown Bank from 1851 until his death
member of the peace convention held in Washington, D.C., in 1861 in an effort to devise means to prevent the impending war
died near Hagerstown, Washington County, Md., January 19, 1867
interment in Rose Hill Cemetery.

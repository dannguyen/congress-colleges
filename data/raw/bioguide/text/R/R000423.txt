ROONEY, Frederick Bernard, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Bethlehem, Northampton County, Pa., November 6, 1925
attended the public schools
graduated from the Bethlehem High School in 1944
served in the United States Army from February 1944 to April 1946, with service in Europe as a paratrooper
B.A., University of Georgia at Athens, 1950
engaged in the real estate and insurance business in 1950
elected to two terms in the State senate and served from November 5, 1958, until his resignation August 6, 1963
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-eighth Congress, by special election, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of United States Representative Francis E. Walter, and reelected to the seven succeeding Congresses (July 30, 1963-January 3, 1979)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Ninety-sixth Congress in 1978
established a consulting business
is a resident of Washington, D.C.

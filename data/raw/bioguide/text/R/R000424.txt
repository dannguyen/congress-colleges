ROONEY, John James, a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y., November 29, 1903
attended the parochial schools and St. Francis Preparatory School and College
the law department of Fordham University, New York, N.Y., LL.B., 1925
was admitted to the bar in 1926 and commenced practice in Brooklyn, N.Y.
served as assistant district attorney in Brooklyn 1940-1944
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-eighth Congress, by special election June 6, 1944, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Thomas H. Cullen
reelected to the fifteen succeeding Congresses and served from June 6, 1944, until his resignation on December 31, 1974
was not a candidate for reelection in 1974 to the Ninety-fourth Congress
resided in Washington, D.C., until his death there on October 26, 1975
interment in Holy Cross Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

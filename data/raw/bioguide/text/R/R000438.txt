ROSE, Robert Lawson, (son of Robert Selden Rose and son-in-law of Nathaniel Allen), a Representative from New York
born in Geneva, N.Y., October 12, 1804
received a limited schooling
moved to Allens Hill, N.Y., and engaged in agricultural pursuits
held several local offices
elected as a Whig to the Thirtieth and Thirty-first Congresses (March 4, 1847-March 3, 1851)
resumed agricultural pursuits
returned to Geneva, Ontario County, N.Y.
subsequently moved to Pleasant Grove, near Funkstown, Washington County, Md., in 1868 and engaged in the manufacture of paper until his death there March 14, 1877
interment in Rose Hill Cemetery, Hagerstown, Washington County, Md.

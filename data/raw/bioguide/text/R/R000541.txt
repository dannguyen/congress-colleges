RUSSELL, William Augustus, a Representative from Massachusetts
born at Wells River, Orange County, Vt., April 22, 1831
pursued an academic course in Franklin, N.H.
engaged in the manufacture of paper in Exeter, N.H., in 1852
moved to Lawrence, Mass., in 1852, where he continued in that business
member of the State house of representatives in 1869
delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1868 and 1876
elected as a Republican to the Forty-sixth, Forty-seventh, and Forty-eighth Congresses (March 4, 1879-March 3, 1885)
after leaving Congress devoted his time to the manufacture of paper
died in Boston, Mass., January 10, 1899
interment in Bellevue Cemetery, Lawrence, Essex County, Mass.

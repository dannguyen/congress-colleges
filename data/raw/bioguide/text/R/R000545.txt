RUTH, Earl Baker, a Representative from North Carolina
born in Spencer, Rowan County, N.C., February 7, 1916
graduated from Central High School, Charlotte, N.C., 1934
A.B., University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, N.C., 1938
M.A., University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, N.C., 1942
Ph.D., University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, N.C., 1955
teacher, Chapel Hill, N.C., and Charlotte, N.C., 1938-1940
United States Navy
member of Salisbury, N.C., city council, 1963-1968
mayor pro tempore of Salisbury, N.C., 1967-1968
elected as a Republican to the Ninety-first and to the two succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1969-January 3, 1975)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Ninety-fourth Congress in 1974
Governor of American Samoa, 1975-1976
died on August 15, 1989, in Salisbury, N.C.
interment in National Cemetery, Salisbury, N.C.

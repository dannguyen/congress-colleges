SAULSBURY, Willard, Sr., (brother of Eli Saulsbury, father of Willard Saulsbury, Jr.), a Senator from Delaware
born in Mispillion Hundred, Kent County, Del., June 2, 1820
attended the common schools, Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa., and Delaware College (now the University of Delaware), Newark, Del.
studied law
admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Georgetown, Del.
attorney general of Delaware 1850-1855
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate in 1858
reelected in 1864 and served from March 4, 1859, to March 3, 1871
chairman, Committee on Enrolled Bills (Thirty-sixth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection
resumed the practice of law
chancellor of the State from 1874 until his death in Dover, Del., April 6, 1892
interment in Christ Episcopal Churchyard.

SCHNEIDER, George John, a Representative from Wisconsin
born in the town of Grand Chute, Outagamie County, Wis., October 30, 1877
moved to Appleton with his parents, attended the public schools of Appleton, Wis.
learned the trade of paper making
vice president of the International Brotherhood of Paper Makers 1909-1927
member of the executive board of the Wisconsin State Federation of Labor 1921-1928
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-eighth through Seventy-second Congresses (March 4, 1923-March 3, 1933)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1932 to the Seventy-third Congress
elected as a Progressive to the Seventy-fourth and Seventy-fifth Congresses (January 3, 1935-January 3, 1939)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1938 to the Seventy-sixth Congress
resumed labor activities and died in Toledo, Ohio, March 12, 1939, while attending a labor meeting
interment in Riverside Cemetery, Appleton, Wis.

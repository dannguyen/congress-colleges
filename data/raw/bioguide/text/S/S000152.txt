SCHUYLER, Karl Cortlandt, a Senator from Colorado
born in Colorado Springs, Colo., April 3, 1877
attended the Colorado Springs public schools
worked on the Colorado Midland Railroad
graduated from the law school of the University of Denver in 1898
admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice in Colorado Springs
in 1905 moved to Denver, Colo., where he continued the practice of law
trustee of the University of Denver and of Colorado WomanÂ’s College at Denver, Colo.
unsuccessful candidate for the Republican nomination for United States Senator in 1920
elected on November 8, 1932, as a Republican to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Charles W. Waterman and served from December 7, 1932, to March 3, 1933
at the same election was an unsuccessful candidate for the full term beginning March 4, 1933
resumed the practice of law in Denver
was struck by an automobile and killed in New York City, July 31, 1933
interment in Fairmount Cemetery, Denver, Colo.

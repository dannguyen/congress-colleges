SCHUYLER, Philip John, (father of Philip Jeremiah Schuyler), a Delegate and a Senator from New York
born in Albany, N.Y., November 20, 1733
attended the common schools of Albany and studied under a private tutor in New Rochelle, N.Y.
served in the British Army and was commissioned captain in 1755
appointed chief commissary in 1756
resigned from the British Army in 1757
rejoined in 1758 as a major
sent to England to settle colonial claims in 1758
returned in 1763 and engaged in the lumber business in Saratoga, N.Y.
built the first flax mill in America
member, New York assembly 1768
Member of the Continental Congress 1775, 1777, and 1779-1780
appointed one of the four major generals in the Continental Army in 1775 and resigned in 1779
member, New York State senate 1780-1784, 1786-1790
elected to the United States Senate and served from July 16, 1789, to March 3, 1791
unsuccessful candidate for reelection
member, State senate 1792-1797
elected to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1797, to January 3, 1798, when he resigned because of ill health
died in Albany, N.Y., November 18, 1804
interment in Albany Rural Cemetery.

SCOTT, Ralph James, a Representative from North Carolina
born in Surry County, near Pinnacle, N.C., October 15, 1905
educated in the public schools
Wake Forest College, LL.B., 1930
was admitted to the bar in 1930 and commenced the practice of law in Danbury, N.C.
member of the State house of representatives, 1936-1937
delegate to State Democratic conventions, 1936-1968
chairman, Democratic Executive Committee of Stokes County, 1936-1970
solicitor of the twenty-first judicial district of North Carolina 1938-1956
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-fifth and to the four succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1957-January 3, 1967)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1966 to the Ninetieth Congress
resumed the practice of law
was a resident of Danbury, N.C., until his death there August 5, 1983
interment in Pannacle Baptist Church cemetery.

SEAMAN, Henry John, a Representative from New York
born in Marshland (now Greenridge), Staten Island, N.Y., April 16, 1805
engaged in agricultural pursuits
promoter of Richmond village in 1836
elected as the candidate of the American Party to the Twenty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1845-March 3, 1847)
director of the Staten Island Railroad in 1851
secretary of the Plank Road Co. in 1856
constructed the bridge over Fresh Kills
died on Staten Island, N.Y., May 3, 1861
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery, New York City.

SENTER, William Tandy, a Representative from Tennessee
born at Bean Station, Grainger County, Tenn., May 12, 1801
attended the common schools
held several local offices
engaged in agricultural pursuits
minister in the Methodist Episcopal Church South, Holston Conference
member of the State constitutional convention which met at Nashville from May 19 to August 30, 1834
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1845)
resumed agricultural pursuits and ministerial work
died at Panther Springs, Hamblen County, Tenn., August 28, 1848
interment in Senter Memorial Church Cemetery.

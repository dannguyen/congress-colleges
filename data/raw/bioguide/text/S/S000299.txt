SHARTEL, Cassius McLean, a Representative from Missouri
born in Crawford County, Pa., April 27, 1860
moved with his parents to Knox County, Mo., and resided there until 1873
moved with his parents to Chautauqua County, Kans.
attended the common schools and Kansas State Agricultural College at Manhattan
taught school
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1881 and commenced practice in Sedan, Kans.
moved to Nevada, Mo., in 1887 and then to Neosho, Newton County, Mo., the same year and continued the practice of law
delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1900 and 1936
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1905-March 3, 1907)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1906
interested in farm loans
president of the Missouri constitutional convention in 1922 and 1923
died in Neosho, Mo., September 27, 1943
interment in Odd Fellows Cemetery.

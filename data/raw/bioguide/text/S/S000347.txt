SHERMAN, Judson W., a Representative from New York
born in that State in 1808
completed preparatory studies
held several local offices in Angelica, N.Y., where he resided
clerk of Allegany County, N.Y., 1831-1837
deputy treasurer of the State of New York about 1850
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
appointed captain and commissary of subsistence of Volunteers on September 7, 1861, was assigned to duty with Brigadier General WoodÂ’s brigade, and resigned his commission November 9, 1861
died at Angelica, Allegany County, N.Y., on November 12, 1881
interment in Until the Day Dawn Cemetery.

SHIELDS, James, (uncle of James Shields [1810-1879]), a Representative from Ohio
born in Banbridge, County Down, Ireland, April 13, 1762
received a good common-school education
entered the University of Glasgow, Scotland, in 1782 and was graduated in 1786
attended medical college for two years
immigrated to the United States in July 1791 and settled in Frederick County, Va., where he taught school
moved to Butler County, Ohio, in 1801
returned to Virginia and became a citizen of the United States in 1804
returned to Ohio in 1807
member of the State house of representatives 1806-1827
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-first Congress (March 4, 1829-March 3, 1831)
was killed through the accidental overturning of a stagecoach near Venice, Butler County, Ohio, August 13, 1831
interment in Venice Cemetery, Venice, Ohio.

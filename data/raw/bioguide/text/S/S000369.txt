SHIPSTEAD, Henrik, a Senator from Minnesota
born in Burbank, Kandiyohi County, Minn., January 8, 1881
attended the public schools at New London, Minn., and the State normal school at St. Cloud, Minn.
graduated from the dental department of Northwestern University, Chicago, Ill., in 1903 and practiced dentistry in Glenwood, Minn., 1904-1920
mayor of Glenwood 1911-1913
member, State house of representatives 1917
moved to Minneapolis in 1920 and resumed the practice of dentistry
unsuccessful candidate for the United States Congress in 1918 and for governor in 1920
elected on the Farmer-Labor ticket in 1922 to the United States Senate
reelected in 1928, 1934, and as a Republican in 1940 and served from March 4, 1923, to January 3, 1947
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1946
chairman, Committee on Printing (Seventieth through Seventy-second Congresses)
died in Alexandria, Minn., June 26, 1960
interment in Kinkead Cemetery.

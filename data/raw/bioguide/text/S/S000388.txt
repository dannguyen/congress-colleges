SHRIVER, Garner E., a Representative from Kansas
born in Towanda, Butler County, Kans., July 6, 1912
attended the public schools of Towanda and Wichita
moved to Wichita, Sedgwick County, Kans., in 1925
University of Wichita, B.A., 1934 (postgraduate study at University of Southern California in 1936), and Washburn Law School, LL.B., 1940 and J.D., 1970
worked for a drug company in Wichita, 1934-1936
teacher at South Haven High School in 1936 and 1937
was admitted to the bar in Wichita in February 1940
served for three years in the United States Navy as an enlisted man and officer, 1943-1946
State representative, 1947-1951
State senator, 1953-1960
elected as a Republican to the Eighty-seventh and to the seven succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1961-January 3, 1977)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1976 to the Ninety-fifth Congress
minority staff director and legal counsel for the Senate VeteransÂ’ Affairs Committee, Washington, D.C., March 1977-1980, and general counsel, 1981-1982
resumed the practice of law
was a resident of Wichita, Kans., until his death there on March 1, 1998.

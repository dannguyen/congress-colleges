SIMMONS, George Abel, a Representative from New York
born in Lyme, N.H., September 8, 1791
attended the district school
was graduated from Dartmouth College, Hanover, N.H., in 1816
moved to Lansingburg, Rensselaer County, N.Y., and was principal of the local academy
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1825 and commenced practice in Keeseville, Essex County, N.Y.
member of the state assembly 1840-1842
member of the state constitutional convention in 1846
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-third Congress and reelected as an Opposition Party candidate to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1857)
chairman, Committee on the Judiciary (Thirty-fourth Congress)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1856
resumed the practice of his profession in Keeseville, N.Y., where he died October 27, 1857
interment in Evergreen Cemetery.

SIMON, Joseph, a Senator from Oregon
born in Bechtheim, Germany, February 7, 1851
immigrated to the United States with his parents, who settled in Portland, Oreg., in 1857
attended the public schools
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1872 and commenced practice in Portland, Oreg.
member of the city council 1877-1880
member, State senate 1880-1898, frequently serving as president
member of the National Republican Committee 1892-1896
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate on October 8, 1898, to fill the vacancy in the term commencing March 4, 1897, and served until March 3, 1903
was not a candidate for reelection
chairman, Committee on Irrigation and Reclamation of Arid Lands (Fifty-sixth and Fifty-seventh Congresses)
mayor of Portland 1909-1911
resumed the practice of law
died in Portland, February 14, 1935
interment in Beth Israel Cemetery.

SIMPSON, James, Jr., a Representative from Illinois
born in Chicago, Ill., January 7, 1905
attended St. PaulÂ’s School in Concord, N.H., 1919-1922, Westminster School, Salisbury, Conn., 1922-1925, and later a student at Harvard University
director of Marshall Field & Co., 1931-1960
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-third Congress (March 4, 1933-January 3, 1935)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1934
was admitted to the Illinois bar in 1939
owner and operator of farms near Wadsworth, Lake County, Ill., and Rapidan, Culpeper County, Va.
entered the United States Marine Corps in 1943 and served thirty-six months, with twenty-four months in the Pacific area, and was discharged as a captain
civilian aide to Secretary of the Army Robert Stevens in 1953 and 1954
died at his farm near Wadsworth, Ill., February 29, 1960
interment in Graceland Cemetery, Chicago, Ill.

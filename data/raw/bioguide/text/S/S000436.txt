SIMPSON, Richard Murray, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Huntingdon, Pa., August 30, 1900
attended the public schools
was graduated from the University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, Pa., in 1923 and from Georgetown Law School, Washington, D.C., in 1942
during the First World War served as a private in the Three Hundred and First Company, Tank Corps
engaged in the insurance business 1923-1937
served in the State house of representatives 1935-1937
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-fifth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Benjamin K. Focht
reelected to the Seventy-sixth and to the ten succeeding Congresses and served from May 11, 1937, until his death in Bethesda, Md., January 7, 1960
interment in Riverview Cemetery, Huntingdon, Pa.

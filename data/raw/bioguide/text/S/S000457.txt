SITES, Frank Crawford, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Shippensburg, Cumberland County, Pa., December 24, 1864
moved with his parents to Harrisburg, Pa., in 1875
attended the public schools
learned the trade of watchmaker and jeweler
and afterward engaged in that business at Harrisburg
director on the Harrisburg school board 1903-1912
appointed postmaster of Harrisburg June 3, 1913, and served until his successor was appointed June 20, 1922
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1923-March 3, 1925)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1924 to the Sixty-ninth Congress
returned to Harrisburg and engaged in the bond business
died in Harrisburg, Pa., May 23, 1935
interment in East Harrisburg Cemetery.

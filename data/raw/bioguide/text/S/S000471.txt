SKINNER, Thomson Joseph, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Colchester, Conn., May 24, 1752
completed preparatory studies
moved to Massachusetts
member of the Massachusetts house of representatives in 1781, 1785, 1789, 1800, and 1801
served in the State senate 1786-1788, 1790-1797, 1802, and 1803
delegate to the State ratification convention in 1788
judge of the court of common pleas 1788-1807
State treasurer in 1806 and 1807
elected as a Republican to the Fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Theodore Sedgwick
reelected to the Fifth Congress and served from January 27, 1797, to March 3, 1799
elected to the Eighth Congress and served from March 4, 1803, to August 10, 1804, when he resigned
died in Boston, Mass., on January 20, 1809.

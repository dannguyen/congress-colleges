SMILIE, John, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Ireland in 1741
immigrated to the United States and settled in Pennsylvania in 1760
attended the public schools
served in the Revolutionary War
moved to Fayette, Pa., in 1780
was a member of the state house of representatives 1784-1786
member of the state constitutional convention in 1790
served in the state senate from 1790 to 1793, when he resigned
elected as an Anti-Administration candidate to the Third Congress (March 4, 1793-March 3, 1795)
elected as a Republican to the Sixth and to the six succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1799, until his death
had been reelected to the Thirteenth Congress, but died in Washington, D.C., December 30, 1812, before the close of the Twelfth Congress
interment in the Congressional Cemetery.

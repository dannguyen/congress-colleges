SMITH, Bernard, a Representative from New Jersey
born in Morristown, N.J., July 5, 1776
completed preparatory studies
collector of customs in 1809 and 1810
postmaster of New Brunswick 1810-1819
elected as a Republican to the Sixteenth Congress (March 4, 1819-March 3, 1821)
did not seek renomination in 1820
appointed register of the land office at Little Rock, Ark., in 1821, and settled in that state
secretary to the governor of Arkansas 1825-1828
appointed by Governor Izard as subagent of the Quapaw Indians in 1825, and served until his death in Little Rock, Ark., July 16, 1835
interment in Mount Holly Cemetery.

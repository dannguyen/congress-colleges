SMITH, Delazon, a Senator from Oregon
born in New Berlin, Chenango County, N.Y., October 5, 1816
graduated from Oberlin College, Ohio, in 1837
studied law and was admitted to the bar
in 1838 established the New York Watchman in Rochester, N.Y., of which he was editor for two years
published and edited the True Jeffersonian and the Western Herald in Rochester in 1840, and in 1841 founded the Western Empire in Dayton, Ohio
special United States commissioner to Quito, Ecuador, 1842-1845
moved to the Territory of Iowa in 1846 and entered the ministry
moved to the Territory of Oregon in 1852
edited the Oregon Democrat
member, Territorial house of representatives 1854-1856
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1857
upon the admission of Oregon as a State into the Union was elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from February 14 to March 3, 1859
unsuccessful candidate for reelection
died in Portland, Oreg., on November 19, 1860
interment in the City Cemetery, Albany, Linn County, Oreg.

SMITH, Gerrit, a Representative from New York
born in Utica, N.Y., March 6, 1797
moved to Peterboro in 1806
attended an academy in Clinton, N.Y.
was graduated from Hamilton College, Clinton, N.Y., in 1818
studied law
engaged in the management of a large estate which he inherited
delegate to the State conventions in 1824 and 1828
unsuccessful Liberty Party candidate for governor in 1840
unsuccessful presidential candidate in 1848
was admitted to the bar in 1853 and commenced practice in Peterboro, N.Y.
elected as a Free-Soil candidate to the Thirty-third Congress and served from March 4, 1853, until August 7, 1854, when he resigned
resumed the practice of his profession, and was a publicist and philanthropist
he revived the Anti-Dramshop Party, but was a delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1872 and supported Grant
died in New York City December 28, 1874
interment in Peterboro Cemetery, Peterboro, Madison County, N.Y.

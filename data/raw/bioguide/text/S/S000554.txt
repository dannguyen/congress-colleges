SMITH, Howard Worth, a Representative from Virginia
born in Broad Run, Fauquier County Va., February 2, 1883
attended the public schools
was graduated from Bethel Military Academy, Warrenton, Va., in 1901 and from the law department of the University of Virginia at Charlottesville in 1903
was admitted to the bar in 1904 and commenced practice in Alexandria, Va.
assistant general counsel, Alien Property Custodian, in 1917 and 1918
served as Commonwealth attorney of Alexandria, Va., 1918-1922
judge of the corporation court of Alexandria 1922-1928
judge of the sixteenth judicial circuit of Virginia 1928-1930
also engaged in banking, farming, and dairying
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-second and to the seventeen succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1931-January 3, 1967)
chairman, Committee on Rules (Eighty-fourth through Eighty-ninth Congresses)
sponsor of the Smith Act of 1940
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1966 to the Ninetieth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Alexandria, Va., where he died October 3, 1976
interment in Georgetown Cemetery, Broad Run, Va.

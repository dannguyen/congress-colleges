SMITH, John Ambler, a Representative from Virginia
born at Village View, near Dinwiddie Court House, Dinwiddie County, Va., September 23, 1847
attended the rural school and was educated at David TurnerÂ’s high school at Richmond
was graduated from the law department of the Richmond (Va.) College
was admitted to the bar in 1867 and commenced the practice of law in Richmond, Va.
appointed commissioner in chancery of the courts of Richmond in 1868
served as Commonwealth attorney of Charles City and New Kent Counties
member of the State senate in 1869
elected as a Republican to the Forty-third Congress (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1875)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1874
resumed the practice of law in Washington, D.C.
member of the immigration commission to London
died in Washington, D.C., on January 6, 1892
interment in Glenwood Cemetery.

SMITH, John Speed, (father of Green Clay Smith), a Representative from Kentucky
born near Nicholasville, Jessamine County, Ky., July 1, 1792
attended a private school in Mercer County
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1812 and commenced practice in Richmond, Ky.
during the War of 1812 enlisted as a private, and subsequently promoted to major
aide-de-camp to General Harrison with the rank of colonel
member of the state house of representatives in 1819
elected as a Republican to the Seventeenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of George Robertson and served from August 6, 1821, to March 3, 1823
was not a candidate for renomination in 1822
again a member of the state house of representatives in 1827, 1830, 1839, 1841, and 1845, and served as speaker in 1827
United States district attorney for Kentucky 1828-1832
member of the state senate 1846-1850
died in Richmond, Ky., June 6, 1854
interment in Richmond Cemetery.

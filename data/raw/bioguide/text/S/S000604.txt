SMITH, Robert, (nephew of Jeremiah Smith and Samuel Smith of New Hampshire), a Representative from Illinois
born in Peterborough, Hillsboro County, N.H., June 12, 1802
attended the public schools and New Ipswich Academy
taught school
engaged in mercantile pursuits in 1822 and in the manufacturing of textile goods in Northfield, N.H., in 1823
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
moved to Illinois and settled in Alton in 1832 and again engaged in mercantile pursuits
elected captain in the state militia in 1832
extensive land owner and engaged in the real estate business
member of the state house of representatives 1836-1840
elected enrolling and engrossing clerk of the state house of representatives in 1840 and 1842
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-eighth and Twenty-ninth Congresses and reelected as an Independent Democrat to the Thirtieth Congress (March 4, 1843-March 3, 1849)
chairman, Committee on Roads and Canals (Twenty-ninth Congress)
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
chairman, Committee on Mileage (Thirty-fifth Congress)
served as paymaster during the Civil War
died in Alton, Ill., December 21, 1867
interment in Alton City Cemetery.

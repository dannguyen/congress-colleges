SMITH, William Ephraim, a Representative from Georgia
born in Augusta, Richmond County, Ga., March 14, 1829
pursued an academic course
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1846, under a special act of the legislature, and practiced in Albany, Ga.
also a planter
ordinary of Dougherty County, Ga., in 1853
solicitor general of the southwest circuit 1858-1860
during the Civil War enlisted in the Confederate Army as a first lieutenant in the Fourth Georgia Volunteer Infantry
elected captain in April 1862
elected to the house of representatives of the Second Confederate Congress in 1863
declined the office of circuit judge of Georgia in 1874
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth, Forty-fifth, and Forty-sixth Congresses (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1881)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1880
resumed the practice of law
president of the Democratic State convention in 1886
served in the State senate 1886-1888
died in Albany, Dougherty County, Ga., March 11, 1890
interment in Oakview Cemetery.

SMITHWICK, John Harris, a Representative from Florida
born near Orange, Cherokee County, Ga., July 17, 1872
attended the public schools
was graduated from Reinhardt Normal College, Waleska, Ga., in 1895 and from the law department of Cumberland University, Lebanon, Tenn., in 1897
was admitted to the bar in 1898 and commenced the practice of his profession in Moultrie, Ga.
moved to Pensacola, Fla., in 1906 and continued the practice of his profession
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1919-March 3, 1927)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1926
engaged in the real estate business in Fort Myers, Lee County, Fla., and in Washington, D.C.
retired in 1932 and resided in Moultrie, Ga., until his death on December 2, 1948
interment in Westview Cemetery.

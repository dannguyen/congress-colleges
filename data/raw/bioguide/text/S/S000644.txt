SMOOT, Reed, a Senator from Utah
born in Salt Lake City, Utah, January 10, 1862
moved with his parents to Provo, Utah County, Utah, in 1874
attended local schools and academies and graduated from the Brigham Young Academy (now Brigham Young University) at Provo in 1879
engaged in banking, mining, livestock raising, and in the manufacture of woolen goods
elected as a Republican to the United States Senate in 1902
reelected in 1908, 1914, 1920 and 1926 and served from March 4, 1903, to March 3, 1933
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1932
chairman, Committee on Patents (Sixtieth Congress), Committee on Printing (Sixty-first and Sixty-second Congresses), Committee on Public Lands (Sixty-second and Sixty-sixth Congresses), Committee on Expenditures in the Interior Department (Sixty-third through Sixty-fifth Congresses), Committee on Public Lands and Surveys (Sixty-seventh Congress), Committee on Finance (Sixty-eighth through Seventy-second Congresses)
co-author of the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act of 1930
moved to Salt Lake City, Utah, in 1933
retired from active business pursuits
served as one of the twelve apostles of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and at the time of his death was next in line to succeed the president of the quorum and third to succeed the president
died in St. Petersburg, Fla., February 9, 1941
interment in Provo City Cemetery, Provo, Utah.

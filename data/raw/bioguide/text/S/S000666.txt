SNYDER, Homer Peter, a Representative from New York
born in Amsterdam, Amsterdam County, N.Y., December 6, 1863
attended the common schools
was employed in various capacities in knitting mills until 1887
moved to Little Falls, N.Y., in 1887 and continued employment in knitting mills
engaged in the manufacture of knitting machinery in 1890 and, later, of bicycles and other wheeled vehicles
director and vice president of the Little Falls National Bank
served one term as school commissioner in 1895 and two terms as fire and police commissioner of Little Falls in 1910 and 1911
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1912 to the Sixty-third Congress
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fourth and to the four succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1915-March 3, 1925)
chairman, Committee on Indian Affairs (Sixty-sixth through Sixty-eighth Congresses), Committee on World War VeteransÂ’ Legislation (Sixty-eighth Congress)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1924
delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1916 and 1920
resumed his former manufacturing pursuits
died in Little Falls, N.Y., December 30, 1937
interment in the Church Street Cemetery.

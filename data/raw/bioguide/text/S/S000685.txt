SOUTHARD, Henry, (father of Isaac Southard and Samuel Lewis Southard), a Representative from New Jersey
born in Hempstead, Long Island, N.Y., October 7, 1747
moved with his parents to Basking Ridge, N.J., in 1755
attended the common schools and worked on a farm
served as a private and later as wagon master during the Revolutionary War
engaged in agricultural pursuits
justice of the peace 1787-1792
member of the State general assembly 1797-1799 and in 1811
elected as a Republican to the Seventh and to the four succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1801-March 3, 1811)
chairman, Committee on Revisal and Unfinished Business (Eleventh Congress)
elected to the Fourteenth, Fifteenth, and Sixteenth Congresses (March 4, 1815-March 3, 1821)
resumed farming
died in Basking Ridge, Somerset County, N.J., May 22, 1842
interment in Basking Ridge Cemetery.

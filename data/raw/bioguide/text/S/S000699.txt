SPANGLER, David, a Representative from Ohio
born in Sharpsburg, Washington County, Md., on December 2, 1796
moved with his parents to Zanesville, Ohio, in 1802
attended the public schools
worked at the blacksmithÂ’s trade
engaged in mercantile pursuits
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1824 and commenced practice in Zanesville
unsuccessful candidate for election to the state house of representatives in 1830
moved to Coshocton, Ohio, in 1832 and continued the practice of law
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1836 and for the nomination for governor of Ohio in 1844
died in Coshocton, Ohio, on October 18, 1856
interment in South Lawn Cemetery.

SPAULDING, Elbridge Gerry, a Representative from New York
born in Summer Hill, Cayuga County, N.Y., on February 24, 1809
completed preparatory studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1836 and commenced practice in Batavia, Genesee County, N.Y.
moved to Buffalo, N.Y., in 1834
mayor of Buffalo in 1847
member of the State assembly in 1848
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-first Congress (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1851)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1850
treasurer of the State of New York in 1854 and 1855
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-sixth and Thirty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1859-March 3, 1863)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1862 to the Thirty-eighth Congress
organized the Farmers & MechanicsÂ’ National Bank in Buffalo in 1864
died in Buffalo, N.Y., May 5, 1897
interment in Forest Lawn Cemetery.

SPEAKS, John Charles, a Representative from Ohio
born in Canal Winchester, Franklin County, Ohio, February 11, 1859
attended the public schools
engaged in milling and the lumber business
fish, game, and conservation officer of Ohio 1907-1918
member of the Ohio National Guard for more than forty years, advancing from private to brigadier general
during the Spanish-American War served as major of the Fourth Regiment, Ohio Volunteer Infantry, participating in the Puerto Rican campaign
commanded the Second Brigade of the Ohio National Guard on the Mexican Border in 1916
during the First World War commanded the Seventy-third Brigade, Thirty-seventh Division
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1918 to the Sixty-sixth Congress
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-seventh and to the four succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1921-March 3, 1931)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1930 to the Seventy-second Congress, and for election in 1932 to the Seventy-third Congress, and in 1934 to the Seventy-fourth Congress
died in Columbus, Ohio, November 6, 1945
interment in Union Grove Cemetery, Canal Winchester, Ohio.

ST. JOHN, Charles, a Representative from New York
born at Mount Hope, Orange County, N.Y., October 8, 1818
attended the common schools and Goshen and Newburgh (N.Y.) Academies
engaged in lumbering on the Delaware River and in mercantile pursuits and banking at Port Jervis, N.Y.
served as internal revenue collector and later as president of the Barrett Bridge Co.
elected as a Republican to the Forty-second and Forty-third Congresses (March 4, 1871-March 3, 1875)
resumed his former business activities
died in Port Jervis, N.Y., July 6, 1891
interment in Laurel Grove Cemetery.

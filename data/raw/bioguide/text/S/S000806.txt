STANTON, Richard Henry, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Alexandria, Va., September 9, 1812
completed preparatory studies
attended Alexandria Academy
studied law
was admitted to the bar and began practice in Maysville, Ky., in 1835
editor of the Maysville Monitor 1835-1842
postmaster of Maysville
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-first, Thirty-second, and Thirty-third Congresses (March 4, 1849-March 3, 1855)
chairman, Committee on Public Buildings and Grounds (Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses), Committee on Elections (Thirty-third Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1854 to the Thirty-fourth Congress
StateÂ’s attorney 1858-1861
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1868
district judge 1868-1874
resumed the practice of law until his retirement in 1885
died in Maysville, Ky., March 20, 1891
interment in Maysville Cemetery.

STEDMAN, William, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Cambridge, Mass., January 21, 1765
was graduated from Harvard University in 1784
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1787 and practiced in Lancaster, Charlestown, and Worcester
appointed justice of the peace in 1790
town clerk of Lancaster 1795-1800
member of the State house of representatives in 1802
executive chancellor of Lancaster, 1803-1807
elected as a Federalist to the Eighth and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1803, until his resignation July 16, 1810
clerk of Worcester County Courts 1810-1816
late in life moved to Newburyport, Essex County, Mass., where he died August 31, 1831
interment in Old Hill Burying Ground.

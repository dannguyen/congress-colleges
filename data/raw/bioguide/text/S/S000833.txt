STEELE, John Benedict, a Representative from New York
born in Delhi, N.Y., March 28, 1814
attended Delaware Academy at Delhi and was graduated in law from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass.
was admitted to the bar of Otsego County in 1839 and commenced practice in Cooperstown, N.Y.
district attorney of Otsego County 1841-1847
moved to Kingston in 1847
elected special judge of Ulster County in 1850
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-seventh and Thirty-eighth Congresses (March 4, 1861-March 3, 1865)
was an unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1864 to the Thirty-ninth Congress
was again a candidate for the nomination in 1866, but died on the eve of the primary
was accidentally killed in Rondout, near Kingston, N.Y., September 24, 1866
interment in Wiltwyck Cemetery, Kingston, N.Y.

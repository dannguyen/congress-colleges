STENNIS, John Cornelius, a Senator from Mississippi
born near De Kalb, Kemper County, Miss., August 3, 1901
attended the county schools
graduated, Mississippi State College 1923
graduated, University of Virginia Law School 1928
admitted to the bar in 1928 and commenced practice in De Kalb, Miss.
member, State house of representatives 1928-1932
district prosecuting attorney 1932-1937
circuit judge 1937-1947
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate on November 4, 1947, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Theodore G. Bilbo and served from November 5, 1947, to January 3, 1953
reelected in 1952, 1958, 1964, 1970, 1976 and 1982 for the term ending January 3, 1989
was not a candidate for reelection in 1988
President pro tempore of the Senate during the One Hundredth Congress
chairman, Select Committee on Standards and Conduct (Eighty-ninth through Ninety-third Congresses), Committee on Armed Services (Ninety-first through Ninety-sixth Congresses), Committee on Appropriations (One Hundredth Congress)
was a resident of Starkville, Miss., and later, Madison, Miss., until his death in Jackson, April 23, 1995
interment in Pinecrest Cemetery, DeKalb, Miss.

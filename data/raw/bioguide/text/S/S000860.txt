STEPHENS, Robert Grier, Jr., (great-great-nephew of Alexander Hamilton Stephens), a Representative from Georgia
born in Atlanta, Fulton County, Ga., August 14, 1913
graduated from Boys High School, Atlanta, Ga., 1931
A.B., University of Georgia, Athens, Ga., 1935
M.A., University of Georgia, Athens, Ga., 1937
LL.B., University of Georgia Law School, Athens, Ga., 1941
attended the University of Hamburg, Germany, 1935-1936
United States Army, 1941-1946
faculty, University of Georgia, Athens, Ga.
legal staff of Mr. Justice Jackson at the Nuremberg trials of major Nazi war criminals
lawyer, private practice
city attorney of Athens, Ga., 1947-1950
member of the Georgia state senate, 1951-1953
member of the Georgia state house of representatives, 1953-1959
delegate, Democratic National Convention, 1964
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-seventh and to the seven succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1961-January 3, 1977)
was not a candidate for reelection to the Ninety-fifth Congress in 1976
died on February 20, 2003, in Athens, Ga.
interment in Oconee Hill Cemetery, Athens, Clarke County, Ga.

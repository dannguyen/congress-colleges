STEVENS, Aaron Fletcher, a Representative from New Hampshire
born in Londonderry, Rockingham County, N.H., August 9, 1819
attended Pinkerton Academy, Derry, N.H., and CrosbyÂ’s Nashua Literary Institute, Nashua, N.H.
at the age of sixteen was apprenticed to the trade of machinist and worked as a journeyman for several years
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Nashua, Hillsborough County, N.H.
member of the State house of representatives in 1845
held several local offices
during the Civil War served in the Union Army as major of the First Regiment, New Hampshire Volunteer Infantry, as colonel of the Thirteenth New Hampshire Volunteer Infantry, and was brevetted brigadier general
delegate to the Whig National Convention in 1852
president of the common council of Nashua in 1853 and 1854
solicitor of Hillsborough County 1856-1861
city solicitor of Nashua in 1859, 1860, 1865, 1872, and 1875-1877
elected as a Republican to the Fortieth and Forty-first Congresses (March 4, 1867-March 3, 1871)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1870 to the Forty-second Congress
again a member of the State house of representatives 1876-1884
resumed the practice of law
died in Nashua, N.H., May 10, 1887
interment in Nashua Cemetery.

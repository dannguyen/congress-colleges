STEWART, James Fleming, a Representative from New Jersey
born in Paterson, N.J., June 15, 1851
attended the public and private schools of Paterson
was graduated from the law department of the University of New York at New York City in 1870
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced the practice of law in New York City
returned to Paterson, N.J., and continued the practice of law in 1875
recorder (criminal magistrate) of the city of Paterson 1890-1895
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1903)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of the Navy (Fifty-fifth through Fifty-seventh Congresses)
was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1902 to the Fifty-eighth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Paterson, N.J., where he died on January 21, 1904
interment in Cedar Lawn Cemetery.

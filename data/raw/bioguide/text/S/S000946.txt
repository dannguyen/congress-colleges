STOKES, Edward Lowber, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Philadelphia, Pa., September 29, 1880
attended the public schools and was graduated from St. Pauls School, Concord, N.H.
employed as a clerk for a trust company and later engaged as an investment dealer
unsuccessful candidate for election to the State house of representatives in 1930
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-second Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of George S. Graham
reelected to the Seventy-third Congress and served from November 3, 1931, to January 3, 1935
was not a candidate for renomination but was a gubernatorial candidate in 1934, a candidate for Congress in 1950, and a candidate for mayor and councilman at large in 1952
engaged in investment banking until his retirement in 1955
resident of Newtown Square, Pa.
died November 8, 1964
interment in St. Davids Church Burial Grounds, Radnor Township, Pa.

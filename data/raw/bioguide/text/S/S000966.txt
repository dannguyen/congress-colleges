STONE, William Alexis, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Wellsboro, Delmar Township, Tioga County, Pa., April 18, 1846
attended the public schools and the State normal school at Mansfield, Tioga County, Pa.
served in the Civil War as second lieutenant of Company A, One Hundred and Eighty-seventh Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry
after the war became a lieutenant colonel in the State National Guard
studied law in Wellsboro, Pa.
was admitted to the bar in 1870 and practiced in Wellsboro and Pittsburgh, Pa.
district attorney of Tioga County from 1874 to 1876, when he resigned and moved to Pittsburgh, Pa.
served as United States attorney for the western district of Pennsylvania 1880-1886
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-second and to the three succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1891, until his resignation on November 9, 1898
Governor of Pennsylvania from 1899 to 1903
resumed the practice of his profession in Pittsburgh, Pa.
prothonotary of the eastern district of Pennsylvania and served from January 1, 1916, until his death in Philadelphia, Pa., on March 1, 1920
interment in Wellsboro Cemetery, Wellsboro, Pa.

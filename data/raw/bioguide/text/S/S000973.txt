STORKE, Thomas More, a Senator from California
born in Santa Barbara, Calif., November 23, 1876
attended the public schools
graduated from Stanford University, Palo Alto, Calif., 1898
editor and publisher of Santa Barbara News-Press and its predecessors
rancher and citrus fruit grower
postmaster, Santa Barbara 1914-1921
appointed on November 9, 1938, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of William Gibbs McAdoo and served from November 9, 1938, to January 3, 1939
was not a candidate for election for the full term
resumed newspaper business
member, California Crime Commission 1951-1952
member of the board of regents of University of California 1955-1960
died in Santa Barbara, Calif., October 12, 1971
interment in Santa Barbara Cemetery.

STRAIT, Thomas Jefferson, a Representative from South Carolina
born in Chester District, S.C., December 25, 1846
attended the common schools of Mayesville, S.C., and Cooper Institute, Mississippi
during the Civil War entered the Confederate Army in 1862 and served throughout the war, first in Company A, Sixth Regiment of Infantry, and later as sergeant in Company H, Twenty-fourth Regiment, GistÂ’s brigade
engaged in agricultural pursuits
taught school in Ebenezer, York County, S.C., in 1880
was graduated from South Carolina Medical College at Charleston in 1885 and practiced medicine
member of the State senate 1890-1893
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-third, Fifty-fourth, and Fifty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1893-March 3, 1899)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1898 to the Fifty-sixth Congress
resumed the practice of his profession in Lancaster, S.C., and died there on April 18, 1924
interment in Westside Cemetery.

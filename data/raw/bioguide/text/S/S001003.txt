STRICKLAND, Randolph, a Representative from Michigan
born in Dansville, N.Y., February 4, 1823
attended the common schools
moved to Michigan in 1844 and taught school in Ingham County
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1849 and commenced practice in De Witt, Clinton County, Mich.
moved to St. Johns, Clinton County, and continued the practice of law
elected prosecuting attorney for Clinton County in 1852, 1854, 1856, 1858, and 1862
member of the State senate in 1861 and 1862
provost marshal of the Sixth Congressional District 1863-1865
delegate to the Republican National Conventions in 1856 and 1868
elected as a Republican to the Forty-first Congress (March 4, 1869-March 3, 1871)
was an unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1870
resumed the practice of law
died in Battle Creek, Mich., May 5, 1880
interment in De Witt Cemetery, De Witt, Mich.

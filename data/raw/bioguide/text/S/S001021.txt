STRONG, William, (cousin of Theron Rudd Strong), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Somers, Conn., May 6, 1808
attended Munson Academy, Massachusetts, and was graduated from Yale College in 1828
taught school in New Haven, Conn.
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1832 and commenced practice in Reading, Pa.
elected as a Democrat to the Thirtieth and Thirty-first Congresses (March 4, 1847-March 3, 1851)
chairman, Committee on Elections (Thirty-first Congress)
did not seek renomination in 1850
associate justice of the supreme court of Pennsylvania 1857-1868
practiced law in Philadelphia, Pa.
appointed as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States in 1870 and served until December 1880, when he retired
resumed the practice of law
died at Lake Minnewassa, N.Y., August 19, 1895
interment in Charles Evans Cemetery, Reading, Pa.

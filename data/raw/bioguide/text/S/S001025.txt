STROTHER, James French, (son of George French Strother and grandfather of James French Strother [1868-1930]), a Representative from Virginia
born in Culpeper, Va., September 4, 1811
completed preparatory studies and attended St. Louis University, Missouri
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Washington, Rappahannock County, Va.
member of the State house of delegates 1840-1851, serving as speaker in 1851
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1850
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-second Congress (March 4, 1851-March 3, 1853)
resumed the practice of law in Culpeper, Va.
died near Culpeper, Va., September 20, 1860
interment in the Masonic Cemetery.

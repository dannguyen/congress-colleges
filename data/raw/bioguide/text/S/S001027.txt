STROWD, William Franklin, a Representative from North Carolina
born near Chapel Hill, Orange County, N.C., December 7, 1832
attended the country schools, Bingham private school, Melbane, N.C., the local academy at High Hill, N.C. and Graham Institute
farmer
Army, Confederate States of America
member of the North Carolina state constitutional convention in 1875
unsuccessful Populist candidate for election to the Fifty-third Congress in 1892
elected as a Populist to the Fifty-fourth and to the succeeding Congress (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1899)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1898
died on December 12, 1911, in Chapel Hill, N.C.
interment in Chapel Hill Cemetery, Chapel Hill, N.C.

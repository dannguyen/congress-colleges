STUART, Philip, a Representative from Maryland
born near Fredericksburg, Va., in 1760
completed his preparatory education
moved to Maryland
served in the Revolutionary Army as a lieutenant in the Third Continental Dragoons
wounded at Eutaw Springs September 8, 1781
transferred to BaylorÂ’s dragoons November 9, 1782
lieutenant, Second Artillerists and Engineers, June 5, 1798
resigned November 15, 1800
served in the War of 1812
elected as a Federalist to the Twelfth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1811-March 3, 1819)
died in Washington, D.C., August 14, 1830
interment in the Congressional Cemetery.

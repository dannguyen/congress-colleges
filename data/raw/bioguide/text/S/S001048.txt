STURGES, Lewis Burr, (son of Jonathan Sturges), a Representative from Connecticut
born in Fairfield, Conn., March 15, 1763
was graduated from Yale College in 1782
engaged in mercantile pursuits in New Haven
returned to Fairfield, Conn., in 1786
clerk of the probate court for the district of Fairfield 1787-1791
member of the State house of representatives 1794-1803
elected as a Federalist to the Ninth Congress to fill in part the vacancies caused by the resignations of Calvin Goddard and Roger Griswold
reelected to the Tenth and to the four succeeding Congresses and served from September 16, 1805, to March 3, 1817
moved to Norwalk, Huron County, Ohio, where he died March 30, 1844

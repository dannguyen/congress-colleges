SULLIVAN, William Van Amberg, a Representative and a Senator from Mississippi
born near Winona, Montgomery County, Miss., December 18, 1857
attended the common schools in Panola County and the University of Mississippi at Oxford
graduated from Vanderbilt University, Nashville, Tenn., in 1875
admitted to the bar in 1875 and commenced practice in Austin, Tunica County
moved to Oxford, Lafayette County, Miss., in 1877
member of the board of city aldermen
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-fifth Congress and served from March 4, 1897, to May 31, 1898, when he resigned, having been appointed Senator
appointed and subsequently elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Edward C. Walthall and served from May 31, 1898, to March 3, 1901
not a candidate for reelection
retired from active business and resided in Washington, D.C.
died in Oxford, Miss., March 21, 1918
interment in St. Peter's Cemetery.

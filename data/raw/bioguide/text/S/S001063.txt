SULLOWAY, Cyrus Adams, a Representative from New Hampshire
born in Grafton, Grafton County, N.H., June 8, 1839
attended the common schools, Colby Academy, and Kimball Academy
studied law in Franklin, N.H.
was admitted to the bar in 1863 and commenced practice in Manchester, N.H.
member of the State house of representatives in 1872, 1873, and 1887-1893
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth and to the eight succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1913)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of Justice (Fifty-fifth Congress), Committee on Invalid Pensions (Fifty-sixth through Sixty-second Congresses)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1912 to the Sixty-third Congress
elected to the Sixty-fourth and Sixty-fifth Congresses and served from March 4, 1915, until his death in Washington, D.C., March 11, 1917
interment in the City Cemetery, Franklin, N.H.

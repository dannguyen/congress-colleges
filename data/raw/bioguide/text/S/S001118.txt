SWIFT, Oscar William, a Representative from New York
born in Paines Hollow, Herkimer County, N.Y., April 11, 1869
moved to Michigan with his parents, who settled in Adrian in 1877
attended the public schools and the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor
was graduated from the New York Law School, New York City, in 1896
was admitted to the bar in 1897 and commenced practice in New York City
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fourth and Sixty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1915-March 3, 1919)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1918 to the Sixty-sixth Congress
resumed law practice in New York City
died in Brooklyn, N.Y., June 30, 1940
interment in Kensico Cemetery, Valhalla, N.Y.

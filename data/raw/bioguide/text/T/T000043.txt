TARSNEY, John Charles, a Representative from Missouri
born in Medina, Lenawee County, Mich., November 7, 1845
attended the common schools
during the Civil War enlisted in the Fourth Regiment, Michigan Volunteer Infantry, in August 1862
mustered out of the service in June 1865
attended high school in Hudson, Mich.
was graduated from the law department of the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor in 1869
was admitted to the bar the same year and commenced practice in Hudson, Mich.
moved to Kansas City, Mo., in 1872
city attorney of Kansas City in 1874 and 1875
elected as a Democrat to the Fifty-first, Fifty-second, and Fifty-third Congresses (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1895)
chairman, Committee on Labor (Fifty-second Congress)
presented credentials as a Member-elect to the Fifty-fourth Congress and served from March 4, 1895, to February 27, 1896, when he was succeeded by Robert T. Van Horn, who contested his election
appointed by President Cleveland associate justice of the supreme court of Oklahoma Territory in 1896 and served until 1899
returned to Kansas City, Mo., in 1899 and resumed the practice of law
died in Kansas City, Mo., September 4, 1920
interment in Mount St. Mary's Cemetery.

TENER, John Kinley, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in County Tyrone, Ireland, July 25, 1863
immigrated to the United States with his parents, who settled in Pittsburgh, Pa., in 1872
attended the public and high schools
was employed by manufacturing firms and corporations in and about Pittsburgh
engaged as a professional baseball player 1885-1890
entered the banking business in Charleroi, Pa., in 1901, serving as cashier and later as president of the First National Bank
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-first Congress and served from March 4, 1909, to January 16, 1911, when he resigned, having been elected Governor
Governor of Pennsylvania 1911-1915
president of the National League of Professional Baseball Clubs 1914-1918
elected as director of the Philadelphia National League Baseball Club in 1931
engaged in the insurance business until his death
died in Pittsburgh Pa., May 19, 1946
interment in Homewood Cemetery.

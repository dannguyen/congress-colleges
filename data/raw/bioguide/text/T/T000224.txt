THOMPSON, William George, (brother of John McCandless Thompson), a Representative from Iowa
born near Butler, Butler County, Pa., January 17, 1830
attended the common schools and the Witherspoon Institute in Butler, Pa.
studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1853
moved to Iowa the same year and settled in Marion, Linn County, where he commenced the practice of law
prosecuting attorney of Linn County 1854-1856
member of the State senate 1856-1860
served during the Civil War as major of the Twentieth Regiment, Iowa Volunteer Infantry, in 1862
district attorney for the eighth judicial district for six years
appointed chief justice of the Territory of Idaho and served from January 13, 1879, until his resignation in April of that year
elected as a Republican to the Forty-sixth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Rush Clark
reelected to the Forty-seventh Congress and served from October 14, 1879, to March 3, 1883
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1882
served on the city council of Marion
member of the State house of representatives 1885-1887
judge of the eighteenth judicial district of Iowa 1894-1906
moved to Kenwood Park, Linn County, Iowa, in 1896 and died there April 2, 1911
interment in Oak Shade Cemetery, Marion, Iowa.

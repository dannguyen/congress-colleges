TIMBERLAKE, Charles Bateman, a Representative from Colorado
born in Wilmington, Clinton County, Ohio, September 25, 1854
attended the common schools and Earlham College, Richmond, Ind., 1871-1874
taught school
moved to Colorado in 1885, and settled near Holyoke, Phillips County
engaged in agricultural pursuits and stock raising
member of the Republican State committee 1892-1910
superintendent of schools of Phillips County 1889-1895
county clerk 1895-1897
appointed receiver of the United States land office at Sterling, Colo., on July 1, 1897, and served until April 30, 1914
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-fourth and to the eight succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1915-March 3, 1933)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1932
engaged in banking in Sterling, Colo., until his death there on May 31, 1941
interment in Grand View Cemetery, Fort Collins, Colo.

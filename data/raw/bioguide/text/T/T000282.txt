TINCHER, Jasper Napoleon, a Representative from Kansas
born near Browning, Sullivan County, Mo., November 2, 1878
moved with his parents to Medicine Lodge, Barber County, Kans., in 1892
attended the common and high schools
taught school in Hardtner, Kans., from 1896 until February 1899
worked and studied in a law office and was admitted to the bar in May 1899
commenced the practice of law in Medicine Lodge, Kans.
also interested in farming and stock raising
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1919-March 3, 1927)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1926
moved to Hutchinson, Kans., in 1926 and practiced law until his death there on November 6, 1951
interment in Memorial Park Cemetery.

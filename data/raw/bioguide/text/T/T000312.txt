TOOLE, Joseph Kemp, a Delegate from the Territory of Montana
born in Savannah, Andrew County, Mo., May 12, 1851
attended the public schools in St. Joseph, Mo., and the Western Military Academy, Newcastle, Ky.
moved to Helena, Mont., in 1870
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1871 and commenced practice in Helena, Mont.
district attorney of the third judicial district of Montana 1872-1876
member of the Territorial house of representatives 1879-1881
member and president of the Territorial council 1881-1883
delegate to the State constitutional conventions at Helena, Mont., in 1884 and 1889
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-ninth and Fiftieth Congresses (March 4, 1885-March 3, 1889)
did not seek renomination in 1888, having become a gubernatorial candidate
elected as the first Governor of Montana and served from November 8, 1889, until January 1, 1893
resumed the practice of law in Helena
delegate to the Democratic National Conventions in 1892 and 1904
again elected Governor in November 1900
reelected in 1904 and served from January 7, 1901, until April 1, 1908, when he resigned on account of ill health
lived in retirement, dividing his time between his home in Helena, Mont., and San Francisco, Calif.
died in Helena, Mont., March 11, 1929
interment in Resurrection Cemetery.

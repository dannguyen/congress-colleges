TOWNSEND, Martin Ingham, a Representative from New York
born in Hancock, Mass., February 6, 1810
moved with his parents to Williamstown, Mass, in 1816
attended the common schools, and was graduated from Williams College, Williamstown, Mass., in 1833
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1836 and commenced practice in Troy, N.Y.
district attorney of Rensselaer County 1842-1845
delegate to the State constitutional convention in 1867 and 1868
regent of New York University 1873-1903
elected as a Republican to the Forty-fourth and Forty-fifth Congresses (March 4, 1875-March 3, 1879)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1878
United States district attorney for the northern district of New York 1879-1887
member of the State constitutional convention in 1890
retired from legal practice in 1901
died in Troy, N.Y., March 8, 1903
interment in Oakwood Cemetery.

TRIGG, Abram, (brother of John Johns Trigg), a Representative from Virginia
born on his fathers estate, near Old Liberty (now Bedford), Va., in 1750
completed academic studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Montgomery County, Va.
lived on his estate, Buchanans Bottom, on New River
held local offices, such as clerk and judge, and various other offices in Montgomery County
served in the Revolutionary War as lieutenant colonel of militia in 1782 and later as general of militia in Virginia
delegate to the Virginia ratification convention of 1788
elected as a Republican to the Fifth and to the five succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1797-March 3, 1809)
died and was buried on the family estate
death date unknown.

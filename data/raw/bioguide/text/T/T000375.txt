TRIMBLE, Lawrence Strother, a Representative from Kentucky
born near Flemingsburg, Fleming County, Ky., August 26, 1825
completed preparatory studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1847 and commenced practice in Paducah, Ky.
member of the State house of representatives in 1851 and 1852
judge of the equity and criminal court of the first judicial circuit of Kentucky 1856-1860
president of the New Orleans & Ohio Railroad Co. 1860-1865
unsuccessful candidate for election in 1868 to the Thirty-eighth Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-ninth, Fortieth, and Forty-first Congresses (March 4, 1865-March 3, 1871)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1870
resumed the practice of law
moved to Albuquerque, N.Mex., in 1879 and continued the practice of law until 1889, when he retired
died in Albuquerque, N.Mex., August 9, 1904
interment in Fairview Cemetery.

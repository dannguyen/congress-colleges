TROTTER, James Fisher, a Senator from Mississippi
born in Brunswick County, Va., November 5, 1802
moved to eastern Tennessee
attended private schools
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1820 and commenced practice in Hamilton, Monroe County, Miss., in 1823
member, State house of representatives 1827-1829
member, State senate 1829-1833
judge of the circuit court of Mississippi in 1833
appointed as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of John Black and served from January 22 to July 10, 1838, when he resigned
judge of the supreme court of Mississippi 1839-1842, when he resigned
moved to Holly Springs, Marshall County, Miss., and resumed the practice of law in 1840
vice chancellor of the northern district of Mississippi 1855-1857
professor of law at the University of Mississippi 1860-1862
appointed circuit judge in 1866 and served until his death in Holly Springs, Miss., March 9, 1866
interment in Hill Crest Cemetery.

TRUMBULL, Joseph, a Representative from Connecticut
born in Lebanon, Conn., December 7, 1782
completed preparatory studies
was graduated from Yale College in 1801
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1803 at Windham, Conn., and commenced practice in Hartford, Conn.
in 1828 became president of the Hartford Bank and later of the Providence, Hartford & Fishkill Railroad Co.
member of the State house of representatives in 1832
elected to the Twenty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of William W. Ellsworth and served from December 1, 1834, to March 3, 1835
resigned the presidency of the bank in November 1839
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1843)
again a member of the State house of representatives in 1848
Governor of Connecticut in 1849 and 1850
again elected to the State house of representatives in 1851
died in Hartford, Conn., August 4, 1861
interment in the East Cemetery, Lebanon, Conn.

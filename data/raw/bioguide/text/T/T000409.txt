TUNNELL, James Miller, a Senator from Delaware
born in Clarksville, Sussex County, Del., August 2, 1879
attended the public schools
graduated from Franklin College (now combined with Muskingum College at New Concord, Ohio) in 1900
taught in the public schools, advancing to principal of schools at Frankford, Selbyville, and Ocean View, Del., 1903-1907
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1907 and commenced practice in Georgetown, Del.
president of the board of education of Georgetown, Del., 1919-1932
unsuccessful Democratic candidate for election to the United States Senate in 1924
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate in 1940 and served from January 3, 1941, to January 3, 1947
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1946
chairman, Committee on Pensions (Seventy-eighth and Seventy-ninth Congresses)
banker
owned and operated a number of farms in Sussex County, Del.
died in Philadelphia, Pa., November 14, 1957
interment in Blackwater Church Cemetery, near Clarksville, Del.

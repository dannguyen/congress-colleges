UPSHAW, William David, a Representative from Georgia
born near Newnan, Coweta County, Ga., October 15, 1866
attended the country schools, the public schools of Atlanta, Ga., and Mercer University, Macon, Ga.
engaged in agricultural and mercantile pursuits until physically incapacitated by an accident
founded The Golden Age, magazine at Atlanta, Ga., February 22, 1906
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-sixth and to the three succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1919-March 3, 1927)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination in 1926
vice chairman of the Scandinavian Commercial Commission
nominated for President by the Prohibition Party in 1932
unsuccessful candidate for the Democratic nomination for United States Senator in 1942
resumed his former pursuits as a lecturer, evangelist, and writer
vice president of the Linda Vista Baptist Bible College and Seminary and member of the faculty, San Diego, Calif.
at the age of seventy-two was ordained a minister of the Baptist Church
died in Glendale, Calif., November 21, 1952
interment in Forest Lawn Cemetery.

UPTON, Charles Horace, a Representative from Virginia
born in Salem, Mass., August 23, 1812
attended the public schools
was graduated from Bowdoin College, Brunswick, Maine, in 1834
moved to Falls Church, Va., in 1836 and engaged in agricultural and literary pursuits
held several local offices
presented credentials as a Unionist Member-elect to the Thirty-seventh Congress under an election held on May 23, 1861, and served until February 27, 1862, when the House declared he was not entitled to the seat
appointed by President Lincoln in 1863 United States consul to Switzerland and served from July 9, 1863, until his death in Geneva, Switzerland, June 17, 1877
interment in the Congressional Cemetery, Washington, D.C.

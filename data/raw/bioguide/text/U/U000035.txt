UTTER, George Herbert, a Representative from Rhode Island
born in Plainfield, Union County, N.J., July 24, 1854
moved with his parents to Westerly, R.I., in 1861
attended the public schools of Westerly and Alfred (N.Y.) Academy
was graduated from Amherst College, Massachusetts, in 1877
engaged as a printer and newspaper publisher in Westerly, R.I.
personal aide on the staff of Gov. A.O. Bourn 1883-1885
member of the State house of representatives 1885-1889, serving as speaker the last year
served in the State senate 1889-1891
secretary of state of Rhode Island 1891-1894
Lieutenant Governor in 1904
Governor of Rhode Island in 1905 and 1906
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-second Congress and served from March 4, 1911, until his death in Westerly, R.I., November 3, 1912
interment in Riverbend Cemetery.

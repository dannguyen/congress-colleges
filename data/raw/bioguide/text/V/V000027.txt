VANDER JAGT, Guy Adrian, a Representative from Michigan
born in Cadillac, Wexford County, Mich., August 26, 1931
graduated from Cadillac High School, Cadillac, Mich.,1949
B.A., Hope College, Holland, Mich., 1953
B.D., Yale University, New Haven, Conn., 1955
Bonn University, Rotary Fellowship, 1956
J.D., University of Michigan, 1960
lawyer, private practice
member of the Michigan state senate, 1965-1966
elected simultaneously as a Republican to the Eighty-ninth and to the Ninetieth Congress, by special election, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of United States Representative Robert P. Griffin, and reelected to the twelve succeeding Congresses (November 8, 1966-January 3, 1993)
unsuccessful candidate for renomination to the One Hundred Third Congress in 1992
died on June 22, 2007, in Washington, D. C.

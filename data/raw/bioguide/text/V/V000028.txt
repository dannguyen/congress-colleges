VANDERPOEL, Aaron, a Representative from New York
born in Kinderhook, Columbia County, N.Y., February 5, 1799
pursued classical studies
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1820 and commenced practice in Kinderhook, N.Y.
member of the State assembly 1826-1830
elected as a Jacksonian to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1833-March 3, 1837)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1836 to the Twenty-fifth Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth Congress (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1841)
retired from Congress and settled in New York City
judge of the superior court 1842-1850
died in New York City July 18, 1870
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

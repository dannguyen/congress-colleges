VAN RENSSELAER, Jeremiah, (cousin of Killian Killian Van Rensselaer), a Representative from New York
born in New York on August 27, 1738
completed preparatory studies at the manor house, Rensselaerswyck, and in private schools in Albany
graduated from Princeton College in 1758
took an active interest in the Revolutionary War
member of the Albany Committee of Safety
elected to the First Congress (March 4, 1789-March 3, 1791)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Second Congress in 1790
member of the New York state assembly in 1789
member of the first board of directors of the Bank of Albany in 1792 and president of that bank 1798-1806
lieutenant governor of New York 1801-1804
curator of the Evangelical Lutheran Seminary at Albany in 1804
died on February 19, 1810, in Albany, N.Y.
interment in the Dutch Reformed Cemetery
reinterment in Albany Rural Cemetery, Menands, N.Y.

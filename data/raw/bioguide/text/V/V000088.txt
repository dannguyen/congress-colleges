VERPLANCK, Daniel Crommelin, (father of Gulian Crommelin Verplanck), a Representative from New York
born in New York City March 19, 1762
was educated under private tutors
was graduated from Columbia College (now Columbia University), New York City, in 1788
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in New York City in 1789
also engaged in banking
elected as a Republican to the Eighth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Isaac Bloom
reelected to the Ninth and Tenth Congresses and served from October 17, 1803, to March 3, 1809
was not a candidate for renomination in 1808
resumed the practice of law
judge of the court of common pleas of Dutchess County 1828-1830
died at his home, Mount Gulian, near Fishkill, Dutchess County, N.Y., March 29, 1834
interment in Trinity Church Cemetery, Fishkill, N.Y.

VOLLMER, Henry, a Representative from Iowa
born in Davenport, Iowa, July 28, 1867
attended the public and high schools of Davenport
distributing clerk of the Fiftieth Congress in 1887 and 1888
studied law at the University of Iowa at Iowa City and Georgetown University, Washington, D.C.
was admitted to the bar in 1889 and commenced practice in Davenport, Iowa
member of the board of aldermen of Davenport in 1889
served as mayor of Davenport from 1893 to 1897
member of the board of education 1898-1901
corporation counsel in 1913 and 1914
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Irvin S. Pepper and served from February 10, 1914, to March 3, 1915
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1914 to the Sixty-fourth Congress
resumed the practice of his profession
died in Piedmont, Calif., August 25, 1930
the remains were cremated and the ashes placed in a crypt in the California Crematorium.

WAGGAMAN, George Augustus, a Senator from Louisiana
born in Caroline County, Md., in 1782
completed preparatory studies under private tutors
studied law
admitted to the bar in Caroline County, Md., in 1811
served in the War of 1812 under General Andrew Jackson at New Orleans
settled in Baton Rouge, La., and commenced the practice of law in 1813
attorney general of the third district of Louisiana in 1813
judge of the third judicial circuit court in 1818
assistant judge of the criminal court in New Orleans in 1819
interested in sugarcane growing
secretary of state of Louisiana 1830-1832
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Edward Livingston and served from November 15, 1831, to March 3, 1835
resumed the practice of law in New Orleans and also again engaged in sugarcane planting
participated as a principal in a duel and received injuries from which he died in New Orleans, La., March 31, 1843
interment in Girod Cemetery.

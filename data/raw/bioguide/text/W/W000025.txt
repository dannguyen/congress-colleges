WAIT, John Turner, a Representative from Connecticut
born in New London, Conn., August 27, 1811
moved with his mother to Norwich, Conn.
attended the common schools and Trinity College, Hartford, Conn., for two years
engaged in mercantile pursuits
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1836 and began practice in Norwich
StateÂ’s attorney for the county of New London 1842-1844 and 1846-1854
unsuccessful candidate for election as Lieutenant Governor in 1854, 1855, 1856, and 1857
served in the State senate in 1865 and 1866, the latter year as president pro tempore
member of the State house of representatives in 1867, 1871, and 1873, serving as speaker in 1867
elected as a Republican to the Forty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Henry H. Starkweather
reelected as a Republican to the Forty-fifth and to the four succeeding Congresses and served from April 12, 1876, to March 3, 1887
was not a candidate for renomination in 1886
resumed the practice of his profession
died in Norwich, Conn., April 21, 1899
interment in Yantic Cemetery.

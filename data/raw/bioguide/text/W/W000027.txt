WAKEMAN, Abram, a Representative from New York
born in Greenfield Hill, Fairfield County, Conn., May 31, 1824
completed preparatory studies and was graduated from Herkimer Academy, New York
studied law at Little Falls, N.Y.
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in New York City in 1847
member of the State assembly in 1850 and 1851
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1855-March 3, 1857)
unsuccessful Republican candidate for reelection in 1856 to the Thirty-fifth Congress
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1856
at the outbreak of the Civil War raised the Eighty-first Pennsylvania Volunteers
postmaster of New York City from March 21, 1862, to September 18, 1864
surveyor of the port of New York City
resumed the practice of law
died in New York City June 29, 1889
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

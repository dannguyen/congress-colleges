WALKER, E. S. Johnny, a Representative from New Mexico
born in Fulton, Ky., June 18, 1911
attended the public schools
moved with family to Albuquerque, N.Mex., in 1926
graduated from Albuquerque High School
attended University of New Mexico in Albuquerque and the National University (now George Washington University) in Washington, D.C.
served in North African and European Theaters of Operation, 1942-1945
member of State house of representatives, 1949-1952 (majority whip)
commissioner, State Land Office, 1953-1956 and 1961-1964
commissioner of the State Bureau of Revenue, 1960
organizer, director, and member of the State Oil and Gas Accounting Commission
elected as a Democrat to the Eighty-ninth and Ninetieth Congresses (January 3, 1965-January 3, 1969)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Ninety-first Congress
died in Albuquerque, N.Mex., on October 8, 2000
interment at Santa Fe National Cemetery.

WALLACE, Daniel, a Representative from South Carolina
born near Laurens, S.C., May 9, 1801
received a limited schooling
moved to Union County in 1833
major general of State militia
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Union and Jonesville, Union County, S.C.
also engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the State house of representatives 1846-1847
elected as a Democrat to the Thirtieth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of James A. Black
reelected to the Thirty-first and Thirty-second Congresses and served from June 12, 1848, to March 3, 1853
resumed agricultural pursuits
died in Jonesville, S.C., May 13, 1859
interment in Old Presbyterian Cemetery, Union, S.C.

born in New Ipswich, Hillsborough County, N.H., December 21, 1823
attended the common schools
engaged in the manufacture of paper
was a member of the select council of Fitchburg, Mass., in 1864, 1865, and 1867
served in the State house of representatives in 1873
member of the GovernorÂ’s council 1880-1882
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-first Congress (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1891)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1890 to the Fifty-second Congress
again engaged in the manufacture of paper
died in Fitchburg, Worcester County, Mass., on February 27, 1903
interment in Laurel Hill Cemetery.

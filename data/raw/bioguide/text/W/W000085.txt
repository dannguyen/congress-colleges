WALLACE, William Copeland, a Representative from New York
born in Brooklyn, N.Y., May 21, 1856
was graduated from Adelphi Academy, Brooklyn, N.Y., in 1873, from Wesleyan University, Middletown, Conn., in 1876, and from the law department of Columbia College (now Columbia University), New York City, in 1878
commenced the practice of law in New York City
assistant United States attorney for the southern district of New York 1880-1883
appointed judge advocate general on the staff of Governor Morton in 1894
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-first Congress (March 4, 1889-March 3, 1891)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1890 to the Fifty-second Congress
resumed the practice of his profession in Brooklyn, N.Y.
also engaged extensively in banking
died at his summer home in Warwick, Orange County, N.Y., September 4, 1901
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

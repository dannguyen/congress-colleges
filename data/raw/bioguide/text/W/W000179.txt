WASHINGTON, George Corbin, (grandnephew of George Washington), a Representative from Maryland
born on Haywood Farms, near Oak Grove, Westmoreland County, Va., August 20, 1789
attended Harvard University
studied law, but devoted himself to agricultural pursuits on his plantation in Maryland
resided for the most part at Dumbarton Heights, in Georgetown, D.C.
elected to the Twentieth, Twenty-first, and Twenty-second Congresses (March 4, 1827-March 3, 1833)
chairman, Committee on District of Columbia (Twenty-second Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1832
elected as an Anti-Jacksonian to the Twenty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1837)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1836
president of the Chesapeake & Ohio Canal Co.
was appointed by President Tyler in 1844 as a commissioner to adjust and settle the claims arising under the treaty of 1835 with the Cherokee Indians
died in Georgetown, D.C., July 17, 1854
interment in Oak Hill Cemetery.

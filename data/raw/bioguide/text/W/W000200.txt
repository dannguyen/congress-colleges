WATSON, David Kemper, a Representative from Ohio
born near London, Madison County, Ohio, on June 18, 1849
was graduated from Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa., in 1871 and from the law department of Boston University in 1873
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice
assistant United States district attorney for the southern district of Ohio during the administration of President Arthur
elected attorney general of Ohio in 1887 and reelected in 1889
special counsel for the United States in the suits brought by the Government against the Pacific railroads in 1892
elected as a Republican to the Fifty-fourth Congress (March 4, 1895-March 3, 1897)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1896 to the Fifty-fifth Congress
appointed by President McKinley as a member of the commission to revise and codify the laws of the United States
resumed the practice of law
died in Columbus, Ohio, September 28, 1918
interment in Greenlawn Cemetery.

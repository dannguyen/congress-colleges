WATSON, Thomas Edward, a Representative and a Senator from Georgia
born in Columbia County, near Thomson, Ga., September 5, 1856
attended the common schools and Mercer University, Macon, Ga.
taught school
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1875 and commenced practice in Thomson, McDuffie County, Ga., in 1876
also engaged in agricultural pursuits
member, State house of representatives 1882-1883
presidential elector on the Democratic ticket in 1888
elected as a Populist to the Fifty-second Congress (March 4, 1891-March 3, 1893)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1892 to the Fifty-third Congress and in 1894 for election to the Fifty-fourth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Thomson, Ga.
nominated for Vice President by the Populist National Convention in 1896 and for President by the PeopleÂ’s Party in 1904
published a magazine for many years and later engaged in the newspaper business
author
unsuccessful candidate for the United States House of Representatives in 1918
elected as a Democrat to the United States Senate and served from March 4, 1921, until his death in Washington, D.C., September 26, 1922
interment in Thomson Cemetery, Thomson, Ga.

WATTS, John Sebrie, a Delegate from the Territory of New Mexico
born in Boone County, Ky., January 19, 1816
moved to Indiana, where he completed preparatory studies
was graduated from Indiana University at Bloomington
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
member of the State house of representatives in 1846 and 1847
associate justice of the United States court in the Territory of New Mexico from 1851 to 1854, when he resigned
resumed the practice of law
elected as a Republican to the Thirty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1861-March 3, 1863)
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1864
took an active part in equipping troops for the Union Army during the Civil War
appointed chief justice of the supreme court of New Mexico July 11, 1868, by President Johnson, and served in that capacity one year
resumed the practice of law in Santa Fe
returned to Bloomington, Monroe County, Ind., where he died June 11, 1876
interment in Rose Hill Cemetery.

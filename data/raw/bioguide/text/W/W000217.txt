WAYNE, Isaac, (son of Anthony Wayne), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born near Paoli, Chester County, Pa., in 1772
attended the common schools and was graduated from Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa.
studied law
was admitted to the Chester County bar in 1795
member of the State house of representatives 1799-1801 and in 1806
served in the State senate in 1810
during the War of 1812 was captain of a troop of Pennsylvania Horse Cavalry, raised and equipped by himself, and was subsequently colonel of the Second Regiment, Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry
unsuccessful Federalist candidate for Governor in 1814
elected to the Eighteenth Congress (March 4, 1823-March 3, 1825)
engaged in agricultural pursuits
died in Chester County, Pa., October 25, 1852
interment in St. David's Episcopal Church Cemetery, Radnor, Pa.

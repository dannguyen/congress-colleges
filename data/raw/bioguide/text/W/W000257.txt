WEISS, Samuel Arthur, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Krotowocz, Poland, April 15, 1902
immigrated to the United States in 1903 with his parents, who settled in Glassport, Pa.
attended the public schools
B.S., Duquesne University, Pittsburgh, Pa., 1925 and from the law department of the same university, LL.B., 1927 and J.D., 1929
was admitted to the bar in 1927 and commenced practice in Pittsburgh, Pa.
director of the Roselia Maternity Hospital, Pittsburgh, Pa.
served in the State house of representatives 1935-1939
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-seventh Congress
reelected to the two succeeding Congresses and served until his resignation on January 7, 1946 (January 3, 1941-January 7, 1946)
elected in November 1945 a judge of Common Pleas Court of Allegheny County, Pa., for the term commencing in January 1946
reelected in 1955 for the term ending January 1966, and again in 1965 for the term ending January 1976
retired in 1967
president, Pennsylvania State Judicial Administration, 1968
died in Pittsburgh, Pa., February 1, 1977
interment in B'nai Israel Cemetery.

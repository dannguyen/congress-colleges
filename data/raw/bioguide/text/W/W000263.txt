WELCH, John, a Representative from Ohio
born near New Athens, Harrison County, Ohio, October 28, 1805
received a liberal schooling and was graduated from Franklin College
moved to Athens County in 1828 and settled in Rome Township
engaged in the milling business
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Athens, Ohio, in 1833
prosecuting attorney of Athens County 1841-1843
member of the State senate 1845-1847
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-second Congress (March 4, 1851-March 3, 1853)
declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1852
delegate to the Whig National Convention in 1852
resumed the practice of law
judge of the court of common pleas 1862-1865
associate justice of the supreme court of Ohio 1865-1878 and was chief justice in 1877 and 1878
died in Athens, Ohio, August 5, 1891
interment in West Union Street Cemetery.

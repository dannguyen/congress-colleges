WELLS, Daniel, Jr., a Representative from Wisconsin
born in West Waterville, Maine, July 16, 1808
attended the public schools
taught school
engaged in the mercantile business at Palmyra, Maine
moved to Milwaukee, Wis., in 1838 and engaged in banking and lumbering pursuits
appointed probate judge of Milwaukee in 1838
member of the Territorial council 1838-1840
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-third and Thirty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1857)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures in the Department of State (Thirty-third Congress)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1856
engaged in the development of railroads
a director of the Chicago, Milwaukee & St. Paul Railroad in 1865 and 1866
president of the La Crosse & Milwaukee Railroad, the Southern Minnesota Railroad, and the St. Paul & Minnesota Valley Railroad
died in Milwaukee, Wis., March 18, 1902
interment in Forest Home Cemetery.

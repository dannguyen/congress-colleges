WENTWORTH, Tappan, a Representative from Massachusetts
born in Dover, N.H., February 24, 1802
received a liberal schooling
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1826 and commenced practice in York County, Maine
moved to Lowell, Mass., in 1833 and continued the practice of law
member of the common council 1836-1841
served in the State house of representatives in 1851, 1859, 1860, 1863, and 1864
member of the State senate in 1848, 1849, 1865, and 1866
elected as a Whig to the Thirty-third Congress (March 4, 1853-March 3, 1855)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1854 to the Thirty-fourth Congress
engaged in the practice of his profession until his death in Lowell, Mass., June 12, 1875
interment in Lowell Cemetery.

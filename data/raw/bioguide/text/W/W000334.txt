WHEELER, Grattan Henry, a Representative from New York
born near Providence, R.I., August 25, 1783
attended public and preparatory schools
moved to New York with his parents, who settled in Steuben County about 1800
agriculturist and lumberman near Wheeler, N.Y.
member of the State assembly in 1822, 1824, and 1826
served in the State senate 1826-1830
elected as an Anti-Masonic candidate to the Twenty-second Congress (March 4, 1831-March 3, 1833)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Twenty-third Congress
resumed former pursuits
presidential elector on the Whig ticket in 1840
died in Wheeler, Steuben County, N.Y., March 11, 1852
interment in a private cemetery on the Wheeler homestead.

WHITE, Allison, a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Pine Township, near Jersey Shore, Pa., December 21, 1816
attended the public schools and was graduated from Allegheny College, Meadville, Crawford County, Pa.
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Lock Haven, Pa.
was elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-fifth Congress (March 4, 1857-March 3, 1859)
chairman, Committee on Expenditures on Public Buildings (Thirty-fifth Congress)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1858 to the Thirty-sixth Congress
engaged in the lumber and coal business at Philadelphia
died in Philadelphia, Pa., on April 5, 1886
interment in Highland Cemetery, Lock Haven, Clinton County, Pa.

WHITE, Edward Douglass, (son of James White and father of Edward Douglass White [1845-1921]), a Representative from Louisiana
born in Nashville, Tenn., in March 1795
moved with his father to what is now St. Martin Parish, La., in 1799
attended the common schools, and was graduated from the University of Nashville, Tennessee, in 1815
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Donaldsonville, La.
was appointed judge of the city court of New Orleans and moved there in 1825
elected to the Twenty-first, Twenty-second, and Twenty-third Congresses and served from March 4, 1829, to November 15, 1834, when he resigned
Governor of Louisiana from 1834 to 1838
moved to Thibodaux
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses (March 4, 1839-March 3, 1843)
resumed the practice of his profession
also engaged as a planter
died in New Orleans, La., April 18, 1847

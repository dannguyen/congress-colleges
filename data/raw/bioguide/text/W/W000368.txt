WHITE, Francis Shelley (Frank), a Senator from Alabama
born in Prairie, Noxubee County, Miss., March 13, 1847
attended the common schools and was tutored at home
during the Civil War served in the Confederate Army as a private
after the close of the war engaged in agricultural pursuits until 1868
studied law
admitted to the bar in 1869 and commenced practice in West Point, Miss.
member, State house of representatives 1875, 1882-1883
moved to Birmingham, Jefferson County, Ala., in 1886 and continued the practice of law
elected on May 11, 1914, as a Democrat to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Joseph F. Johnston and served from May 11, 1914, to March 3, 1915
was not a candidate for renomination
chairman, Committee on Revolutionary Claims (Fifty-third Congress)
resumed the practice of his profession until his death in Birmingham, Ala., August 1, 1922
interment in Elmwood Cemetery.

WHITE, John, (cousin of Addison White and uncle of John Daugherty White), a Representative from Kentucky
born near Cumberland Gap (now Middlesboro), Ky., February 14, 1802
received a limited schooling
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Richmond, Madison County, Ky.
member of the State house of representatives in 1832
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-fourth and to the four succeeding Congresses (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1845)
Speaker of the House of Representatives (Twenty-seventh Congress)
appointed judge of the nineteenth judicial district of Kentucky and served from February 8, 1845, until his death in Richmond, Ky., September 22, 1845
interment in the State Cemetery, Frankfort, Franklin County, Ky.

WHITE, Joseph Livingston, a Representative from Indiana
born in Cherry Valley, Otsego County, N.Y., birth date unknown
completed preparatory studies
studied law in Utica, N.Y.
was admitted to the bar, and commenced practice in Madison, Ind.
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-seventh Congress (March 4, 1841-March 3, 1843)
moved to New York City and resumed the practice of law
while on a business trip to Nicaragua, Central America, he was shot by a Mr. Gavett as he was leaving the ship at Corinto (Punta Ycacos), Nicaragua, January 5, 1861, dying at Corinto on January 12, 1861
interment in the cemetery at Corinto, Nicaragua.

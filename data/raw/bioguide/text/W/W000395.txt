WHITE, Stephen Van Culen, a Representative from New York
born in Chatham County, N.C., August 1, 1831
moved to Illinois with his parents, who settled near Otterville, Jersey County, Ill.
attended the free school founded by Dr. Silas Hamilton in Otterville, Ill., and was graduated from Knox College, Galesburg, Ill., in 1854
entered a mercantile house in St. Louis, Mo.
studied law and was admitted to the bar November 4, 1856
moved to Des Moines, Iowa, in 1856, and practiced law until January 1, 1865
acting United States district attorney for Iowa in 1864
moved to New York City in 1865 and engaged in banking
member of the New York Stock Exchange
was an astronomer and upon the organization of the American Astronomical Society in 1883 was elected its first president
elected as a Republican to the Fiftieth Congress (March 4, 1887-March 3, 1889)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1888 to the Fifty-first Congress
resumed the practice of law
died in Brooklyn, N.Y., January 18, 1913
interment in Greenwood Cemetery.

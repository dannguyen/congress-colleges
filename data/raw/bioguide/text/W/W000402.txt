WHITEHILL, James, (son of John Whitehill and nephew of Robert Whitehill), a Representative from Pennsylvania
born in Strasburg, Lancaster County, Pa., on January 31, 1762
studied law
was admitted to the bar and commenced practice in Strasburg
associate judge of the Lancaster County Court from January 3, 1811, to February 1, 1813, when he resigned, having been elected to Congress
served in the War of 1812 as major general of Pennsylvania Militia
elected as a Republican to the Thirteenth Congress and served from March 4, 1813, to September 1, 1814, when he resigned
engaged in mercantile pursuits in Strasburg
burgess of Strasburg in 1816
again associate judge of the county court from October 17, 1820, until his death in Strasburg, Pa., on February 26, 1822
interment in the Presbyterian Church Cemetery, Leacock, Lancaster County, Pa.

WHITEHOUSE, John Osborne, a Representative from New York
born in Rochester, Strafford County, N.H., July 19, 1817
received a common-school education
moved to New York City in 1835 and was engaged as a clerk until 1839, when he moved to Brooklyn, N.Y., and engaged as a merchant and manufacturer of shoes
moved to Poughkeepsie, Dutchess County, N.Y., in 1860 and continued the shoe manufacturing business
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-third and Forty-fourth Congresses (March 4, 1873-March 3, 1877)
chairman, Committee on Reform in the Civil Service (Forty-fourth Congress)
was not a candidate for reelection in 1876 to the Forty-fifth Congress
resumed the shoe manufacturing business
also interested in banking and railroading
owner of the Daily News 1872-1880
died in Poughkeepsie, N.Y., August 24, 1881
interment in Greenwood Cemetery, Brooklyn, N.Y.

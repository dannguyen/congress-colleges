WHITESIDE, Jenkin, a Senator from Tennessee
born in Lancaster, Pa., in 1772
pursued preparatory studies
studied law and was admitted to the bar
moved to Tennessee and commenced practice in Knoxville
one of the commissioners of Knoxville in 1801 and 1802
elected as a Democratic Republican to the United States Senate in 1809 to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Daniel Smith
reelected the same year and served from April 11, 1809, to October 8, 1811, when he resigned
resumed the practice of law
died in Nashville, Tenn., September 25, 1822
interment was probably in the Old Cemetery west of the Nashville sulphur spring.

WILLEY, Earle Dukes, a Representative from Delaware
born in Greenwood, Sussex County, Del., July 21, 1889
attended the public schools and George Washington University Law School, Washington, D.C.
was graduated from Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa., in 1911
principal of Greenwood High School 1911-1915
secretary to Hon. Thomas W. Miller, Washington, D.C., 1915-1917
was admitted to the bar in 1920 and commenced practice in Dover, Del.
State librarian 1917-1921
deputy attorney general and prosecuting attorney for Kent County, Del., 1921-1931
judge of the court of common pleas of Kent County 1931-1939 and of the juvenile court of Kent and Sussex Counties 1933-1939
unsuccessful candidate for Lieutenant Governor in 1940
secretary of state 1941-1943
trustee of the University of Delaware, of the Elizabeth W. Murphey School for Orphan Children, and of the State College for Colored Students
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-eighth Congress (January 3, 1943-January 3, 1945)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1944 to the Seventy-ninth Congress
resumed the practice of law in Dover, Del., until his death March 17, 1950
interment in St. Johnstown Cemetery, near Greenwood, Del.

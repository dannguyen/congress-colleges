WILLIAMS, Alpheus Starkey, a Representative from Michigan
born in Saybrook, Middlesex County, Conn., September 20, 1810
was graduated from Yale College in 1831
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1837 and commenced practice in Detroit, Mich.
judge of probate 1840-1844
editor of the Detroit Daily Advertiser 1843-1847
served in the war with Mexico
commissioned lieutenant colonel of the First Michigan Infantry December 8, 1847
mustered out July 29, 1848
postmaster of Detroit 1849-1853
commissioned brigadier general of Michigan Volunteers April 24, 1861, and of United States Volunteers May 17, 1861
brevetted major general of Volunteers January 12, 1865
mustered out January 15, 1866
unsuccessful nominee for Governor of Michigan in 1866
Minister Resident to San Salvador 1866-1869
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-fourth and Forty-fifth Congresses and served from March 4, 1875, until his death in Washington, D.C., December 21, 1878
chairman, Committee on District of Columbia (Forty-fifth Congress)
interment in Elmwood Cemetery, Detroit, Mich.

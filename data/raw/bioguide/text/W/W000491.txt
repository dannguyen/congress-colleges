WILLIAMS, Benjamin, a Representative from North Carolina
born near Smithfield, Johnston County, N.C., January 1, 1751
attended the country schools
engaged in agricultural pursuits
member of the provincial congress in 1774 and 1775
served in the Revolutionary Army as second lieutenant
promoted to captain in the Second Regiment July 19, 1776
promoted to colonel for gallantry at Guilford, N.C., July 12, 1781
member of the State house of commons in 1779, 1785, and 1789
member of the State senate in 1780, 1781, April 1784, October 1784, and 1786, and again in 1807 and 1809
elected to the Third Congress (March 4, 1793-March 3, 1795)
was not a candidate for renomination
Governor of North Carolina 1799-1802, and from December 1, 1807 until December 12, 1808
died in Moore County, N.C., July 20, 1814
interment in the family cemetery in Moore County, near Carbonton, N.C.
reinterment, 1970, at his last residence in Moore County, now known as ``The House in the Horseshoe,'' a mile and a half from his original grave on Governor's Creek.

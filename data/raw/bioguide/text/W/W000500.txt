WILLIAMS, George Short, a Representative from Delaware
born in Ocean View, Sussex County, Del., October 21, 1877
attended the public schools and Wilmington Conference Academy, Dover, Del.
was graduated from Dickinson College, Carlisle, Pa., in 1900
instructor in Ironwood (Mich.) High School 1902-1904
engaged in the lumber business in Delaware and North Carolina 1905-1923
also interested in banking
mayor of Millsboro, Del., 1921-1927
treasurer of the State of Delaware 1929-1933
president of the State board of education 1927-1934
deputy motor vehicle commissioner 1935-1937
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1940
elected as a Republican to the Seventy-sixth Congress (January 3, 1939-January 3, 1941)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection to the Seventy-seventh Congress
motor vehicle commissioner 1941-1946
administrative assistant to Senator John J. Williams from January 1947 to January 1959
died in Millsboro, Del., November 22, 1961
interment in Union Cemetery, Georgetown, Del.

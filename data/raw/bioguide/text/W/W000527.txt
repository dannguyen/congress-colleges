WILLIAMS, Lewis, (brother of John Williams, of Tennessee, and Robert Williams and cousin of Marmaduke Williams), a Representative from North Carolina
born in Surry County, N.C., February 1, 1782
was graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1808
member of the State house of commons in 1813 and 1814
elected as a Republican to the Fourteenth through the Seventeenth Congresses
reelected to the Eighteenth as a Crawford Republican
reelected to the Nineteenth and Twentieth Congresses as an Adams
reelected to the Twenty-first through the Twenty-fourth Congresses as an Anti-Jacksonian
reelected to the Twenty-fifth through the Twenty-seventh Congresses as a Whig and served from March 4, 1815, until his death
chairman, Committee on Claims (Fifteenth through Twenty-first Congresses), Committee on Territories (Twenty-third Congress)
died in Washington, D.C., February 23, 1842
interment in Panther Creek Cemetery, Surry County, N.C.

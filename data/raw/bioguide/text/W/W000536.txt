WILLIAMS, Sherrod, a Representative from Kentucky
born in Pulaski County, Ky., in 1804
moved with his parents to Wayne County
received a limited education
learned the trade of brickmaker in Monticello when about fifteen years of age
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced
member of the State house of representatives 1829-1834 and in 1846
elected as a Whig to the Twenty-fourth, Twenty-fifth, and Twenty-sixth Congresses (March 4, 1835-March 3, 1841)
chairman, Committee on Invalid Pensions (Twenty-sixth Congress)
was not a candidate for reelection
moved to one of the Southern States, where he died
death date unknown.

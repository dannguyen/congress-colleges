WILLOUGHBY, Westel, Jr., a Representative from New York
born in Goshen, Conn., November 20, 1769
moved to Newport, N.Y.
studied medicine and engaged in practice
appointed judge of the court of common pleas of Herkimer County in 1805 and served until 1821
president of the Herkimer County Medical Society 1806-1816 and 1818-1836
served in the State assembly in 1808 and 1809
president of the College of Physicians and Surgeons for the Western District of New York 1812-1844
member of the medical staff of the militia and served in the War of 1812
successfully contested as a Republican the election of William S. Smith to the Fourteenth Congress and served from December 13, 1815, to March 3, 1817
founded the town of Willoughby, Ohio, and also Willoughby College (now a part of Syracuse University)
died in Newport, Herkimer County, N.Y., October 3, 1844
interment in the First Baptist Church Cemetery.

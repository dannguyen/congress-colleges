WILSON, John Thomas, a Representative from Ohio
born in Bell, Highland County, Ohio, April 16, 1811
received a limited schooling
engaged in mercantile and agricultural pursuits
during the Civil War was appointed first lieutenant of Company E, Seventieth Regiment, Ohio Volunteer Infantry, November 2, 1861, and was discharged as captain November 27, 1862
member of the State senate 1863-1866
elected as a Republican to the Fortieth, Forty-first, and Forty-second Congresses (March 4, 1867-March 3, 1873)
chairman, Committee on Agriculture (Forty-first and Forty-second Congresses)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1872 to the Forty-third Congress
engaged in the handling of loans and mortgages
died in Tranquillity (near what is now known as Seaman), Adams County, Ohio, on October 6, 1891
interment in Tranquillity Cemetery.

WINANS, James January, a Representative from Ohio
born in Maysville, Ky., June 7, 1818
moved with his parents to Greene County, Ohio
attended the common schools and the University of Lexington, Kentucky
studied law
was admitted to the bar in Lexington, Ky., in 1841 and commenced practice in Indiana
moved to Xenia, Greene County, Ohio, in 1843 and continued the practice of law
clerk of the Greene County Courts 1845-1851
served in the State senate in 1857
member of the State house of representatives in 1863
judge of the court of common pleas 1864-1871
elected as a Republican to the Forty-first Congress (March 4, 1869-March 3, 1871)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1870 to the Forty-second Congress
resumed the practice of law
died in Xenia, Ohio, April 28, 1879
interment in Woodlawn Cemetery.

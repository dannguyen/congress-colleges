WINSTEAD, William Arthur, a Representative from Mississippi
born near Philadelphia, Neshoba County, Miss., January 6, 1904
attended the public schools, Clarke Memorial College, Newton, Miss., and the University of Alabama at Tuscaloosa
was graduated from Mississippi Southern College at Hattiesburg in 1931
engaged in agricultural pursuits
superintendent of education of Neshoba County, Miss., 1935-1942
elected as a Democrat to the Seventy-eighth and to the ten succeeding Congresses (January 3, 1943-January 3, 1965)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1964 to the Eighty-ninth Congress
resumed agricultural pursuits
automobile dealer
commissioner, Mississippi department of Public Welfare, 1968-1971
died March 14, 1995 in Philadelphia, Miss.
interment at Cedar Lawn Cemetery, Philadelphia, Miss.

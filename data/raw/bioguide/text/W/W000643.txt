WINTER, Charles Edwin, a Representative from Wyoming
born in Muscatine, Iowa, September 13, 1870
attended the public schools and Iowa Wesleyan University at Mount Pleasant
was graduated from the Nebraska Wesleyan University at Lincoln in 1892
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1895 and commenced practice in Omaha, Nebr.
moved to Encampment, Carbon County, Wyo., in 1902 and to Casper, Natrona County, Wyo., in 1903
delegate to the Republican National Convention in 1908
judge of the sixth judicial district of Wyoming 1913-1919
resigned from the bench and resumed the practice of law at Casper, Wyo.
elected as a Republican to the Sixty-eighth, Sixty-ninth, and Seventieth Congresses (March 4, 1923-March 3, 1929)
was not a candidate for renomination in 1928, but was an unsuccessful candidate for election to the United States Senate
attorney general of Puerto Rico in 1932 and 1933, and served as Acting Governor
resumed the practice of law
died in Casper, Wyo., April 22, 1948
interment in Highland Cemetery.

WOODRUM, Clifton Alexander, a Representative from Virginia
born in Roanoke, Roanoke County, Va., April 27, 1887
attended the public schools of his native city and the University College of Medicine (now combined with the Medical College of Virginia), Richmond, Va.
became a registered pharmacist and engaged in his profession in Roanoke
studied law at Washington and Lee University, Lexington, Va.
was admitted to the bar in 1908 and commenced practice in Roanoke, Va.
Commonwealth attorney of Roanoke 1917-1919
judge of the Hustings Court of Roanoke 1919-1922
elected as a Democrat to the Sixty-eighth and to the eleven succeeding Congresses and served from March 4, 1923, until his resignation on December 31, 1945, to become president of the American Plant Food Council, Inc.
died in Washington, D.C., October 6, 1950
interment in Fairview Cemetery, Roanoke, Va.

WOODS, William, a Representative from New York
born in Washington County, N.Y., in 1790
received a limited schooling
moved to Bath, Steuben County, N.Y., in 1813
studied law
was admitted to the bar and practiced in Bath, N.Y.
member of the State assembly 1823-1825
elected to the Eighteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of William B. Rochester and served from November 3, 1823, to March 3, 1825
resumed the practice of his profession
surrogate of Steuben County 1827-1835
died in Bath, N.Y., August 7, 1837
interment in Grove Cemetery.

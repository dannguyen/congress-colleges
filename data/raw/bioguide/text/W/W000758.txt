WRIGHT, Edwin Ruthvin Vincent, a Representative from New Jersey
born in Hoboken, N.J., January 2, 1812
completed preparatory studies
engaged in newspaper work in 1835
editor of the Jersey Blue in 1836, a newspaper published in Hoboken, N.J.
admitted to the bar in 1839 and commenced practice in Jersey City
lawyer, private practice
subsequently settled in Hudson City and continued the practice of law
member of the New Jersey state council in 1843
district attorney for Hudson County 1851-1855
mayor of Hudson, N.J., in 1855
elected as a Democrat to the Thirty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1865-March 3, 1867)
owing to ill health, was not a candidate for renomination in 1866 to the Fortieth Congress
died in Jersey City, N.J., January 21, 1871
interment in Hoboken Cemetery, New Durham, Hudson County, N.J.

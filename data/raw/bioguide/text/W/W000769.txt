WRIGHT, Samuel Gardiner, a Representative from New Jersey
born in Wrightstown, N.J., November 18, 1781
was mainly self-educated
engaged in mercantile pursuits in Philadelphia, Pa., with a country place near Imlaystown, N.J.
owned several iron furnaces in New Jersey and Delaware
was elected as a Whig to the Twenty-ninth Congress and served from March 4, 1845, until his death near Imlaystown, Monmouth County, N.J., July 30, 1845, before the assembling of Congress
interment in the East Branch Cemetery, near Imlaystown, N.J.

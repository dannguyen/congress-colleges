YAPLE, George Lewis, a Representative from Michigan
born in Leonidas, St. Joseph County, Mich., on February 20, 1851
moved with his parents to Mendon, Mich., in 1857
attended the common schools and Albion (Mich.) College
was graduated from the Northwestern University, Evanston, Ill., in 1871 and completed a postgraduate course in 1874
studied law
was admitted to the bar in 1872, but was engaged in agricultural pursuits until 1877, when he commenced the practice of law at Mendon, Mich.
unsuccessful Greenback candidate for election in 1880 to the Forty-seventh Congress
elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (March 4, 1883-March 3, 1885)
unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1884 to the Forty-ninth Congress
unsuccessful candidate for election as Governor in 1886
delegate to the Democratic National Convention in 1888
resumed the practice of law in Mendon, Mich.
circuit judge of the fifteenth circuit of Michigan 1894-1911
became a member of the Republican Party in 1916
retired from active pursuits and resided in Mendon, Mich., until his death December 16, 1939
interment in Mendon Cemetery.
